Two roads, decided by your hands

Preview: Surat Al-Layl swears by the night and the day, then tells you all your striving splits into just two roads. Here is what decides which one you walk.

Assalamu alaikum,

Surat Al-Layl opens in the dark. It swears by the night as it covers everything over, then by the day as it breaks open, then by the One who made you male and female. Three oaths, and then one short verdict that sorts all of humanity: your efforts are diverse.

Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud lingers on that word. Allah does not use the plain word for 'different.' He uses shatta, which means something that was once one piece and then shattered into many. Mankind began as one. Now our striving runs every which way. But at the very end, the Sheikh says, it all funnels into exactly two roads.

The first traveler gives, and fears Allah, and believes in the best reward, and Allah promises to ease him toward ease. The second hoards, and feels he needs no one, and denies the best, and is eased toward difficulty. And here is the thing the Sheikh keeps returning to: the fork is not how much money you have. It is what the money does to your heart. When Allah describes the worst people, He does not say they have wealth. He says they love it. Money in the pocket is fine. Money in the heart is the danger.

Then the surah lowers the bar so no one is shut out. The verse about purifying yourself drops the small word that would have meant 'so that your giving cleans you,' because, the Sheikh explains, giving does not clean you, Allah does. You give in hope, and He purifies. And the condition is only to give, even half a date.

It ends with Abu Bakr buying Bilal's freedom under the midday boulder, purely for Allah, owing no one, seeking nothing but His Face. And the last word of the surah is a promise sworn to anyone who gives like that: he is going to be satisfied.

May Allah make us people who give for His Face alone.
