Adding up what you have. Measuring it against the next person. Quietly keeping score.

Caption

Surat At-Takathur catches the whole of mankind mid-count, busy adding up a world it will set down.

Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud draws it out: the opening word, alhakum, is from lahw, the distraction that grips the heart, and takathur is the rivalry for more, more wealth, more children, more of everything. But the crime, he insists, is not the having; it is being pulled away. Then a verb worth pausing on: the counting stops 'until you visit the graves,' not buried, but visited, because the grave is a short stay you come out of, not a home. And the surah ends on the question that follows you home, that you will be asked about every blessing, the way the Prophet ﷺ wept over cold water, dates, and meat in the famine of Madinah and called it the blessing you will be asked about.
