Ad-Duha
The surah Allah sent down when the revelation had gone quiet and His Prophet ﷺ was grieving, to tell him he was never once abandoned
cover
For days, no revelation came.

The voice that arrived like sunrise went silent, and the Prophet ﷺ was left grieving in the quiet.
01 / 08
Then the mockery: 'Looks like your Lord has bid you farewell, and is displeased with you.'

Surat Ad-Duha came down to answer exactly that.
02 / 08
Allah does not begin by scolding the mockers.

He swears by the soft morning light, the light that fills the whole sky without worrying who is watching.
03 / 08
He swears by the night too, using the word SAJA: gone completely still, cut off.

The same stillness as the revelation that had paused. The oath names his pain before it heals it.
04 / 08
Then the gentlest words:

'Your Lord has not taken leave of you, nor detested you.'

The word for 'leave,' the Sheikh notes, is the warm goodbye between people who love each other.
05 / 08
And notice His name: Rabbuka. Your Carer.

The One who raised and provided for you from the start. How could HE of all beings abandon you?
06 / 08
Then He hands him his own life as proof:

Weren't you an orphan, and He sheltered you? Searching, and He guided you? In need, and He enriched you?
07 / 08
Silence from Allah is not abandonment by Allah.

What is ahead is better than all that has passed.

Surat Ad-Duha, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
08 / 08
Caption

Surat Ad-Duha is the surah for the silent seasons, when the help seems to have stopped and a voice starts whispering that you have been left behind.

Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud sets the scene: revelation paused, the Prophet ﷺ grieved, and a woman taunted that his Lord had abandoned him. Allah answers not with a rebuke but with an oath on the soothing morning light, and the gentlest of words: your Lord has not bid you farewell (the warm goodbye between people who love each other, the Sheikh notes), nor is He displeased with you. Then He points the Prophet ﷺ back over his own life, orphan to shelter, searching to guidance, need to plenty, and forward to a giving that will not stop until he is pleased.
