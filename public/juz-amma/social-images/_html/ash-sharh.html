Juz Amma · Surah 94
Ash-Sharh
The surah where Allah, fresh off comforting His Messenger ﷺ, keeps counting the gifts, and lands on a promise you can lean your whole weight against
buruja.comcover
Surat Ash-Sharh does not begin a new conversation.

It leans across from the surah before it and keeps Allah comforting His Messenger ﷺ, still counting His gifts to him one by one.
buruja.com01 / 08
The first gift: 'Did We not expand your chest?'

Sharh is what you do when you open something sealed and complicated, so it can finally be understood.
buruja.com02 / 08
Why open the CHEST and not the heart?

The chest is where worry, grief and doubt pile up until the heart is squeezed into a corner and you feel nothing from your worship.

He cleared it out.
buruja.com03 / 08
An opened chest does two things.

It makes you wide enough to absorb every insult and not be moved. (It is what Musa asked for before Pharaoh.)

And it readies you to carry something heavy.
buruja.com04 / 08
Then Allah lifts a burden off him.

Not himl, an ordinary load. WIZR: a weight so heavy his back had begun to crack under it.

And He takes it clean away.
buruja.com05 / 08
He says it twice: with the hardship, ease.

Not AFTER it. WITH it. Laid right alongside it, walking beside it the whole way.
buruja.com06 / 08
The Prophet ﷺ came out LAUGHING at the reason it repeats.

The hardship is one word, said once. The ease is said twice.

So one hardship will never overcome two eases.
buruja.com07 / 08
When you finish, do not collapse. Stand.

The end of the surah is the condition for its beginning. Want the expanded chest? Stand in prayer, turn your longing to Him.

Surat Ash-Sharh, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
