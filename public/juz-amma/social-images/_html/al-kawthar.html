Juz Amma · Surah 108
Al-Kawthar
The shortest surah in the Qur'an, handed to the Prophet ﷺ on the night they called him cut off, a river of abundance against a single insult
buruja.comcover
It is the shortest surah in the Qur'an. Three ayat.

And Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud wants you to feel the weight pressed into that small space.
buruja.com01 / 08
The Prophet ﷺ had just buried a son.

A man in Makkah told his friends: leave him, he is cut off now. No boys, no name. When he dies he is finished.
buruja.com02 / 08
Into that wound, Allah does not send a word about the child.

He sends a flood: 'Indeed, We have granted you al-Kawthar.'

Abundance.
buruja.com03 / 08
Notice the order.

The gift comes FIRST. Before a single command, Allah fills His Prophet's ﷺ hands. Then He says what to do with them.
buruja.com04 / 08
What is al-Kawthar?

A river in Paradise. Whiter than milk, sweeter than honey, banks of gold, cups as many as the stars.

And the word itself just means: abundant good.
buruja.com05 / 08
Then the command, the answer to the gift:

'So pray to your Lord and sacrifice.'

Prayer lowers the whole self. Sacrifice pries loose the wealth the soul loves to keep.
buruja.com06 / 08
And the last word settles the insult.

It is the HATER who is the one cut off.

Allah raised His Prophet's ﷺ name over the whole earth, said in every adhan until the end of time.
buruja.com07 / 08
What you give for Allah never cuts you off.

The world counts what you keep. Allah counts what you surrender to Him.

Surat Al-Kawthar, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
