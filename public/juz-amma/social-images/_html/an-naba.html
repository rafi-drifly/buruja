Juz Amma · Surah 78
An-Naba
The surah that opens on a question the deniers were whispering, and an answer that will not let you sit still
buruja.comcover
Surat An-Naba does not open with a command or a story.

It opens with a rumor: a crowd, leaning in, asking about something the Prophet ﷺ kept warning them of.
buruja.com01 / 08
There are only two reasons anyone asks a question.

To learn something, or to mock someone.

The deniers were using the second one.
buruja.com02 / 08
Notice the grammar.

Allah does not turn to face them. He turns to His Messenger: 'what are THEY asking about?'

The turning away itself carries His anger.
buruja.com03 / 08
He calls it 'the great news.'

Not khabar, the quiet word for news, but NABA: news that is great, that you will feel, and that demands you move.
buruja.com04 / 08
Then He does not argue. He points.

Is this the bed you made, this whole earth? These the pegs, these mountains? You couldn't even choose your own gender.
buruja.com05 / 08
Every night you are cut off from the world and your soul. A small death.

And every morning you rise.

So where did you get the idea that one sleep is the sleep you never wake from?
buruja.com06 / 08
The rain falls on a dead seed, and it is 'brought out' of the dark ground.

The verb is the one used for raising people.

The proof of the next life was lying around you the whole time.
buruja.com07 / 08
News this great is meant to move you.

An alarm is not a fact to nod at. It is a reason to stand.

Surat An-Naba, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
