Juz Amma · Surah 86
At-Tariq
The surah that swears by a star knocking through the dark, and turns it into proof that the One who made you can bring you back
buruja.comcover
Surat At-Tariq opens like a knock at your door in the dead of night.

One sharp sound that pulls your eyes up and will not let them rest.
buruja.com01 / 10
at-Tariq comes from a root meaning to strike, to knock.

It is the one who arrives at night. And a knock at night is loud, because the night is silent.

Something that comes in the dark, and demands your attention.
buruja.com02 / 10
Then Allah asks: do you even know what the knocker is?

And He answers it Himself: the piercing star.
buruja.com03 / 10
The word is THAQIB: blazing bright, and boring a hole.

Picture the night as a dark curtain, and the star's light as a point piercing through it, traveling the long road down to your eye.
buruja.com04 / 10
Why swear by it? Here is the response to the oath:

There is no soul except that a guardian is set over it.
buruja.com05 / 10
Angels who shield you from harm that was never decreed. Scribes who record every deed, every word, even the blink of your eye.

Appointed over you since the womb.
buruja.com06 / 10
The one place you keep your secrets, your own self, keeps none of them.

The recorder stands above it, catching everything.
buruja.com07 / 10
Then Allah says: look at what you were made from.

A gushing drop, from between the backbone and the ribs. A lifeless thing that becomes a living child.

The One who did that can bring you back.
buruja.com08 / 10
On the Day the secrets are put on trial, what comes out is the intention you kept hidden.

And no soul has any power of its own, or any helper.
buruja.com09 / 10
The Qur'an is not entertainment.

Not a thing to enjoy and forget. A decisive word, meant to change you.

They plot. Allah plots. He gives them only a little while.

Surat At-Tariq, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
