Juz Amma · Surah 112
Al-Ikhlas
The surah that answers one question, who is your Lord, and the Prophet ﷺ said its four lines weigh a third of the whole Qur'an
buruja.comcover
Surat Al-Ikhlas is four short lines a child memorizes before he can tie his shoes.

The Prophet ﷺ said it weighs a third of the entire Qur'an.
buruja.com01 / 08
Sheikh Abu Bakr opens with a question that stings:

Do you actually know the One you stand before five times a day?
buruja.com02 / 08
Musa (ﷺ) asked to see Allah on the mountain.

A single sliver of Allah's light crushed that mountain to dust. He woke and said: 'I have repented to You.'

Repented from asking before he had grasped who Allah is.
buruja.com03 / 08
This surah is unlike any other.

No story. No ruling. No scene from the Day of Judgement.

It speaks about nothing but Allah, from its first letter to its last.
buruja.com04 / 08
It came as an answer.

Quraysh asked the Prophet ﷺ to describe his Lord's lineage: is He made of gold? Silver? Metal? Wood?

To ask a Lord's lineage is to ask for His weakness.
buruja.com05 / 08
He is AHAD: One, unique in a way nothing shares.

He is AS-SAMAD: a word found nowhere else in the Qur'an. The One everything turns to in need, and the One who needs no one at all.
buruja.com06 / 08
He neither begets nor is born. No child. No parent. No beginning.

And nothing is His equal. Not slightly. Not greatly. Not at all.
buruja.com07 / 08
Is He really the One in your life?

It is easy to say Allahu ahad. The proof is what you drop and run for when the prayer is called.

Surat Al-Ikhlas, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
