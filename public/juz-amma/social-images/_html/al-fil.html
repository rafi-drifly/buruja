Juz Amma · Surah 105
Al-Fil
The surah where an army big enough to flatten a city is undone by birds, and the proudest plan in Arabia is turned into chewed straw
buruja.comcover
An army marched on Makkah that was bigger than the city itself.

Sixty thousand men, and out front, an elephant tall enough to flatten the Kaaba on its own.
buruja.com01 / 08
Surat Al-Fil tells you how it ended.

It does not describe a battle. There was none.

Allah answered the whole army with birds.
buruja.com02 / 08
Here is the jolt.

Abraha, who led that army, was a Christian. The people he came to destroy were idol-worshippers.

So why did Allah destroy the Christian and save the idolaters?
buruja.com03 / 08
Because Abraha carried the one thing Allah will not tolerate: arrogance.

The Prophet ﷺ said even an atom's weight of it keeps a man from Paradise.

Religion on paper won't save you if pride is underneath it.
buruja.com04 / 08
The answer is almost absurdly small.

Not an earthquake. Not a flood.

Birds. Harmless birds, each carrying three pebbles of baked clay.
buruja.com05 / 08
The surah opens on 'the people of the elephant': war beasts, sixty thousand men.

It closes on 'eaten straw': chewed-up husks a breeze blows away.

That is the distance arrogance falls.
buruja.com06 / 08
When they pointed the great elephant at the Kaaba, it knelt and refused to move.

Point it anywhere else, it ran.

A beast with no intellect obeyed Allah when an army would not.
buruja.com07 / 08
Be at least as obedient as the elephant.

Never lend a hand, a tongue, or a post to tearing down the religion of Allah.

Surat Al-Fil, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
