Juz Amma · Surah 110
An-Nasr
The surah that arrived as a triumph and carried, folded inside the congratulation, the gentlest notice that a life's work was done
buruja.comcover
Surat An-Nasr looks like a banner raised over a victory.

Makkah returned. Whole tribes pouring into Islam.

Almost no one heard the second thing it was saying.

It is the last complete surah of the Qur'an to be revealed.

The strongest opinion: it came down at the farewell Hajj, in the days at Mina.

The Prophet ﷺ lived only about two more months after it.
buruja.com02 / 08
The first word is 'idha' (when), not 'in' (if).

Allah does not say IF the help comes. He says WHEN.

The doubt is cut before the sentence has begun.
buruja.com03 / 08
After the greatest victory of his life, the command is not 'celebrate.'

It is: glorify your Lord, and seek His forgiveness.

That, the Sheikh says, is what gratitude actually looks like.
buruja.com04 / 08
Why would a sinless Prophet ﷺ seek forgiveness?

Because even the obedient seek it, for the worship itself.

No one, not even the angels, worships Allah as He deserves.
buruja.com05 / 08
Umar tested the senior Companions on this surah.

The young Ibn Abbas answered: it is about the death of the Messenger ﷺ.

Umar said, 'I know of no interpretation but yours.'
buruja.com06 / 08
The last word of the last surah is not 'victory.'

It is TAWWAB: the One who keeps turning toward you, again and again, more than you ever turn to Him.

The triumph ends on an open door.
