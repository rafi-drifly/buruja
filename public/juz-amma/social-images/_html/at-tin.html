Juz Amma · Surah 95
At-Tin
The surah that swears by a fruit, an oil, and two holy mountains, and lands on what you were really built to be
buruja.comcover
Surat At-Tin opens in a strange place for a chapter about your own worth.

Not with you. With a plate of food and a map: a fig, an olive, a mountain, a city.
buruja.com01 / 08
Allah swears by the fig and the olive.

Blessed foods, the Sheikh says, and also a nod to holy lands: Jerusalem, and the mountain where Nuh's ark came to rest.
buruja.com02 / 08
Then Mount Sinai.

Where Allah spoke to Musa. Where He said remove your sandals. Where He unveiled Himself, and the mountain could not bear it and shattered to pieces.
buruja.com03 / 08
Then Makkah, the secure city.

Where even a man who met his father's killer would not lift a hand. The place itself disarms the violence in you.
buruja.com04 / 08
And then every oath lands on its point. The point is YOU.

'We have certainly created man in the best of stature.'

Beautiful outside. Balanced within. Built exactly for your purpose.
buruja.com05 / 08
Which is why worship is never beyond you.

You were not asked to do something you were not made for. You were shaped to fit it.
buruja.com06 / 08
But the very next verse is a drop.

'Then We return him to the lowest of the low.'

Top of creation, and free every day to climb or to fall.
buruja.com07 / 08
So after all this, what makes you deny?

The Sheikh's answer stings: most who mock the religion aren't confused by it. They just don't want to admit they're choosing wrong.

Surat At-Tin, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
