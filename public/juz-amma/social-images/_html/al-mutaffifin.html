Juz Amma · Surah 83
Al-Mutaffifin
The surah that begins with woe over a few stolen grams, and ends with two records, one sunk in the lowest pit and one raised to the highest heaven
buruja.comcover
We picture a thief as someone who robs a bank.

Surat Al-Mutaffifin opens its woe on someone much quieter: the one who keeps a kilo just under a kilo.
buruja.com01 / 08
A mutaffif deals in tiny, unnoticed amounts.

Five grams of rice. Two minutes off the clock.

Things too small for anyone to come back and argue over.
buruja.com02 / 08
Watch the attitude.

When he buys, he leans over the scale: 'add more, you under-weighed me.'

When he sells, he wants you to walk away losing, even by 1%.
buruja.com03 / 08
The grammar gives him away.

Allah says they weigh THEM, not weigh FOR them.

The cheat doesn't weigh the goods. He weighs the buyer: your clothes, where you live, and prices you accordingly.
buruja.com04 / 08
Then the question that exposes the root:

'Do they not think they will be resurrected?'

The cheating grows because he's quietly sure no one is keeping score.
buruja.com05 / 08
A heart truly certain it will stand before the Lord of the worlds does not steal five grams.

The small cheat is a denial of the Day, acted out at a scale.
buruja.com06 / 08
The surah ends by opening two records.

The wicked's: cast down into sijjin.

The righteous's: raised to illiyyun, just beneath the Throne.
buruja.com07 / 08
Your record is a certificate Allah shows off to the angels.

Every fast, every night prayer, every footstep to the masjid that fades from the ground but never fades with Him.

Surat Al-Mutaffifin, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
