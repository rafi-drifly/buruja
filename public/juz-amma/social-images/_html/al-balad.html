Juz Amma · Surah 90
Al-Balad
The surah that swears by Makkah, tells you that you were made for struggle, and then points to one steep road worth climbing
buruja.comcover
Surat Al-Balad opens like a courtroom.

One word, raised like a hand to stop you: NO.

Then Allah swears by the most sacred city on earth: Makkah.
buruja.com01 / 09
Allah swears by the city the Prophet ﷺ was standing in...

while the people who shared his streets were already planning to spill his blood there.

They'd break their most sacred law just to be rid of him.
buruja.com02 / 09
Then the hard truth the oath was building to:

'We have certainly created man into hardship.'

Kabad. Toil. Struggle that does not let up.
buruja.com03 / 09
Why? Allah could have left us in Paradise.

Because the tranquil soul promised in the surah before is not handed out free.

The road to that peace runs uphill, for Allah's sake.
buruja.com04 / 09
He gave you two eyes. A tongue. And two lips.

The lips, the Sheikh says, so you could CLOSE them, and govern the tongue behind them.

When something slips out, the gift isn't to blame. You are.
buruja.com05 / 09
Then He showed you two roads. The high one and the low one.

The proud man took the easy road down.

He did not storm the steep pass: al-aqaba.
buruja.com06 / 09
And what is the steep pass?

Freeing a trapped soul. Feeding on a day of hunger. Reaching for an orphan, for a needy person sunk in the dust.

The climb up is built of mercy spent on the people the proud walk past.
buruja.com07 / 09
Two hands at the end.

The companions of the right, walking to the Garden.

The companions of the left, over whom is a fire closed in: the lid pressed shut, with no door.
buruja.com08 / 09
You were made for the climb, not the couch.

The only question is which struggle you choose.

Surat Al-Balad, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
