Juz Amma · Surah 88
Al-Ghashiyah
The surah that names the Day after the way it will cover you, and then asks you to look at a camel
buruja.comcover
Surat Al-Ghashiyah does not open with a command or a story.

It opens like a hand on your shoulder, waking you: 'Has the news reached you?'
buruja.com01 / 08
The surah before it ended with Allah speaking to the deniers directly.

This one turns away from them and speaks to the Prophet ﷺ about them instead.

The turning away is the warning.
buruja.com02 / 08
The Day is named Al-Ghashiyah: the thing that covers everything.

It covers the earth. And it covers every plan and worry you are drowning in right now. In one instant, all of it goes under.
buruja.com03 / 08
Two faces, that Day.

One working and exhausted, worn out running for an escape that isn't there, its terror worn on the outside.

The other soft with delight, pleased with everything it strove for.
buruja.com04 / 08
Then Allah does not argue. He points.

'Do they not look at the camel, how it was created?'

The very animal Quraysh had mocked Him with.
buruja.com05 / 08
The strongest of tamed animals. It could crush a man.

Yet a child can lead a whole train of them, and when the master says kneel, even on a hot rock, it kneels.

Be like that before your Lord.
buruja.com06 / 08
The earth was 'spread out' flat under your feet.

The same word means a roof.

Because one day this earth becomes the roof over your grave.
buruja.com07 / 08
Just remind. You are not a controller over them.

Put the reminder before people, and leave the rest to Allah.

Surat Al-Ghashiyah, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
