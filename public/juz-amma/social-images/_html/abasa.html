Juz Amma · Surah 80
Abasa
The surah where Allah corrects His own beloved Messenger over a single frown, and teaches you who is really worth your time
buruja.comcover
Surat Abasa does not open by putting the deniers in their place.

It opens by gently correcting the best of creation ﷺ over a single frown.
buruja.com01 / 08
The Prophet ﷺ was finally getting through to the leaders of Makkah.

A blind companion came rushing in to learn, calling out, not seeing he was busy.

A small frown crossed the Prophet's face.
buruja.com02 / 08
It was not a sin. The Prophet ﷺ is protected from sin.

The blind man could not even see the frown.

Standards just rise with rank. And this is the most beloved of creation.
buruja.com03 / 08
Even the correction is merciful.

Allah speaks of him in the third person: 'He frowned, He turned away.'

In Arabic, that is how you go gentle on someone before you face them.
buruja.com04 / 08
There were two men.

The one who felt he needed nothing (Quraysh). And the one who came running in fear of Allah (the blind man).

Allah says: stop chasing the proud one. Turn to the one who came running.
buruja.com05 / 08
Then He deflates the arrogance.

You began as a single weak drop you would rush to wash off your clothes. Your whole life was measured while you were nothing.

You control neither your birth nor your death.
buruja.com06 / 08
Then: look at your food.

Rain poured in torrents. An earth cracked open by a fragile plant. Eight foods from one soil and one water.

Gratitude alone should bring you back to Him.
buruja.com07 / 08
Give your time to the one reaching for the truth.

Not the one you are trying to impress.

Surat Abasa, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
