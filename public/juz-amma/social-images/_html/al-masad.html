Juz Amma · Surah 111
Al-Masad
The surah where Allah Himself answers an uncle who raised his hands against His Messenger, and names him, and his wife, into the Fire
buruja.comcover
Surat Al-Masad is the only surah where Allah names a living man, condemns him, and answers him in person.

No story from a distance. A direct reply from above the heavens.
buruja.com01 / 09
After three years of secret calling, the Prophet ﷺ climbed Safa at dawn and gathered all of Makkah.

'Say there is no god but Allah, and you will succeed.'

The crowd stood silent. Except one man.
buruja.com02 / 09
His uncle, Abu Lahab, stood up, dusted his hands at him as if shaking off filth, and said:

'May you perish. Is this what you gathered us for?'

The first man to insult the Messenger ﷺ in public.
buruja.com03 / 09
The Prophet ﷺ said nothing back.

No curse. No insult. No retaliation.

Because the One who defends the believers had already taken it up Himself.
buruja.com04 / 09
Notice the detail.

The three surahs after this all begin with 'Say.'

This one does not. Allah did not tell the Prophet ﷺ to say it. He wanted to be the only one to answer.
buruja.com05 / 09
'May the hands of Abu Lahab perish.'

The very hands he had just dusted in contempt.

And every scheme those hands ever made against Islam collapsed.
buruja.com06 / 09
His wealth did not avail him.

When money can no longer help you, the Sheikh says, that IS the punishment. It exists to ransom and to feed. His could do neither.
buruja.com07 / 09
His name was the verdict.

Abu Lahab, 'father of flame,' the vanity he was so proud of, became the Fire of flame he is given.
buruja.com08 / 09
Which hands are you raising, and toward what?

Abu Lahab had beauty, wealth, and the noblest family on earth. It saved him from nothing.

Surat Al-Masad, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
