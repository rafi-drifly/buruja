Juz Amma · Surah 103
Al-Asr
The three short ayat that, ash-Shafi'i said, would have been enough on their own to guide mankind
buruja.comcover
Surat Al-Asr is three ayat long.

Imam ash-Shafi'i said that if people pondered only this one surah, it would have been enough to guide them.
buruja.com01 / 10
Allah opens with an oath.

He swears by al-asr: time.

When Allah swears by a thing, He is telling you to look hard at what you walk past every day.
buruja.com02 / 10
Then, in the next breath, the verdict:

Mankind is in loss.

Not the wicked. Not some. AL-INSAN. Every last one.
buruja.com03 / 10
The word is khusr, the merchant's word.

Time is the capital you were handed at birth, and it is being spent whether you trade with it or not.
buruja.com04 / 10
The salaf guarded their time more fiercely than their gold.

One rehung his door so it opened the other way, just to save a few steps.

Lost money returns. A lost hour does not.
buruja.com05 / 10
The Prophet ﷺ named the trap:

Two blessings cheat most people, health and free time.

You notice their worth once they are already gone.
buruja.com06 / 10
Then Allah leaves one door open. The exception is FOUR things:

Believed. Did righteous deeds. Advised one another to truth. Advised one another to patience.
buruja.com07 / 10
The word for advising is tawasaw.

It is the word for a dying person's last counsel.

So advice between believers should be sincere, gentle, urgent, and true. When it is rejected, the fault is often the harshness of the one who gave it.
buruja.com08 / 10
Patience comes last for a reason.

Faith, deeds, and calling to truth all collapse without it.

The surah opens on time and closes on patience: your whole life is meant to be filled with sabr, or it drains into loss.
buruja.com09 / 10
You do not make it out of the loss alone.

The first two save you. The last two are how a community pulls each other out.

Surat Al-Asr, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
