At-Takathur
The surah that catches you mid-count, busy piling up a world you will leave, and asks what all that counting was really for
buruja.comcover
Surat At-Takathur opens by catching you mid-count.

Adding up what you have. Measuring it against the next person. Quietly keeping score.
buruja.com01 / 08
Two tribes boasted over wealth, men, and horses, and could not stop.

Until the living ran out, and they walked into the graveyard to count their dead.
buruja.com02 / 08
The crime is not the wealth.

The crime is the wealth that distracts.

Dawud and Sulayman held kingdoms and were never once pulled off their prayer.
buruja.com03 / 08
'This rivalry distracted you.'

From what? The verse never says.

The silence is on purpose. Sit with it, and fill in your own name.
buruja.com04 / 08
The counting stops, it says, 'until you visit the graves.'

Not buried. VISITED.

A visit is short. You do not move in. You are coming back out.
buruja.com05 / 08
So the cure for the chasing is to visit the grave now, while you still can.

Go alone. Feel what waits. The world drains out of the heart.
buruja.com06 / 08
The surah ends on a question that follows you home:

you will be asked, that Day, about every blessing you enjoyed.
