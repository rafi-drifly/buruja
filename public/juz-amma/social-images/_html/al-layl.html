Juz Amma · Surah 92
Al-Layl
The surah that swears by the night and the day, then tells you that everything you are running toward splits into just two roads
buruja.comcover
Surat Al-Layl opens in the dark.

It swears by the night as it covers everything, then by the day as it breaks open, then by the One who made you male and female.
buruja.com01 / 08
Then one short verdict sorts all of humanity:

your efforts are 'diverse.'

Not the plain word for different. SHATTA: something that was once one piece, now shattered into many.
buruja.com02 / 08
And all that scatter, the Sheikh says, funnels at the end into just two roads.

The one who gave and feared Allah.

The one who hoarded and felt he needed no one.
buruja.com03 / 08
Notice what decides the fork.

Not how much money you have.

When Allah describes the worst people, He doesn't say they HAVE wealth. He says they LOVE it. And love lives in the heart.
buruja.com04 / 08
Money in the pocket is fine.

Money in the heart is the danger.
buruja.com05 / 08
The verse says the giver gives 'to purify himself' but drops the word that would mean 'so his giving cleans him.'

Because giving doesn't clean you.

Allah does. You give in hope; He purifies.
buruja.com06 / 08
And the bar is only to GIVE.

Not to give a lot. Even half a date.

So even the poorest can reach the rank of the most pious.
buruja.com07 / 08
It ends with Abu Bakr freeing Bilal from under the midday boulder, owing no one, seeking nothing but Allah's Face.

And the last word of the surah is sworn to anyone who gives like that:

he is going to be satisfied.

Surat Al-Layl, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
