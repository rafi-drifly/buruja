Juz Amma · Surah 89
Al-Fajr
The surah that swears by the first light, walks you past the ruins of the mightiest men who ever lived, and ends with a soul called home
buruja.comcover
Surat Al-Fajr does not open with an argument.

It opens with a hand raised to swear: by the dawn, by ten nights, by the even and the odd, by the night as it slips away.
buruja.com01 / 09
Every oath points at one thing.

The dawn breaks only after the night runs out. That is the resurrection: the world is the long night, the Day is the light that breaks at its end.
buruja.com02 / 09
Even your week is an argument.

Every day is paired with a night, 'even.'

There is one Day coming with no night paired to it. The 'odd' one. Standing alone.
buruja.com03 / 09
Then the surah walks you through ruins.

Aad raised pillars. Thamud carved homes into solid mountains by hand. Pharaoh hauled massive stone across the land for the pyramids.
buruja.com04 / 09
Notice the question.

Allah does not ask 'WHAT happened to them.' He asks 'HOW your Lord dealt with them.'

Because the real shock was: with all that power, how were they brought down?
buruja.com05 / 09
A wind for Aad. A flood for Pharaoh.

The mightiest builders who ever lived are now a ruin the caravans drive past.

So what is a people of mud houses feeling so secure about?
buruja.com06 / 09
When Allah gives him wealth: 'My Lord has honored me.'

When Allah withholds it: 'My Lord has humiliated me.'

Both wrong. The gift and the lack are the test, not the score.
buruja.com07 / 09
Hardship announces itself as a test.

Ease slips in disguised as a reward.

That is the harder trial: the one that never feels like one.
buruja.com08 / 09
Aim to be the soul at peace.

The surah ends with the gentlest words in it: 'O reassured soul, return to your Lord, well-pleased and pleasing.'

Surat Al-Fajr, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
