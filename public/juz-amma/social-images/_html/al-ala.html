Juz Amma · Surah 87
Al-A'la
The surah the Prophet ﷺ loved, that opens by lifting the name of your Lord above every defect and closes by asking which life you actually prefer
buruja.comcover
Surat Al-A'la was one of the surahs the Prophet ﷺ loved most.

He recited it where the biggest crowds gathered: Jumu'ah, the two Eids, the night prayer.

Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud explains why: it is a whole religion folded small.
buruja.com01 / 08
It opens with a command, not an argument: 'Exalt the name of your Lord, the Most High.'

To make tasbih is to clear away from Allah every defect.

The root is a picture of something that floats and refuses to sink.
buruja.com02 / 08
You act it out every single day.

In sujud your forehead is in the dust while your tongue says He is the Most High.

You get as low as a body can go, and from there you announce that He is highest.
buruja.com03 / 08
Instead of debating the Day, He points at the world.

He balanced the fluid in your two ears so you don't topple over.

He planned the universe 50,000 years before making it, so you'd learn not to rush.
buruja.com04 / 08
He brings the green pasture up out of the dead earth.

The verb for 'brought out' is the one He uses for raising people.

The proof of your own return was growing in the ground the whole time.
buruja.com05 / 08
The command to the Prophet ﷺ is just one word: remind.

No object. Not 'remind so-and-so.'

Because judging who deserves the message was never your job. He reminded even Abu Lahab.
buruja.com06 / 08
Then He stops saying 'him' and turns to face YOU:

'But you prefer the worldly life, while the Hereafter is better and lasts.'

The world wins because it's close, in your hand. But 'close' is what the word dunya means: close, and low.
buruja.com07 / 08
You are choosing a life, not just sins.

He asks you to prefer what lasts on nothing but His word. That preference is the whole test.

Surat Al-A'la, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
