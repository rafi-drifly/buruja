Juz Amma · Surah 107
Al-Ma'un
The surah that measures your faith not by what you say, but by how you treat the orphan, the poor, and the small thing your neighbor asks to borrow
buruja.comcover
Surat Al-Ma'un does not begin with your prayer.

It begins with an orphan at the door, and a poor man you could feed and would not.
buruja.com01 / 08
It opens on a strange figure: 'the one who denies the Recompense.'

Not a disbeliever who never accepted. A man who KNOWS it's true and calls it a lie anyway.
buruja.com02 / 08
Sheikh Abu Bakr asks the question that unlocks the surah:

What really holds a person back from doing wrong?

The sense that he'll answer for it later.
buruja.com03 / 08
A camera in the building, so you behave.

3am, empty road, and still you stop at the red light.

Take that away, and cruelty becomes comfortable.
buruja.com04 / 08
So this man shoves the orphan away.

And won't even URGE that the poor be fed. Forget feeding them himself, he won't encourage anyone else to.
buruja.com05 / 08
Notice: Allah names the poor man in the singular.

You're not asked to feed the world.

Only to be moved for one.
buruja.com06 / 08
Three ayat, three broken pieces:

A sin of the heart (denying the Deen). A sin of the limbs (shoving the orphan). A sin of the tongue (not urging the poor be fed).

That's exactly how faith is defined.
buruja.com07 / 08
Your faith is measured at your neighbor's door.

The cure for a hard heart, from the Prophet ﷺ: wipe the orphan's head, and feed the poor.

Surat Al-Ma'un, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
