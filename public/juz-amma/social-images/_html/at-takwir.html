Juz Amma · Surah 81
At-Takwir
The surah that switches off the sun, drops the stars, and undoes the world, then turns and asks you what could possibly be worth chasing instead of the truth in your hands
buruja.comcover
Surat At-Takwir does not ease you in.

The sun is folded up. The stars drop out of the sky. The mountains drift loose. The seas turn to fire.
buruja.com01 / 08
The Prophet ﷺ said: whoever wants to see the Day of Judgement with his own eyes, let him read this surah.

Every word in it is built to be seen.
buruja.com02 / 08
The first verb is kuwwirat: folded up.

It is the word for winding a turban round the head until it disappears, bit by bit.

That is what happens to the sun's light.
buruja.com03 / 08
The most precious thing the Arab owned, a pregnant she-camel worth a fortune, is simply left behind.

Like a man fleeing a fire. Nobody counts his savings while the sky is folding.
buruja.com04 / 08
The pagans read the stars for secret news.

So Allah swears by those same stars, the ones that hide and run and vanish, to tell them the opposite.
buruja.com05 / 08
Every shooting star you see is a small rehearsal of the Day the lights go out.

The sign they misread was arguing for the very Day they denied.
buruja.com06 / 08
Then He defends the revelation they laughed at: it is the word brought by a noble messenger.

The angel Jibril: noble, powerful, secure, obeyed, and above all, trustworthy.
buruja.com07 / 08
Then one quiet question, after the sun is switched off and the stars have dropped:

'So where are you going?'

Surat At-Takwir, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
