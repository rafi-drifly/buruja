Quraysh
The only surah named after a tribe, naming the four favors that should have bought their worship, and asking for it back
buruja.comcover
Surat Quraysh is the only surah in the Qur'an named after a tribe.

It reads like a thank-you note Allah writes on a people's behalf, because they never sent it.
buruja.com01 / 08
He lists what He did for them.

He united them, after they were so divided they'd sooner watch each other starve than share a loaf.
buruja.com02 / 08
He opened their trade: the winter caravan to Yemen, the summer one to Sham.

They traveled with their camels loaded and full, and feared no one.
buruja.com03 / 08
Why no fear?

The nations had learned not to touch the people Allah defended against the elephant.

'These are the people of Allah. Stay away.'
buruja.com04 / 08
Then He writes the one line they owed Him back:

'Let them worship the Lord of this House.'
buruja.com05 / 08
Notice the word.

Not 'let them THANK Me.' 'Let them WORSHIP Me.'

The only real way to thank Allah is to worship Him.
buruja.com06 / 08
The Prophet ﷺ stood in prayer until his feet swelled and cracked.

Asked why, when he was already forgiven, he said:

'Shall I not be a grateful slave?'
buruja.com07 / 08
When food and safety are handled in your life, ask why.

The answer is so you'd have time to worship.

Surat Quraysh, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
