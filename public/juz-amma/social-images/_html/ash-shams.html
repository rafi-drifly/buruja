Juz Amma · Surah 91
Ash-Shams
The surah that swears by the sun seven times over to teach you the one thing you can ruin or rescue: your own soul
buruja.comcover
Surat Ash-Shams does not open with a command or a story.

It opens with Allah swearing, seven times over, by things you see every day and never stop to weigh: the sun, its glow, the moon, the day, the night, the sky, the earth.
buruja.com01 / 08
Here is what is easy to miss.

Every one of those oaths is really about the SUN.

The moon is sworn by only as the thing that follows it. The day only as the thing that reveals it. The night only as the thing that covers it.
buruja.com02 / 08
And an oath is a place to STOP.

When the Maker swears by something, He is pointing at it: look closely, this is great.

The verses are short so you will slow down.
buruja.com03 / 08
Then, after circling the whole cosmos, He turns to the one thing you carry in your chest:

'By the soul and He who proportioned it.'

He even drops the word 'the' to magnify it.
buruja.com04 / 08
You are two things, the Sheikh says: a body and a soul.

The body is fed from the earth. The soul starves for revelation from above.

And they pull in opposite directions, like the sun and the moon.
buruja.com05 / 08
He inspired the soul to know its own wickedness and its own taqwa.

So the guilt that fires when you sin is a GIFT.

It is proof, written inside you, that a Day of reckoning is coming.
buruja.com06 / 08
Then the whole surah turns on two verbs.

He has won who PURIFIES the soul.

He has lost who BURIES it.
buruja.com07 / 08
Thamud saw a camel walk out of solid rock, and still took the knife to it.

So their Lord pounded them level with the ground, for their sin.

Surat Ash-Shams, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
