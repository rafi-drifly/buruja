Juz Amma · Surah 82
Al-Infitar
The surah that splits the sky open above your head, then turns and asks you, to your face, what could possibly have fooled you about your generous Lord
buruja.comcover
Surat Al-Infitar does not ease you in.

The surah before asked where you think you're going. This one answers: toward a Day when the sky above your head tears open.
buruja.com01 / 08
Look at the sky tonight.

That flawless blue dome, no crack, no seam. Look again, a third time, a fourth. You'll only ever find it whole.

Al-Infitar: the day it splits straight through.
buruja.com02 / 08
This is not the surah before, repeated.

That one was darkness, the lights going out. This one is motion: stars scattered, seas burst from their beds, graves overturned.

The next stage of the same collapse.
buruja.com03 / 08
Then, with the whole universe in pieces, Allah turns to face you.

Not a crowd. One human being, alone:

'O man, what deceived you about your Lord, the Generous?'
buruja.com04 / 08
Feel the wording.

Not 'what fooled you about Allah.' About your LORD, so you remember you're the servant.

Then 'the Generous,' the way a kind teacher's question cuts deeper than a harsh one's.
buruja.com05 / 08
He created you. Proportioned you. Balanced you, body and soul.

Put you together in a form you never chose.

A Lord this generous, and still something fooled you?
buruja.com06 / 08
Two angels sit at your shoulders.

Noble and recording. They never doze, never tamper, never stop writing until the Day.

They record even what you do without meaning to.
buruja.com07 / 08
On that Day no one can help you. So move today.

Not even the Prophet ﷺ for his own daughter, until Allah grants him leave.

The command that Day is Allah's. The decision today is still yours.

Surat Al-Infitar, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
