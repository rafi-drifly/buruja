Juz Amma · Surah 79
An-Nazi'at
The surah that opens on angels ripping souls loose, and closes on a Day so near it shrinks your whole life to an afternoon
buruja.comcover
Surat An-Nazi'at does not ease you in.

It opens mid-motion: oaths by creatures caught ripping something out by force.

The whole surah is about things wrenched loose.
buruja.com01 / 09
The opening word, nazi'at, is the verb for a hand pulled out so hard everyone stares.

On the majority reading: angels, tearing the denier's soul out because he does not want to go.
buruja.com02 / 09
Beside them, the ones who slip the believer's soul free.

Smoothly. Painlessly. Like a shoelace that comes undone with one pull.

Same moment, two completely different exits.
buruja.com03 / 09
Hearts that Day will pound.

Allah uses a noun, not a verb, so it is a state that holds.

The fear does not spike and settle. It keeps pounding, and grows.
buruja.com04 / 09
The deniers' favorite sneer: will we really be returned, after we are decayed bones?

Allah's answer is almost dismissive.

It is but one shout. And suddenly they are standing on the open plain.
buruja.com05 / 09
Pharaoh, the worst tyrant alive, was met with the softest door:

'Would you purify yourself?'

He refused, crowned himself a god, and was pulled out of his palace into the sea.
buruja.com06 / 09
Then Allah stops arguing and points.

Is this the sky you built? Raised with no pillars. Its night darkened, its morning drawn out.

The seed comes out of the dark earth using the same word He uses for raising you.
buruja.com07 / 09
Everything in your hand is a tool, not a destination.

Your car. Your phone. Your wealth.

Mata' means a thing used, not a thing savoured. The savouring is kept for Paradise.
buruja.com08 / 09
The root problem is preference, not just denial.

The one bound for the Fire preferred this world to the next.

On the Day they see the Hour, their whole life will feel no longer than an afternoon.

Surat An-Nazi'at, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
