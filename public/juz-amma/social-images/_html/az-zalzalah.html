Juz Amma · Surah 99
Az-Zalzalah
The surah where the ground you trusted turns witness, and an atom's weight of anything is finally seen
buruja.comcover
You trust the ground. You build on it, you bury your dead in it, you walk it like it will hold forever.

Surat Az-Zalzalah opens on the one moment it stops holding.
buruja.com01 / 08
Which earthquake is this?

Not one of our world. Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud places it at the resurrection itself: the quake that heaves the dead up out of their graves after the Trumpet.
buruja.com02 / 08
Notice the first word.

Allah does not say IF the earth is shaken. He says WHEN.

The doubt the deniers clung to is gone in a single syllable.
buruja.com03 / 08
Every earthquake you've seen BURIES. The tower falls, the visible becomes hidden.

This one runs the opposite way. It disgorges. The earth, sick of what's inside it, vomits everything out.
buruja.com04 / 08
Out pours the buried gold men spent their lives chasing.

The raised soul stands stunned: THIS is what I ran after my whole life? And now it spills out for free.
buruja.com05 / 08
Then the heart of the surah:

The ground will SPEAK. It reports every deed done on its surface, for you or against you. Your own skin and hands testify alongside it.
buruja.com06 / 08
How can the earth speak?

The next ayah answers at once: because your Lord commanded it to, the way the heavens and earth once told Him they came willingly.
buruja.com07 / 08
The ground is keeping a record.

So scatter your good across every patch of earth you can. Pray where no one has prayed. Each spot will speak up for you.

Surat Az-Zalzalah, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
