Juz Amma · Surah 113
Al-Falaq
The first of the two surahs of refuge, where you cling to the Lord who splits the dawn and ask Him to tear through every darkness in your life
buruja.comcover
Surat Al-Falaq is one of the first surahs you memorized, and one of the last in the Qur'an.

Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud says that is no accident.
buruja.com01 / 08
It sits right after Al-Ikhlas, the surah of pure belief.

Your belief is the most precious thing you own.

Which makes it the thing most worth attacking. Al-Falaq is the wall around it.
buruja.com02 / 08
Notice how it opens. Not on darkness, but on LIGHT.

'I seek refuge in the Lord of the daybreak.'

Falaq is the dawn that splits the dark sky.
buruja.com03 / 08
And falaq is everything that splits open.

The seed cracking for the plant. The womb opening for the child. The cloud breaking for the rain.

He is the Lord of all that bursts forth.
buruja.com04 / 08
Look at the word for refuge: a'udh.

It is not 'I ask and wait.' It means you give yourself up and step INSIDE Allah's protection.

And the verb is present-tense. You do it again, and again.
buruja.com05 / 08
Then the surah narrows, from the widest evil to the narrowest:

Everything He created. The dark when it settles. The blowers in the knots. Down to one envier when he envies.
buruja.com06 / 08
'When he envies.'

Envy kept inside the heart harms no one.

It is only when it comes out, into backbiting, into harming someone's livelihood, that it becomes the evil you seek refuge from.
buruja.com07 / 08
Once you hand every evil to the Lord of the daybreak, you can stop carrying it.

Seek His protection, and move on toward Him.

Surat Al-Falaq, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
