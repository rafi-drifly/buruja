Juz Amma · Surah 100
Al-Adiyat
The surah that paints a war horse so loyal it will die for you, then asks why you will not even live for your Lord
buruja.comcover
Surat Al-Adiyat does not open with a command or a warning.

It opens with a horse.

And by the time you understand why, it is already too late.
buruja.com01 / 08
Allah swears by the war horses charging the enemy.

The desert Arab loved nothing the way he loved his horse. So the surah opens on the one thing that pulls him to the edge of his seat.
buruja.com02 / 08
Five ayat of pure cinema:

The mares breathing hard at full gallop. Sparks flying off the rock in the dark. A raid in broad daylight. A wall of dust. A charge into the very center of the enemy.

Then the screen goes black.
buruja.com03 / 08
And the real subject walks in:

'Indeed mankind, to his Lord, is ungrateful.'

It was never about the horse.
buruja.com04 / 08
The horse charges into spears and dies for the one who fed it.

And you, fed and made and given everything by your Lord, will not even live for Him?

The animal's loyalty is the standard.
buruja.com05 / 08
He names the disease: KANUD. Extreme ingratitude.

The heart that counts only what is missing. Upset at the one drink not in the fridge, blind to the ten that are there.
buruja.com06 / 08
And its root?

A love of wealth tied too tight. Money that slipped out of the pocket and into the heart.

The Sheikh: money in the pocket is fine. Money in the heart evicts the love of Allah.
buruja.com07 / 08
Clean the inside, not just the outside.

What is in the breasts will be peeled out and laid bare. Your clothes are for people to see. Your heart is for Allah.

Surat Al-Adiyat, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
