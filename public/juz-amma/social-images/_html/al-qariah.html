Juz Amma · Surah 101
Al-Qari'ah
The surah that names the Day after a sound, a single word that strikes you, then refuses to tell you what it is until you have read to the end
buruja.comcover
Surat Al-Qari'ah does not open with a sentence.

It opens with one word, dropped on you like a sound in the dark, and then it leaves you standing there with it.
buruja.com01 / 08
Al-Qari'ah comes from qar': two things colliding so hard they throw off a blasting sound that shakes you from the inside.

The Arabs used it for a fist hammering on a door in the dead of night.
buruja.com02 / 08
Notice the grammar.

The word stands alone with nothing said about it. So the question rises in you by itself: what is it?

The next ayah is that exact question, handed back to you.
buruja.com03 / 08
'And what can make you know what it is?'

You never reach the answer on your own. The only way to know this Day is for the One who built it to tell you.
buruja.com04 / 08
It is the Day people scatter like moths.

No direction, crashing about, nowhere to run. Alone, even in the largest gathering in all of history.
buruja.com05 / 08
The mountains, the strongest thing on the earth, are beaten loose like colored wool and blown away.

If the mountains cannot stand it, what about your heart?
buruja.com06 / 08
Then one still moment after all the ruin: your deeds on a scale.

On the Day the mountains lose their weight, your deeds gain theirs. But only the ones done sincerely for Allah.
buruja.com07 / 08
Heavy scales: a pleasant life. Light scales: a Fire, intensely hot.

The whole universe is torn up just to bring those scales out.

Surat Al-Qari'ah, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.
