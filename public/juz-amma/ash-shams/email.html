Seven oaths, and the one thing they were all for

Preview: Ash-Shams swears by the sun seven times over. Here is the one thing Allah was building toward.

Assalamu alaikum,

Surat Ash-Shams opens with Allah swearing, over and over, by things you see every day and never stop to weigh: the sun, its glow, the moon, the day, the night, the sky, the earth. Seven oaths.

Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud points out something easy to miss. Every one of those oaths is really about the sun. The moon is sworn by only as the thing that follows it; the day only as the thing that reveals it; the night only as the thing that covers it. Look at the end of each verse and the pronoun keeps pointing back: the sun, the sun, the sun.

And an oath, the Sheikh keeps saying, is a place to stop. When the Maker swears by something, He is pointing at it: look closely, this is great. The verses are short so you will slow down.

Then, after circling the whole cosmos, He turns the camera onto the one thing you carry in your own chest. He swears 'by the soul and He who proportioned it,' and He drops the word 'the' to magnify it: pay attention, you have no idea how great a matter this is. He balanced it, He says, and inspired it to know its own wickedness and its own taqwa. That alarm that goes off when you sin, the Sheikh calls a gift: it is proof, written inside you, that a Day is coming when you will answer.

And then the whole surah turns on two verbs. He has won who purifies the soul. He has lost who buries it. Rather than describe the loser in the abstract, Allah walks you into history and shows you Thamud, a people who saw a camel walk out of solid rock and still took the knife to it, and were pounded level with the ground for their sin.

May Allah make us of those who purify the soul, not of those who bury it.
