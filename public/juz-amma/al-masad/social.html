Caption

Surat Al-Masad is the one surah where Allah names a living man, condemns him, and answers him in person.

Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud sets the scene: the Prophet ﷺ climbs Safa at dawn, gathers Makkah, and calls them to one word. His uncle Abu Lahab stands up, dusts his hands at him in contempt, and curses him. The Prophet ﷺ says nothing back, and the reply comes down from above the heavens in Allah's own words, may those very hands perish. Then the surah strips Abu Lahab of everything he was proud of: his wealth that could not help him, his name 'father of flame' turned into his Fire, and his wife the carrier of firewood, a warning, the Sheikh says, against kindling enmity and carrying tales in our own day.

He had beauty, wealth, and the noblest family on earth, and it saved him from nothing. Which hands are you raising? 🤍
