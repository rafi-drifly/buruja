The hands he dusted at the Prophet ﷺ

Preview: An uncle stood up in front of all Makkah and cursed his own nephew. Here is how Allah answered him, by name.

Assalamu alaikum,

Surat Al-Masad is the one surah where Allah names a living man, condemns him, and answers him directly.

Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud sets the scene. After three years of secret calling, the Prophet ﷺ climbed the hill of Safa at dawn and gathered all of Makkah. Say there is no god but Allah, he told them, and you will succeed. The crowd stood silent, stunned. Except his uncle, Abu Lahab, who stood up, dusted his hands at him as if shaking off something filthy, and said, may you perish, is this what you gathered us for?

The Prophet ﷺ said nothing back. He did not curse, did not insult, did not retaliate. And here is the detail the Sheikh will not let you miss: the answer came down from above the seven heavens, in Allah's own words. The three surahs after this one all begin with 'say.' This one does not. Allah did not tell the Prophet ﷺ to say it, because He wanted to be the only one to answer.

May the hands of Abu Lahab perish, and ruined is he. The very hands he had just dusted in contempt.

And then the surah strips him of everything he was proud of. His wealth: it did not avail him, the Sheikh says, because when money can no longer help you, that is itself the punishment. His name: Abu Lahab, father of flame, the vanity he loved, turned into the fire he is given. Even his wife, a noblewoman of Quraysh, is named the carrier of firewood, for kindling enmity and carrying tales, and the Sheikh warns us plainly not to become carriers of firewood ourselves, through the gossip and rumor that light fires between people.

The lesson the Sheikh leaves you with is quieter than the curse. Abu Lahab had beauty, wealth, and the noblest family on earth, with the Messenger of Allah ﷺ for a nephew, and it saved him from nothing, because he raised his hands against the truth. The surah asks which hands you are raising, and toward what.

May Allah keep our hands from ever being raised against His truth, and make our wealth and our children a mercy that follows us, not a ruin.
