They called him cut off

Preview: A man in Makkah said the Prophet ﷺ was finished. Allah answered with the abundance of al-Kawthar.

Assalamu alaikum,

It is the shortest surah in the Qur'an, only three ayat, and Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud wants you to feel what is packed into that small space.

The Prophet ﷺ had just buried a son. A man in Makkah turned to his friends and said, in effect: leave him, he is cut off now, no boys, no name to carry him, when he dies he is finished.

And into that wound Allah does not send a word about the child. He sends a flood: 'Indeed, We have granted you al-Kawthar.' Abundance. Notice the order, the Sheikh says. The gift comes first, before a single command. Allah fills His Prophet's ﷺ hands, and only then says what to do with them.

What is al-Kawthar? The Prophet ﷺ told us himself: a river in Paradise, whiter than milk and sweeter than honey, its banks of gold, its cups as many as the stars in the sky, the pool his Ummah will drink from on the Day of Judgement. But the word means abundant good, and the river, Ibn Abbas said, is only one piece of it. Everything Allah poured on this man is in that one word.

Then the command, and it is the answer to the gift: 'so pray to your Lord and sacrifice.' Of all worship, these two, the Sheikh notes, because prayer lowers the whole self and sacrifice pries loose the wealth the soul loves to keep. And both for your Lord alone, not a sliver turned to anyone else.

And the last ayah settles the insult: it is the hater who is the one truly cut off, while Allah raised His Prophet's ﷺ name over the whole earth, said in every adhan until the end of time. They thought a grave would end him. Instead he became the most remembered human who ever lived.

The lesson the Sheikh leaves you with: what you give for Allah never cuts you off. The world counts what you keep. Allah counts what you surrender to Him.
