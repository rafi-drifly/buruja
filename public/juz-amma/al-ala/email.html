The surah the Prophet ﷺ loved most

Preview: Al-A'la is a whole religion folded small. It opens by lifting His name and ends by asking a question you cannot dodge.

Assalamu alaikum,

Surat Al-A'la was one of the surahs the Prophet ﷺ loved most. He carried it into the prayers with the biggest crowds, Jumu'ah and the two Eids and the night. Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud explains why: it is the entire religion folded small.

It opens not with an argument but with a command: sabbih, exalt the name of your Lord, the Most High. To make tasbih, the Sheikh says, is to declare Allah's perfection, to clear away from Him every defect. And the root is a picture of something that floats without ever sinking, a perfection that holds its level and refuses to drop. We act it out every day in sujud, our forehead in the dust while our tongue says He is the Most High. You get as low as a body can go, and from there you announce that He is highest.

Then, instead of debating the Day, He points at the world. He created and balanced everything, evened out the fluid in your two ears so you do not topple over. He planned the universe fifty thousand years before He made it, so you would learn not to rush. He brings the green pasture up out of the dead earth, using the very verb He uses for raising people, then turns it to black stubble, a picture of every worldly pleasure and what it comes to.

And at the end He stops speaking about 'him' and turns to face you: but you prefer the worldly life, while the Hereafter is better and lasts. The Sheikh says the world wins because it is close, right in your hand, while the Garden is far and unseen. But close is exactly what the word dunya means: close, and low. He asks you to prefer the life that lasts on nothing but His word, and that preference is the whole test.

May Allah make us of those who purify themselves, mention His name, and are not fooled by what is merely close.
