The horse that would die for you

Preview: Al-Adiyat lets you fall in love with a charging war horse, then turns the lens on you. Here is the question it leaves you with.

Assalamu alaikum,

Surat Al-Adiyat opens in a strange place for a surah about your heart. It opens with a horse.

Allah swears by the war horses charging at the enemy, and Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud wants you to feel why. The desert Arab had no screens; his cinema was poetry by the fire, and the one thing he loved above all was his horse. So the surah shows him exactly what he loves: five ayat of pure film, the mares (faster than the males) breathing hard at full gallop, striking sparks off the rock in the dark, raiding in broad daylight, kicking up a wall of dust, plunging together into the very center of the enemy. The Arab is leaning forward, desperate to know what happens next.

And then the screen goes black, and the real subject walks in: 'Indeed mankind, to his Lord, is ungrateful.'

Do you see the turn, the Sheikh asks. The horse gives its master everything, even its life, when every other animal flees from danger. And you, fed and made and given your eyes and your hands by your Lord, do you show Him anything like that loyalty? The verse uses the singular, one human being, because the only person you are meant to think about here is yourself.

Then He names the disease: kanud, extreme ingratitude, the heart that counts only what is missing. Upset at the one drink not in the fridge, blind to the ten that are. And its root, the next ayah says, is a love of wealth tied too tight, money that slipped out of the pocket and into the heart, where it evicts the love of Allah.

The cure is the gentlest thing: visit the grave. Remember the Day the earth is overturned and the same wealth you chased is flung out, worthless. The Day even what your chest is hiding will be peeled out and laid bare before the One who is fully Aware.

So clean the heart now. Your clothes are for people to see; your heart is for Allah.

May Allah untie our hearts from this passing world and bind them to Him.
