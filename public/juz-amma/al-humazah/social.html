Al-Humazah
The surah that curses the man who crushed people with his tongue and hoarded his wealth, and shows you the Fire built to crush him back
cover
Surat Al-Humazah does not open with a story.

It opens with a curse: woe to a man who tears people down with his tongue, and hoards a pile of wealth he is sure will keep him alive forever.
01 / 08
He assumes his money made him immortal.

But what actually outlives you is not your wealth. It is the good you leave behind.

The coins he refused to spend will follow him.
02 / 08
Then one word slams the door: KALLA. No.

Not your wealth, not your mockery, will buy you a single extra breath.
03 / 08
And he is 'thrown' in.

The word is the one used for flinging away something worthless, the way you spit out a date stone and flick it off your fingers.

The man who judged everyone's worth is tossed aside like nothing.
04 / 08
He is thrown into AL-HUTAMAH, the Crusher.

He crushed people with his tongue. So he meets a Fire that crushes him back.

The punishment is shaped like the crime.
05 / 08
It is the Fire of Allah. Kindled, and never once let go out.

No worldly flame sits anywhere near it.
06 / 08
It climbs, slowly, over the people, until it reaches the one thing that can bear nothing at all: the heart.

Why the heart? Because that is where his sins lived. The arrogance. The love of hoarding.
07 / 08
The punishment is shaped like the crime.

Every belittling glance and every hoarded coin has a shape it comes back in.

Surat Al-Humazah, retold from Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud.

Caption

Surat Al-Humazah is nine short ayat that take one man's small, smug world, the sneer on his face and the pile of wealth under his arm, and answer it with a Fire named for exactly what he did.

Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud draws it out: the man mocked and belittled people (humazah, lumazah) and hoarded his money, sure it made him immortal. So Allah says kalla, no, and has him thrown in like something worthless, into al-hutamah, the Crusher, a Fire that crushes him the way he crushed others. It is the Fire of Allah, never extinguished, and it climbs until it reaches the heart, the very place his arrogance and greed came from. Then it is sealed upon him in stretched-out columns: he wanted a long life, and he gets eternity, inside the Fire.
