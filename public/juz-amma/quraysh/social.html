Surat Quraysh is the only surah in the Qur'an named after a tribe.

It reads like a thank-you note Allah writes on a people's behalf, because they never sent it.

Caption

Surat Quraysh is the only surah named after a tribe, and it lists four favors Allah did for them before asking for the one thing those favors were meant to buy.

Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud draws it out: Allah united Quraysh after bitter division, opened their winter and summer caravans, fed them out of real starvation, and made them safe when the world was dangerous, all so they would worship the Lord of His House. And He says 'worship,' not 'thank,' because the only real way to thank Allah is to worship Him. The favor He names last, fed from hunger and safe from fear, is the one that reaches us: when those two are handled in your life, the freed time was given to you for a reason.

Which favor stayed with you? 🤍
