Caption

Surat At-Tin opens on a fig, an olive, and two mountains, and then turns the whole oath on you.

Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud draws it out: Allah swears by blessed foods and holy lands, by the mountain where He spoke to Musa, by the city where even violence falls silent. And all of it swears toward one verse: 'We have certainly created man in the best of stature.' You were made at the very top of creation, beautiful outside and balanced within, built exactly for the worship you were asked to give. Yet the very next breath warns how far you can fall, to the lowest of the low, unless you are among those who believe and do righteous deeds.

Which oath stayed with you? 🤍
