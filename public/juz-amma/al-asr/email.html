By time, you are in loss

Preview: Surat Al-Asr is three lines long. Imam ash-Shafi'i said pondering it alone would have been enough to guide mankind. Here is why.

Assalamu alaikum,

Surat Al-Asr is three ayat long, shorter than most single verses you will read elsewhere. And yet Imam ash-Shafi'i said that if people did nothing but sit and ponder this one surah, it would have been enough to guide them.

Sheikh Abu Bakr Zoud has you start with what Allah swears by: al-asr, time. When Allah takes an oath by something, He is lifting it in front of you, telling you to look hard at what you walk past every day. And what do we take more for granted than time? It pours out of our lives all day, free and silent, and we never feel a drop of it leave.

Then, in the very next breath, the verdict: mankind is in loss. Not the wicked among them. Mankind. Every last one. The word is the merchant's word, khusr, and the Sheikh notes the phrasing is la-fi khusr, in loss: not standing on the surface of it but immersed, drowning, surrounded on every side. Time is the capital you were handed at birth, and it is being spent whether you trade with it or not.

The Sheikh lingers on how the early Muslims understood this. People guard their money, he says, but the salaf guarded their time far more fiercely, because lost wealth can return and a lost hour cannot. One of them rehung his door so it opened the other way, just to save a few steps and a few seconds. The Prophet ﷺ said two blessings cheat most people: health and free time.

And then Allah leaves one door open, four things wide: those who believed, did righteous deeds, advised one another to truth, and advised one another to patience. The first two save you. The last two are how a community pulls itself out of the loss together.

The Sheikh draws out the last two beautifully. The word for advising, tawasaw, is the word for the counsel a dying person leaves behind, so the manner of it should be a dying man's manner: sincere, gentle, urgent, and true. Often, he says, when advice is rejected the fault is in the harshness of the one who gave it. And patience comes last because the other three cannot survive without it: faith, deeds, and calling people to the truth all collapse unless something steady is holding them up. The surah that opened on time closes on patience, so that your whole life, every hour of your asr, is meant to be filled with sabr, or else it drains into loss.

May Allah make us of those who trade their hours for something that lasts.
