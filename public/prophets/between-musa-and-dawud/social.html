Between Musa and Dawud
Joshua, a plague, a hundred years asleep, and a boy with a sling

Day 25 of 29.

The corridor between two giants: Musa has just left, Dawud is about to arrive.

In between, a question your faith hangs on: can Allah give life back to the dead?
01 / 08
When the angel of death came for Musa, he struck him.

So Allah offered him more years, one for every hair beneath his hand on an ox.

And then what? he asked. And then death. In that case, let it come now.
02 / 08
After Musa, a new generation entered the holy land under Yusha (Joshua).

Victory was near but the sun was setting. So he spoke to the sun.

And Allah held it in place until the battle was won.
03 / 08
A plague came. Thousands fled their homes in terror of dying, sure they had escaped to high ground.

Then Allah said one word: die.

Then He brought them back to life. You cannot outrun the death He writes. (al-Baqarah 2:243)
04 / 08
A man asked: how will Allah bring this ruined town back to life?

So Allah made him the answer. He died for a hundred years, then woke thinking it was an afternoon.

His food: still fresh. His donkey: rebuilt from bones, before his eyes. (al-Baqarah 2:259)
05 / 08
They wanted a king. Allah sent Talut.

They rejected him: not rich enough, not nobly born.

But Allah chose him for knowledge and strength. Never weigh the messenger by his pocket. (al-Baqarah 2:247)
06 / 08
At the river, eighty thousand were told: do not drink, just one handful.

Most drank. Only a few thousand crossed.

Facing a giant's army, the believers said: how many a small company has overcome a large one, by Allah's permission. (al-Baqarah 2:249)
07 / 08
A shepherd boy with a sling stepped out against Jalut. The whole field laughed.

He said Bismillah, and one pebble dropped the giant dead.

This was Dawud. The same law would give the Prophet ﷺ Badr: 313 against a thousand. #StoriesOfTheProphets

Caption

Day 25 of 29: between Musa and Dawud. A corridor of short, half-known stories, all circling one question your iman rests on: can Allah give life back to the dead? He shows you a people restored after they fled a plague (al-Baqarah 2:243), and a man woken after a hundred years to find his food still fresh and his donkey rebuilt bone by bone (2:259), until even the one who asked 'how?' can only say, 'I know that Allah is over all things competent.' Then He proves His power on the battlefield: Talut's small obedient band breaking Jalut's army through a shepherd boy named Dawud, who said Bismillah and dropped a giant with one stone (2:246-251). 'How many a small company has overcome a large company by permission of Allah', the very pattern Allah would repeat for the Prophet ﷺ at Badr, 313 against a thousand.
