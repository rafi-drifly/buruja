A fire on a dark mountain, and the voice of the One who made him

A homesick shepherd, lost on a dark road home, walks toward a distant fire to ask for directions.

He walks instead into a conversation with God.

03 / 11
Ten years in Madyan. Married. Settled. And missing Egypt, the one place that wanted him dead.

So Musa, peace be upon him, turns back, travelling by night on roads he barely knows.
buruja.com
04 / 11
He sees a fire on the mountain.

Mufti Menk stops the story here: how often are we the lost traveller, when Allah sends up a light?

Guidance does not pour itself into you. You see it, and you walk.
buruja.com
05 / 11
Then a voice, in the dark, by name:

O Musa. Indeed, I am your Lord.

Allah, speaking to a human being directly. From that night Musa carried a title no one else has: Kalimullah, the one Allah spoke to.
buruja.com
06 / 11
Throw down your staff.

Musa does not ask why. He obeys, and it becomes a serpent.

The lesson Mufti Menk will not let us miss: the answer comes after the obedience, not before.
buruja.com
07 / 11
Go to Pharaoh. Indeed, he has transgressed.

One man, sent to the tyrant of the age. And Musa does not run.

He makes du'a: My Lord, expand my chest, ease my task, loosen my tongue.
buruja.com
08 / 11
And he asks for a helper, by name.

Appoint for me, from my family, Harun my brother.

The greatest favour a man ever did his brother: Musa asked Allah to make Harun a prophet, and He did.
buruja.com
09 / 11
Speak to Pharaoh with gentle speech.

To the man who called himself god.

No one you will meet is worse than Pharaoh. No one among us is better than Musa. So call people to Allah kindly.
