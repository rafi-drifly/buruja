They could push a thumb into solid rock like play dough, and carve whole palaces out of a mountain.

So what, they reasoned, could ever harm them?

Thamud: the nation Allah raised up after Ad.

Grateful at first. Then came the wealth, and the long ease, and slowly Allah slipped out of the centre of their lives.

Get close to Him while it is easy.

Allah sent them one of their own.

Salih, peace be upon him, from a great family, so respected they were grooming him to lead.

Then he said it: worship Allah alone. The same call as every prophet before him ﷺ.

05 / 10
So they wrote their own test, and made it impossible.

Split that rock. Bring out a she-camel. Pregnant. Deep red. Bigger than any we've seen.

The rock split. Out she walked. They called it magic.
buruja.com
06 / 10
The whole test was a drink of water.

One day the spring is hers. The next, your herds. Take turns. Touch her not.

That was all. Share, and leave her alone.
buruja.com
07 / 10
Nine men went out and hamstrung her.

Then they turned to Salih: bring on your punishment.

He said: enjoy your homes. Three days. A promise that will not be broken.
buruja.com
08 / 10
Their walls stopped wind. Stopped water. Stopped earthquakes.

So Allah sent none of those.

He sent the sayhah: one blast of sound. By morning they lay prone in the homes they trusted.

You may already recite their ending.

Surah ash-Shams swears by the sun, the moon, the soul, and lands here: Thamud, the wretched one, the camel they killed.

Salih's last words: you do not love those who advise. Be the one who does.
