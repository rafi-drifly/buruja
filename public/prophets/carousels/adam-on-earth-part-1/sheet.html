Day 3 of 29.

The first sin in creation was not Adam's.

It was Iblis, who would not bow, and then blamed Adam for the expulsion his own arrogance earned.

Iblis said he would come at you from the front, the back, the right, the left.

He never said from above.

Because from above comes the help of Allah, and there he has no reach.

Watch what happens when one of us falls ill.

We pray. We see the doctor. Two months pass, no cure.

And the whisper returns: there's another way. Come to the healer.

Same bait Iblis sold Adam. Health and wealth, forever.

Cut these lemons. Weigh each one. Bring a tongue. Slaughter a dove.

The whole time, Iblis is laughing.

Didn't I tell You I'd lead them to worship me? Tell them to pray, they won't. Tell them to cut eighty lemons, they will.

06 / 10
A prophet, the first man, why would he sin?

He forgot. No firm resolve to disobey.

But you? You don't forget. You plan it, enjoy it, and plan it again.

Watch how the question itself is a trap.

07 / 10
Before you were born, Allah drew every soul from the back of Adam and asked one question:

Am I not your Lord?

And every soul, yours included, answered: yes, we testify.

08 / 10
You don't remember that day. None of us does.

So Allah says: no matter, I'll keep sending you reminders.

That is what the Qur'an is. Belief is the oldest promise you ever made, being called in.

Follow Iblis for seventy years.

Turn back to Allah for one sincere second, and every drop of it is wiped clean.

Turning the page is not hard. But a page does not turn itself.

Adam put his finger to it. So can you.
