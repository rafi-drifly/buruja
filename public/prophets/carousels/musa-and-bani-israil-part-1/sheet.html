The sea closed over Pharaoh. They were soaked, free, alive, having walked across the floor of an ocean.

It did not last them a week.

On the road they passed a people bowing to idols.

Make us a god like theirs, they told Musa.

The people who just watched God split a sea. The crowd is never your proof.

Then they were thirsty.

He struck a rock with the same staff that parted the sea, and twelve springs burst out, one for each tribe.

Then they were hungry, and food came down from an open sky.

05 / 10
Every favour was a rope thrown down from above.

'They did not wrong Us,' the Qur'an says, 'but they were wronging themselves.'

What you do with the gift decides everything.

Musa went up the mountain for forty nights.

There he asked the one thing his heart could not stop wanting: my Lord, show me Yourself.

The mountain could not bear a fraction of that light. It crumbled to dust.

While he was gone, a man called the Samiri melted their gold into a calf.

He left it hollow, so the wind made it low.

'It has life in it,' they said. And they bowed. (al-A'raf 7:148)

A god you have to carry, preferred over the One who split a sea.
