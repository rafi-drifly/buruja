Sulayman and the kingdom, part 1

A king who asked only to be grateful

A king is buried, and his son inherits him. Not his gold, a prophet leaves none, but his throne, his prophethood, and a kingdom no one before or since has been handed.

Sulayman, peace be upon him.

03 / 10
He prayed for it after a test.

The most powerful man alive lay sick on his throne like a dead man. Not mankind, not the jinn, not the birds could cure him.

Then he understood: no one can take my life but You. And he asked.
buruja.com
04 / 10
Allah gave him the wind.

A month's journey in a morning. Another month in an evening.

And the jinn, building and diving at his command. One of the largest armies in history. And he stayed humble.
buruja.com
05 / 10
His whole army stops for an ant.

She warns her colony: get inside, or Sulayman's soldiers will crush you without noticing.

He hears her, and smiles.
buruja.com
06 / 10
And then the prayer she pulls out of him.

Not thank you, Allah. But: my Lord, ENABLE me to be grateful.

Real shukr is not a word on the tongue. It is being made into someone who truly is.
buruja.com
07 / 10
One small bird is missing from a vast army, and he notices.

The hoopoe returns: I bring news from Sheba. A queen, a great throne, and a people bowing to the sun instead of Allah.
buruja.com
08 / 10
He does not march. He writes.

Her gift arrives, lavish. His answer:

Do you try to enrich me with wealth? What Allah gave me is better. Send it back.
buruja.com
09 / 10
The throne of Sheba, carried across an empire, lands before him in the blink of an eye.

He does not say: look what I can do.

He says: this is from the favour of my Lord, to test whether I am grateful. (an-Naml 27:40)
