The magicians who believed in an instant, and the king who believed too late

Day 21 of 29.

Two men say the exact same words of faith.

Allah accepts one and throws the other back.

The only difference is timing.

The magicians believed in an instant when Musa's staff swallowed their magic.

Pharaoh threatened to crucify them.

They said: do what you want. There is an Allah who will deal with you too.

They died that day as martyrs. Their faith was one hour old.

04 / 10
Pharaoh told a nation he was their god.

Then he said out loud: I fear this man will change your religion.

A god does not fear.

The mask slipped in a single sentence.
buruja.com
05 / 10
Drought. Flood. Locusts. Lice. Frogs. Blood.

Every plague, they begged Musa: lift it and we will believe.

Every reprieve, they broke their word.

O Allah let me pass and I will never miss Fajr. Then Fajr sleeps.
buruja.com
06 / 10
One man in Pharaoh's own court believed in secret.

He stood alone and said: why kill a man for saying 'My Lord is Allah'?

When the punishment came, Allah saved him first.

Stand with the truth even alone.

07 / 10
Strike the sea with your staff.

It split, and each side stood like a great towering mountain.

More than half a million people crossed on dry ground between two walls of water.

08 / 10
The water closing over his head, Pharaoh finally said it:

I believe in the God of the Children of Israel.

The answer from the heavens: Now? When you disobeyed before?

Faith at the deadline is one breath too late.

The sea spat his body back onto the shore.

'Today We will save you in body, that you may be a sign to those who come after you.' (Yunus 10:92)

The tyrant kept as a warning.

Believe while the believing still counts.
