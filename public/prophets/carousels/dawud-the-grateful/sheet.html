Dawud, the grateful

Given a kingdom, and he stayed on his knees

Day 26 of 29.

Dawud, peace be upon him. King. Prophet. Judge. Warrior. Given the Zabur, an army, wealth beyond his age.

And still, every night, he was on his knees, weeping.
buruja.com
03 / 10
Allah gave him a voice unlike any other.

When he praised his Lord, the mountains echoed it back. The birds gathered overhead and sang it with him.

Creation let him hear, out loud, what it is always doing.
buruja.com
04 / 10
The iron went soft in his hands.

He shaped it into the finest armour of the age. And Allah did not say 'now rest.'

He said: make it well, work righteousness, be grateful.
buruja.com
05 / 10
The fast Allah loved best.

He fasted every second day, all his life. He stood a third of every night in prayer.

The Prophet ﷺ named it the most beloved fasting and prayer to Allah. A king's choice, not a poor man's.

06 / 10
We don't own a fraction of what Dawud owned.

And still we are heavy to pray, slow to fast, tired before the Qur'an.

He had the world in one hand and stayed on his knees with the other.

07 / 10
Two men scaled his prayer wall.

'My brother has ninety-nine ewes; I have one, and he wants mine too.'

Dawud ruled for him at once. Then he looked up, and they were gone. Angels.
buruja.com
08 / 10
The test was the judging itself.

He had decided for one side before hearing the other. He fell prostrate, sought forgiveness, and Allah forgave him and confirmed him.

Never rule on half a story. Never judge in anger.

09 / 10
He clutched a kingdom, and left it at a wall no king can build: the angel of death.

The only wealth that crossed with him was what he spent on his knees.

Given everything. Grateful to the end.
