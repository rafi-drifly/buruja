Back home, a blind father had never stopped waiting.

Decades. The same du'a every day. His sons told him he would die saying Yusuf's name.

But no one despairs of Allah's mercy except those who disbelieve.

Out of the prison, onto a throne.

Yusuf read the king's dream of seven fat cows and seven lean, and was set over the storehouses of Egypt.

The pit they dug to bury him became the throne they would beg at.

Then the moment the whole surah is built to reach.

No revenge. No speech. One breath:

"No blame upon you today. May Allah forgive you, and He is the most merciful of the merciful." (12:92)

His shirt was laid over his father's face, and Yaqub could see again.

The brothers finally said it: O our father, ask forgiveness for our sins, we were sinners.

The dream of sun, moon, and eleven stars had come true.

Years earlier, this surah was revealed to the Prophet ﷺ in his year of grief.

Then, at the Conquest of Makkah, he stood before the Quraysh who had exiled him and chose Yusuf's exact words:

"No blame upon you today. Go, you are free."
