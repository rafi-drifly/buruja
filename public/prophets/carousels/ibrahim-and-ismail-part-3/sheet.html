Ibrahim and Ismail, part 3

A wife, a baby, and a valley with nothing in it

Day 12 of 29.

He waited 86 years for a son.

Then Allah told Ibrahim to leave the baby and his mother in a valley with no water, no shade, no people, and walk away.

Two days of food. That was all.

03 / 10
His wife Hajar called after him: where are you going?

He gave no answer. He just kept walking.

So she changed the question: 'Has Allah commanded you to do this?'

He said yes.

And she stopped asking. 'Then He will not abandon us.'

04 / 10
As he walked, he prayed.

'Our Lord, I have settled some of my descendants in a valley without crops, beside Your sacred House, that they may establish prayer.' (Surah Ibrahim 14:37)

Not abandonment. Consecration.

05 / 10
He asked Allah for two things.

Make hearts incline toward them. Provide them with fruits.

Stand in Makkah today, crushed in millions who came from every corner of the earth, eating produce carried in from every land.

That is the du'a, answered.

06 / 10
The water ran out.

Hajar climbed Safa. Nothing. Ran down, climbed Marwah. Nothing.

Back and forth, seven times, calling on Allah.

Then a sound. And by the baby, water gushing from bare sand.

She banked it with her hands: zam, zam, stop, stop.

Zamzam.

Mufti Menk: of all the contested histories, this well has no rival account. One narration. No other.

It is still flowing, this moment, thousands of gallons an hour, drunk by millions on earth.

When you go for Hajj, you walk seven times between Safa and Marwah, hurrying through the low stretch.

That rite, the sa'i, is Hajar's exact run.

Kept alive in your body, so you never forget: effort is part of trust.

She did not wait for gold to fall from the ceiling.

She made tawakkul, and she moved her feet.

Tie your camel. Then trust.

From this child came the Arabs, and from that line came the final Prophet ﷺ.
