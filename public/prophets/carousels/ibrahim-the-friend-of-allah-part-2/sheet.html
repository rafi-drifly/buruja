He walked out of a fire as if it were a cool garden. Tonight he walks into a king's court, across a country that won't believe him, and into a tyrant's house.

And his heart never once trembles.

The secret of everything Ibrahim does:

a qalb salim. A sound heart. Not fearless, but with no defect, no share of worship leased to anyone but Allah.

On the Day that counts, it is the only thing that will help you.

The king said: I too give life and death.

Ibrahim didn't argue. He went higher:

'Allah brings the sun from the east. Bring it from the west.'

The Qur'an: 'the disbeliever was struck dumb.' (2:258)

05 / 10
At Haran they worshipped the stars, and got results.

Mufti Menk's warning: a forbidden thing that seems to work is the test, not the proof.

What is wrong stays wrong, even when it works.

Star, then moon, then sun, each one he called 'my lord,' then watched it set.

He wasn't doubting. He was building a ladder, rung by rung, until nothing was left to worship but the One who made them all.

His question that turned the room inside out:

How can I fear your false gods, when you don't fear having given Allah partners?

Which of us, then, has more right to feel safe? (al-An'am 6:81)

Sarah, alone, taken to a tyrant's chamber, prayed: O Allah, I kept myself pure, protect me.

His hand seized up. He could not touch her.

When a barrier drops before a sin, that is Allah's mercy, not bad luck.

Allah kept one word alive in Ibrahim's children: la ilaha illa Allah.

It ran down his line to Muhammad ﷺ, who cleared Makkah's idols with it.

You are one of those children. It is on your tongue tonight.
