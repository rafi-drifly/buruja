Lut and the overturned towns

One man against a people who called the forbidden normal

Day 14 of 29.

A young man watched Ibrahim walk out of a fire unburned, and believed when almost no one on earth would.

His name was Lut. Lot, in English. Ibrahim's nephew.
buruja.com
03 / 11
Ibrahim sent him north, to the towns near the Dead Sea.

There Lut found a people doing a thing no nation in history had ever done.

Allah's own word for it: al-khaba'ith. The foul deeds.
buruja.com
04 / 11
The men had left the women Allah made their mates, openly, and made it the custom of the town.

Lut held up the mirror: do you approach men with desire instead of women? You are a people gone past every limit.
buruja.com
05 / 11
Their society had inverted everything.

'Throw these people out,' they said of Lut. 'They keep themselves pure.'

Decency was the crime. They had even outlawed him from hosting a single guest.
buruja.com
06 / 11
Here is the line that reaches across the centuries:

Halal and haram are Allah's to define. Not the crowd's. Not the era's.

A whole nation calling a sin normal does not move it one inch on Allah's scale.
buruja.com
07 / 11
Allah's answer to Lut's du'a walked into town: three guests, impossibly beautiful. Angels, though he didn't know it yet.

His own wife, no believer, slipped word to the mob. They came hammering on his door.
buruja.com
08 / 11
'Fear Allah. Do not disgrace me before my guests. Is there not one right-minded man among you?'

Then the angels spoke: O Lut, we are messengers of your Lord. Leave by night. Don't look back.
buruja.com
09 / 11
At dawn: a shattering cry. Then Jibril lifted the towns on the tip of one wing and turned them over, top to bottom. Then a rain of marked stones of baked clay.

One stone was for the wife who looked back.

10 / 11
Allah left the ruin on a road people still travel, so we would see it and take heed.

In Lut's day it was one small place. Today whole nations promote it.

It remains exactly what Allah called it.
