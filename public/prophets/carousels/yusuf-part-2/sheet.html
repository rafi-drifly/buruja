Yusuf, part 2.

The boy from the well is grown now. And the test changes shape: not other people deciding his fate, but his own choice, alone, behind closed doors.

03 / 10
She shut every door. There was no one to see.

And from him, two words: ma'adhallah.

I seek the refuge of Allah.

The only camera that mattered to him was never off.
buruja.com
04 / 10
He ran for the door. His shirt tore at the back.

That tear was his proof: he had been fleeing, not chasing.

The innocent man was almost named the criminal. Allah cleared him with a piece of cloth.
buruja.com
05 / 10
She gave each woman a knife, then sent Yusuf out before them.

They were so overwhelmed they cut their own hands.

The lesson: had they lowered their gaze, the knives would never have touched skin.
buruja.com
06 / 10
Then the threat: do this, or go to prison.

His answer: 'My Lord, prison is dearer to me than what they call me to.'

He chose a cell over a sin. That is the deed that meets you at the gate of Paradise.

07 / 10
The Prophet ﷺ named seven Allah will shade on the Day of no shade.

One of them: called to sin by beauty and rank, who answers, 'I fear Allah.'

Yusuf lived that line.
buruja.com
08 / 10
In the cell, two prisoners asked him to read their dreams.

First he called them to the One God: 'Are many scattered lords better, or Allah, the One?'

He turned his worst day into a pulpit.

09 / 10
Betrayed. Thrown in a well. Sold. Falsely accused. Jailed.

Not one of those was Allah abandoning him.

Each was the road to the throne. Allah was planning the whole time.

Tomorrow: the king's dream.
