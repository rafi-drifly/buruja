Yaqub and Yusuf, part 1

A dream, a brother's envy, and a father's beautiful patience

Allah gives Yusuf something He gives no other prophet: a whole surah of his own, named by Allah the most beautiful of stories.

And it opens not with a disaster, but with a gift.

A boy wakes up bright with excitement and runs to his father.

Eleven stars, the sun, and the moon, all bowing down to me.

Yaqub, a prophet, understood at once: this child will be raised high.

04 / 10
Do not tell your brothers, the father said.

Not because the boys are bad. Because Shaytan is.

Good news in the wrong ears can curdle into envy. Some blessings you keep between you and Allah.
buruja.com
05 / 10
The brothers let it brew.

Look how our father loves him more than us. And we are the strong ones.

Mufti Menk: a problem you refuse to solve only grows. Solve it today, to their face, while it is small.

06 / 10
So they lowered their own brother into a well, a child, and told him it was a game.

But read the verse to the end:

Allah inspired Yusuf, in that very pit, that he would stand before them one day and they would not know him.
buruja.com
07 / 10
They came home weeping, with a bloodied shirt.

A wolf ate him, they cried.

But the shirt was whole. If a wolf had really eaten the boy, the shirt would have been torn. Yaqub saw it, and he knew.

08 / 10
And the words a grieving father chose at the worst moment of his life:

Sabrun jamil. Beautiful patience.

Patience that does not curdle into bitterness, makes no show of its grief, and turns only to Allah.

A caravan drew its bucket from the well, and pulled up a boy.

They sold him cheap, a few dirhams you could count on your fingers.

The pit is not the end of Yusuf. It is the first line of the dream coming true.
