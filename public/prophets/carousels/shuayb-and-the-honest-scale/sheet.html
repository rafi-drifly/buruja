Shu'ayb and the honest scale

The prophet who put faith inside the marketplace

Day 24 of 29.

Shu'ayb, peace be upon him, was sent to a town that was rich, sharp, and crooked.

His whole message lived in one place we keep at a distance from our faith: the marketplace.
buruja.com
03 / 10
His people worshipped a tree and cheated every scale.

They weighed a little light. Poured a little short. Pocketed the difference a thousand times a day.

Shu'ayb told them the scale was a matter between them and their Lord.

He was called the orator of the prophets.

So eloquent he could out-reason a whole town.

Like the Prophet ﷺ, who was unlettered, and yet so eloquent his words now anchor the grammar of Arabic itself.

Little and pure beats much and filthy.

Contaminated wealth does not stay in the bank. It takes your sleep, your health, your peace.

The sneer that survived into our own generation.

The man who prays beautifully inside the masjid, and is a crook the moment he steps out.

'My success is only through Allah.' (Hud 11:88)

You can know the right path and still not be granted the feet to walk it.

The honest trader asks the One who decides whose hands are blessed.

They jeered: follow Shu'ayb and you'll be the losers.

Then came the day of the shadow. A dark cloud, a tremor, a single cry, and they were gone where they sat.

Allah turned the word back: the deniers were the losers. Faith wins in the end.
