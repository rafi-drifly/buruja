Adam was buried. One son had already murdered another. So who would keep the flame?

Meet Shith (Seth), the son who stayed close.

He is not named in the Qur'an.

But the Prophet ﷺ told us of him: of 104 scriptures revealed to the prophets, 50 were given to Shith, peace be upon him.

The trust was not buried with Adam. It was passed on.

Two roads ran out of one family.

The faithful stayed on the mountain. The line of Qabil went down to the valley.

The law to Shith: do not mix. And the heart asked, what is so wrong with them?

It was about to be answered the hard way.

The devil never forgot his oath.

'Incite whoever you can of them with your voice... share with them in wealth and children.' (al-Isra 17:64)

The voice the scholars read as music. A battle plan filed at the start of time, still aimed at you.

A stranger came to the valley.

Disguised, patient, he won their trust, then made sounds no ear had heard: a drum, struck metal, a horn.

Saturday night, they all came for the sound. And the commands of Allah slipped quietly from their minds.

The youth on the mountain came down just to look.

They did not intend evil. But the lights pulled them, and they were seen, and the adorning began (tabarruj, al-Ahzab 33:33).

With the music came the lost gaze. With the lost gaze, the first adultery.

Why was it allowed?

Because everything within reach is a test (al-Ma'idah 5:94). The game beside the pilgrim. The fish on the Sabbath. The sin a hand's length away.

The one who gets your finger tapping can get it doing far worse.

But the door never closes.

Whoever sins, then remembers Allah and seeks forgiveness and stops: forgiveness, and gardens (Aali Imran 3:135-136).

The flame Shith carried ran on, prophet to prophet, until it reached him ﷺ. And now it runs to you. Convey from me, even one verse.
