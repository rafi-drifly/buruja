Nuh and the flood

Nine hundred and fifty years of calling, and a handful who listened

Day 7 of 29.

A man who called his people to Allah for 950 years.

At the end of it, he could count the believers on his fingers.

This is Nuh, peace be upon him.
buruja.com
03 / 10
He called them by night and by day.

In public. In private. One by one.

The more he called, the further they ran. They jammed their fingers in their ears and pulled their clothes over their heads.

For nine and a half centuries.

04 / 10
He held out the gift, not just the warning.

Ask forgiveness of your Lord, he said. He is the Perpetual Forgiver.

He will pour rain on you. Wealth. Children. Gardens. Rivers.

Istighfar is still the key. (Surah Nuh 71:10-12)

05 / 10
For 950 years he never cursed them, even when they reached for stones.

The friends of Allah are slow to call down ruin.

But when one of them finally raises their hands, the answer can come.

Never wound someone serving Allah.

06 / 10
Then Allah said: build an ark.

Nuh was no carpenter. And Allah told him to build it on a mountain, miles from the sea.

The chiefs walked past laughing. So you were a prophet, now you are a carpenter?

Laugh today, he said. A day is coming when we will laugh.

07 / 10
Then the water came from two directions at once.

The gates of the sky thrown open, pouring down. The earth split into bursting springs, rising up.

The two waters met at a point Allah had already measured. (Surah al-Qamar 54:11-12)

08 / 10
Through waves like mountains, Nuh saw his son on the wrong side.

Come aboard, my son. Do not be with the disbelievers.

I will climb a mountain, the boy said. The water will never reach me.

There is no mountain high enough to climb above the decree of Allah.

The man the world laughed at became the man the whole world descends from.

After Adam, every one of us is a child of Nuh.

His patience was the patience of Makkah, told early. And Allah ends his story with one question: is there anyone who will take heed?
