Ibrahim, the friend of Allah, peace be upon him. Part 1.

A boy alone among idols, who looked at the stone his father carved and asked: what is this, and why are you on the ground before it?
buruja.com
03 / 10
He was born in Babylon, under a king who thought he was a god.

His own father, Azar, carved idols and sold them.

The boy watched people beg for help from a stone his father had shaped with a chisel.
buruja.com
04 / 10
His people had one answer:

'We found our fathers worshipping them.'

Ibrahim refused it. Inheritance is not proof. The oldest excuse in the world is still everywhere.
buruja.com
05 / 10
He reasoned his way to the One God:

Who made me, guides me. Who feeds me, heals me when I am sick, will raise me when I die.

A stone can do none of that. I worship only whoever made me.
buruja.com
06 / 10
Alone with the idols, he smashed them all but the biggest, and hung the axe on its shoulder.

Who did this? they raged.

Ask the big one, he said. Ask them, if they can speak.
buruja.com
07 / 10
They had to admit, with their own mouths, that their gods could not speak a word.

So they built a fire so hot the birds dropped from the sky, and flung him in by catapult.
buruja.com
08 / 10
Bound, alone, not one person on his side, he said:

Hasbunallahu wa ni'mal wakil. Allah is enough for me.

Then Allah told the fire: O fire, be coolness and safety upon Ibrahim.
buruja.com
09 / 10
He sat in the inferno as if it were a garden, and walked out untouched.

The tawhid he reasoned out as a boy is your shahada. The line that runs from him reaches the Prophet ﷺ.
