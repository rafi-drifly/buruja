Ayyub and Yunus

The patience that would not break, and the cry in three darknesses

Day 18 of 29.

Two prophets. Between them they cover almost everything a human being is afraid of.

Ayyub, who lost everything slowly. Yunus, who ended up where no rescue could reach.

03 / 10
Ayyub had it all: health, family, wealth.

Then Allah took the wealth. Then the children, one by one. Then his health, until only his heart and tongue still worked.

Not one day did he complain.
buruja.com
04 / 10
His wife finally asked: how long will this last?

He asked her: how long did Allah keep me in ease?

Eighty years. And this hardship? Seven.

How do I lose hope, when the goodness so far outweighs the loss?

'Adversity has touched me, and You are the most merciful of the merciful.' (21:83)

He never even asked to be cured. He named his state, and named his Lord, and trusted His mercy to do the rest.

'We found him patient.' (38:44)

06 / 10
Yunus left his people before Allah allowed it, boarded a ship, and the storm came.

The lots fell on him. Again. A third time.

Overboard, into the open sea. And Allah sent a whale.
buruja.com
07 / 10
He called out in the darknesses.

Three at once: the night, the deep ocean, the belly of the whale.

Nobody could ever find you there. But Allah heard one sentence.

No demand. Just his Lord, and his own fault, laid bare. (21:87)
