Julaybib

From Me, and I Am From Him

We do not know his tribe. We do not know his region. We do not even know his real first name. Julaybib is only a nickname, and he walked through Medina as a man no family claimed.

This is the story of Julaybib (may Allah be pleased with him): a poor and unclaimed young man, mocked for his appearance, who was so beloved to the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ that the Prophet arranged his marriage himself, and later stood over his body and said, three times, that this one was from him.

The Companions

Julaybib

From Me, and I Am From Him

A man no one claimed

Almost nothing of Julaybib's background survives. We do not know a single other name for him. We do not know his tribe, and we do not know what region he came from. He was an unclaimed young man somewhere in Medina, and no family stood behind him.

He was also poor, with no money and no standing, and people spoke of his appearance. He was very short, and the descriptions say there was something in how he looked that others belittled. He was, in plain terms, a man who was bullied for the body he was born in.

Think about everything stacked against him at once. He had no tribe to protect him in a society built on tribe. He had no wealth in a place that respected wealth. He had a body that people mocked. Tribalism, classism, the cruelty of appearance: every one of them stood against this single young man.

Even his name was not really his name. Julaybib appears to be a small, affectionate form of the word for a woman's cloak. Some said it pointed to how he was always wrapped up as he moved through the mosque and the city. Others linked it to the darkness of his skin. Others still heard in it a kind of warmth, the name you give someone you pull close and hold tight.

Beloved to the Prophet

Whatever the name meant, we know it was not an insult, and the reason is telling. The Prophet ﷺ would change a name that carried a bad meaning, even when it belonged to the most powerful person in a gathering. He did not let people be called by cruel nicknames. He left Julaybib's name as it was, so it cannot have been one.

And the Prophet ﷺ loved him. In his dealings he would prefer Julaybib and bring him forward. If there was a gathering, he drew him near. If there was something to be given, he brought him to the front of the line. A man the rest of society passed over was, in the eyes of the Prophet, worth raising up.

Julaybib himself said that, in his early years in Islam, he found softness among the women of Medina that he did not find among the men. The harshness and the mockery came from the men, so he kept away from them. This was before the verses of hijab were revealed, in the first years of a young community still finding its way.

It is a small, human detail, and a sad one. A man hurt by people learns to avoid the people who hurt him. But the Prophet ﷺ never treated him as someone to be tolerated at the edges. He drew him in and gave him a place.

The proposal the Prophet carried himself

Among the Ansar there was a quiet custom. When a daughter or a sister became single again, the family would wait before marrying her to anyone, just in case the Prophet ﷺ himself might one day ask for her hand. They loved him so much that they left the window open, hoping for the honour of being joined to him.

So when the Prophet ﷺ came to a man of the Ansar and said he had come to propose marriage, the family was overjoyed. What greater blessing than to be asked by the Prophet himself? Then he explained that the proposal was not for him. It was on behalf of Julaybib.

The father did not want to refuse the Prophet to his face, so he went to consult the mother. She began to refuse aloud, and the Prophet could hear her from where he sat. She swore she would never marry her daughter to Julaybib. In a moment the visit had turned, in her mind, from the greatest blessing into an insult.

It is worth saying clearly that the Prophet ﷺ never forced a marriage. It was understood in Medina that if a family said no, he would simply walk away. He came as one who intercedes, carrying the request of a man who had no powerful tribe to speak for him. The position no one else would take, he took himself.

Allah will not let me go to waste

Then the daughter, who had heard everything, spoke. She asked if they were really going to turn away the Prophet ﷺ. And she said something that has outlived almost every name from her city: marry me to him, for the Prophet would never let me go to waste. If he is choosing this for me, then he knows what he is doing, and Allah will not lose me.

The narration tells us she was a woman from a notable family. She knew the talk she would face: the tribal pressure, the parents who had not wanted this, the whispers in the streets. She chose Julaybib anyway, because she trusted the one who had chosen for her. Her parents accepted the proposal.

Some of the scholars connect her response to a verse revealed about the believer who trusts the decision of Allah and His Messenger ﷺ over their own preference. She had no hesitation at all. To her, the matter was already settled the moment she learned who had come to ask.

When God and His Messenger have decided on a matter that concerns them, it is not fitting for any believing man or woman to claim freedom of choice in that matter: whoever disobeys God and His Messenger is far astray.Quran 33:36

A prayer over her life

The marriage went ahead, and the Prophet ﷺ made a tender dua for the young woman. He asked Allah to pour good upon her, and good upon that, and good again, and to not make her life one hardship after another.

It was a prayer she would need. A woman of standing was now carrying the weight of every hard look around her, simply because she had trusted what the Prophet ﷺ chose for her. What looked to the world like the lowest possible match was, in truth, being lifted by a prayer from the most beloved of Allah's creation.

Hold on to that picture, because the story will return to her. The dua spoken over her quiet, criticised marriage was not forgotten by the One who heard it.

The one who was missing

The same young man who kept away from the harshness of the men did not keep away from the battlefield. When the Prophet ﷺ went out to fight, Julaybib made no excuse. He picked up a sword and went, and fought alongside the Prophet.

When the battle was over and the dust settled, the Prophet began to ask the sub-tribes if anyone was missing. One by one they counted their men and found them all present. The heads of each clan looked around, did their headcount, and answered that they had lost no one. When he had gone through them all, the Prophet said: but I am missing Julaybib.

Picture that moment. The man with no tribe, the one no household would claim, and the head of the whole army is the one who notices he is gone. Not a clan counting its own, but the Prophet ﷺ counting Julaybib as family. He told them to go and look for him.

They searched the field and found his small body, and around him lay seven men of the enemy that he had killed before he fell. The Prophet confirmed it: Julaybib killed seven, and then they killed him. In the battles of that time, to fell seven of the opposing army was no small feat. The man others would have passed over had fought with a courage that cost the enemy dearly.

This one is from me

The Prophet ﷺ knelt over the body. He looked up to the sky and said: this one is from me, and I am from him. He said it three times. It was the kind of word every companion longed to hear, and it was given to Julaybib.

Because his body was so small, the Prophet ﷺ scooped him up in his arms and held him. He dug the grave himself, and with his own two hands he placed Julaybib in the earth. The man with no tribe and no lineage was buried by the Prophet of Allah.

The last thing the record gives us is not about Julaybib but about his widow. The Prophet had asked that Allah pour good upon her, and the dua was answered. She was blessed with wealth, and it was said that no widow among the Ansar gave more in charity than she did. We do not even know her name for certain, but Allah knew her, and that was enough.

There is a hadith that fits Julaybib as if it were said for him. The Prophet ﷺ said that there may be a person, dusty and dishevelled, turned away from people's doors, who if he were to swear an oath upon Allah, Allah would honour it. Julaybib was exactly that kind of person: unseen by the people, and held close by his Lord.

What Julaybib's life teaches us

His story is short, and almost nothing of his background is known. That is part of the point: the world overlooked him, and the Prophet ﷺ did not.

  • Allah's regard is the only one that counts. Julaybib had no tribe, no wealth, and no standing, and people mocked his appearance. None of it mattered once the Prophet ﷺ said he was from him.
  • Honour the ones society passes over. The Prophet ﷺ brought Julaybib forward, preferred him, and carried his marriage proposal himself. He raised up the man others looked past.
  • Trust in the Prophet's choice changes everything. A young woman said she would marry Julaybib because the Prophet ﷺ would never let her go to waste, and her life was filled with good.
  • Quiet faith can hide great courage. The man who shrank from the harshness of others did not shrink from the battlefield, and he fell having killed seven of the enemy.

Questions

Who was Julaybib?

A companion of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ in Medina whose tribe, region, and even real name are unknown. He was poor and mocked for his appearance, yet deeply beloved to the Prophet, who arranged his marriage and later buried him with his own hands.

What does the name Julaybib mean?

It appears to be a small, affectionate form of the word for a woman's cloak. It may have referred to how he wrapped himself, to the darkness of his skin, or to the warmth with which someone first held him and called him by it. We know it was not an insult, because the Prophet ﷺ did not change it.

How did Julaybib die?

He was martyred in battle alongside the Prophet ﷺ. When the Prophet asked who was missing afterward, he himself named Julaybib. His small body was found with seven of the enemy he had killed lying around him.

What did the Prophet say about Julaybib?

Standing over his body, the Prophet ﷺ said three times: this one is from me, and I am from him. He then carried Julaybib in his arms and buried him personally.

Why this story stays with us

We spend much of our day quietly sorting people without meaning to: who is impressive, who is worth our time, who can safely be passed over. Julaybib (may Allah be pleased with him) was passed over by everyone. The Prophet ﷺ saw him, lifted him, married him off, and at the end held his small body and called him part of himself.

Carry one thing from his life into today. Look again at the person you would normally overlook, and treat them as someone Allah may love far more than He loves any of us. And when your own efforts feel unseen, remember the man whose real name no one wrote down, who is now honoured above almost everyone whose names they did.
