The brother of Ibn Abbas, and the goat worth 500 dinars

Preview: He grew up beside one of Islam's greatest scholars, and never once envied him.

Assalamu alaikum,

Some people are born next to a brighter light. Ubaydullah ibn Abbas (may Allah be pleased with him) was one of them.

His older brother was Abdullah ibn Abbas, one of the greatest scholars the ummah would ever know. Ubaydullah could have spent his whole life feeling small beside that. He did not. He took after their father instead, becoming a merchant of Banu Hashim, vast in wealth and vaster in generosity.

There is one story that captures him. On a journey, his party got lost and ran out of food. A poor bedouin, who owned a single pregnant goat, slaughtered it to feed strangers he did not know. When Ubaydullah reached his wealth again, he sent the man five hundred gold dinars.

A servant thought it was far too much. Five dinars would have been generous, he said. Ubaydullah answered that the bedouin was the more generous of the two of them. How, the servant asked. Because, he said, the bedouin gave away all of his world, and we only gave a little of ours.

Back in Madinah, his brother's house was a university. Students lined up at the door from dawn. Ubaydullah did not envy that. He decided to sponsor it. He filled the house with trays of bread and meat, so that everyone who came to learn from Abdullah was also fed by Ubaydullah. The people of Madinah said the two brothers had taken all the good of the city between them: the most generous in knowledge was Abdullah, and the most generous in feeding the people was Ubaydullah.

There is a lesson in him that is easy to miss. You do not have to envy another person's gift. He never tried to become his brother. He found his own quiet role, the kind that asks for no applause, and he poured himself into it, trusting that Allah was keeping the account no one else could see.

May Allah be pleased with him, and teach us to give in a way that truly costs us.
