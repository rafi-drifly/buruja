He heard one sentence about money and never asked for anything again

Preview: Hakim ibn Hizam was rich, busy, and in no hurry for faith. Then he was past seventy.

Assalamu alaikum,

Hakim ibn Hizam (may Allah be pleased with him) was one of the richest men in Makkah, and the nephew of our mother Khadijah. He was born, remarkably, inside the Kaaba itself, when his mother went into labour there and could not leave.

He was a merchant, always travelling to Yemen and Syria, and he was good at it. "I made a lot of money," he later said, "and I would come back and spend it on the poor of my people." He had a kind instinct and a sharp mind.

But for most of his life he had no time for faith. He was not an enemy of the Prophet ﷺ. He was simply busy, always on the road, always trading. When the boycott starved the Prophet's clan, it was as family that he acted, sending food to them in secret at night.

When Makkah was conquered, the Prophet ﷺ named him among a few men for whom, he said, idol worship was simply beneath them. That day, Hakim became Muslim.

He still loved wealth. After the Battle of Hunayn he asked for a share of the spoils, and was given it, and asked again, and again. So the Prophet ﷺ spoke to him plainly, as an old friend: "This wealth is green and sweet. Whoever takes it without greed will be blessed in it. The upper hand is better than the lower hand."

Hakim was over seventy years old. He had looked at money the same way his whole life. And that one conversation turned it over. He answered: "By the One who sent you with the truth, I will never ask anyone for anything again, as long as I live."

He kept it. When his share of the spoils was offered to him by Abu Bakr, and later by Umar, he refused both times. He took nothing he had not earned with his own hands. He lived to a hundred and twenty, became the richest man in Madinah, and the one who gave the most.

There is a lesson in him that is easy to miss: it is never too late to change. He was an old man, set in his ways, when one sentence remade him.

May Allah be pleased with him, and free our hearts from greed.
