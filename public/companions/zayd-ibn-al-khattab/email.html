The brother of Umar that almost no one remembers

Preview: He became Muslim first, migrated first, and was martyred first. Yet few know his name.

Assalamu alaikum,

Most of us know Umar ibn al-Khattab (may Allah be pleased with him): his strength, his voice, his presence that filled a room. Far fewer know that he had an older brother named Zayd.

Zayd was one year older, and the only full brother Umar had. They grew up in the same harsh home, under a father known for his cruelty. But where Umar became fierce and commanding, Zayd became quiet. The scholars called him the silent giant.

He was tall, with an imposing presence, so tall that when a group approached from a distance, he was the only one people could make out. And yet you almost never hear him speak. In the narrations, his voice carries in only one place: the battlefield.

Here is what is remarkable about this quiet man. He became Muslim a full year before Umar. He migrated to Madinah before Umar. And he was granted martyrdom before Umar. The brother no one talks about reached each milestone first.

At the Battle of Badr, Umar saw him fighting without armour and offered his own. Zayd refused. "I seek martyrdom," he said, "the same way you seek it." So they fought side by side without it.

He carried the banner of the Muslims at the Battle of Yamama, one of the hardest battles they ever faced. As others hesitated, Zayd kept moving forward until the banner fell from his hand and he was martyred.

Umar grieved over no one as he grieved over Zayd. He used to say that the wind never blew from the east without carrying him the scent of his brother. And he would repeat: may Allah have mercy on my brother Zayd. He beat me to Islam, to the migration, and to martyrdom.

There is a lesson here that is easy to miss. You do not have to be the loudest voice in the room to be the better of two. Umar said as much about his quiet brother.

May Allah be pleased with him, and let us put our heads down and reach for Him as Zayd did.
