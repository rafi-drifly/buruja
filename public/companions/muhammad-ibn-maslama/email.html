The companion who carried the name Muhammad before the Prophet

Preview: He was the watchman of Madinah, trusted with everything, and almost forgotten by history.

Assalamu alaikum,

There may be no other companion quite like him. Muhammad ibn Maslama (may Allah be pleased with him) carried the name Muhammad before the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ received revelation, one of only a handful of Arabs who did. It was a rare name, and that single fact almost tells his whole story: a man so easily overshadowed that history nearly lost him.

He was a man of few words and great actions. When the Prophet ﷺ reached Madinah, he gave this quiet man one standing assignment: to watch. He became the watchman, the knight sent on the riskiest missions, the eye that guarded the city through every danger while others slept.

In battle they said they never once saw him take a step back. He attended every expedition alongside the Prophet ﷺ except Tabuk, and the reason was the honour itself: the Prophet ﷺ left him in charge of Madinah while the army was away.

He was trusted because he was trustworthy all the way through. The Prophet ﷺ paired him with Abu Ubaidah, the trustworthy one of this ummah, and once said that there was a man whom fitna would never harm, and named him.

His most striking moment was not a battle at all. The Prophet ﷺ gave him a sword and said, in effect: use this against the enemy, but when you see Muslims fighting one another, break this sword against Mount Uhud and sit in your house. He was not told merely to stop. He was told to destroy the means of it.

He kept that to the end of his life. When the great trial came and believers raised swords against believers, he broke his sword and withdrew completely.

There is a quiet lesson in him for an ordinary day: be reliable when no one is checking, and when something keeps dragging you toward harm, the bravest move is sometimes to step away from it completely, the way he did.

May Allah be pleased with him, and keep our hearts whole through every trial.
