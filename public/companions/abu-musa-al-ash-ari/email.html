The companions' favourite reciter was a teenager from Yemen

Preview: The Prophet would stop outside his door at night just to listen to him recite.

Assalamu alaikum,

If you had asked the companions of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ to name the one reciter they could listen to without ever tiring, almost all of them would have pointed to the same man: Abu Musa al-Ash'ari (may Allah be pleased with him). When they imagined the sound of Paradise, they thought of his voice.

He came to Islam young. As a teenager in Yemen, he heard travellers speak of a Prophet calling to one God, and he did not wait. He joined a caravan to Makkah, met the Prophet ﷺ, and embraced Islam at once. Then he went home and brought his family and his whole tribe with him.

Years later, the Prophet ﷺ would stop outside his door at night simply to listen to him recite. He once told Aisha (may Allah be pleased with her) to come and listen with him. He said Abu Musa had been given one of the instruments of the family of David, the Prophet whose voice the mountains and birds joined in praising God.

When Abu Musa learned that the Prophet ﷺ had been listening, he said, "If I had known you were there, I would have made it more beautiful for you." But the Prophet ﷺ knew the truth: this man recited for Allah, not for people.

And here is the part that stays with me. He was a jurist, a judge, a governor trusted by Umar. He could have rested on any of it. Instead, wherever he went, after the noon prayer he would ask, "Is there anyone who wants to learn the Quran?" Then he taught until the afternoon, sometimes until sunset.

There is a quiet lesson in him. Whatever we are given is worth offering back to the One who gave it. And the little we know is worth teaching to someone who knows less. He was never too important to sit down and teach one person, one word at a time.

He died at sixty-three, the same age as the Prophet ﷺ, with a pure heart and a name the world never managed to spoil.

May Allah be pleased with him, and let us hear that voice in the Garden.
