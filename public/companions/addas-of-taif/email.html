The slave who recognised the Prophet under a tree

Preview: He met the Prophet on the worst day of his life, and recognised the truth in a single word.

Assalamu alaikum,

The Prophet Muhammad ﷺ once called a single day the worst of his life. It was the day he went to Ta'if with hope, and its chiefs mocked him and drove him out with stones, along a road that ran for miles, until he barely knew where he was.

He came to rest in a garden and sat under a tree, bleeding, weeping, turning to Allah with no one in the world to help him. The garden belonged to two men of Makkah who counted themselves among his enemies. They watched him from the house. They did not bring him in. But they sent their slave out with a plate of grapes.

The slave's name was Addas (may Allah be pleased with him).

When the Prophet ﷺ reached for the food, he said, "Bismillah," in the name of Allah. And Addas stopped where he stood. "The people of this land do not say those words," he said. He was a Christian, taken long ago from Nineveh in Iraq, and in all his years of slavery he had never met anyone who worshipped one God.

"Where are you from?" the Prophet ﷺ asked. "Nineveh," he answered. "The land of my brother Yunus," said the Prophet ﷺ. Addas was startled: how do you know Yunus? And the Prophet ﷺ told him that Yunus was a prophet, and he was a prophet, and Allah had sent them both with the same message.

The two men watched their own slave bend down to kiss the hands of the stranger under the tree. Addas had found the one thing he had been missing his whole life.

There is something tender hidden here. Of everyone Allah could have sent to comfort His Prophet ﷺ on that day, He sent a follower of Yunus: the one prophet who had once given up on his people and walked away. As if to say, gently, after the cruellest rejection of his life: do not walk away. You do not know who your people will turn out to be.

We know Addas for that one moment and almost nothing else. He gave his whole heart in it, and that was enough.
