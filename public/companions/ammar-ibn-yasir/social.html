Ammar ibn Yasir
The Son of the First Martyrs

An old man in Iraq, well into his nineties.

Missing an ear. His chest and back covered in burns and deep scars.

One man finally asks: what happened to you?
buruja.com
"These are the marks of what Quraysh tortured me with in the heat of Makkah."

He was Ammar ibn Yasir, the son of the first martyrs of Islam.
buruja.com
Caption

Ammar ibn Yasir (may Allah be pleased with him) was one of the first people to accept Islam, and the son of the first two people ever killed for it.

His family had no tribe to shield them, so Abu Jahl made them an example. His mother Sumayyah was martyred before his eyes. His father Yasir died under the torture. Ammar himself was beaten and burned until he no longer knew what he was saying, yet his heart never let go of faith.

Decades later, scarred and missing an ear, he called those wounds an honour, because he had carried them for Allah.
