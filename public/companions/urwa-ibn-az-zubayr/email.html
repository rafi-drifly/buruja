The man who wrote down the Prophet's life

Preview: Much of the seerah we read was gathered in one small room, by a nephew who sat and listened.

Assalamu alaikum,

Much of what we know about the daily life of the Prophet ﷺ did not come from a battlefield or a famous mosque. It came from one small room, and a nephew who sat with his aunt and wrote down everything he heard.

His name was Urwa ibn az-Zubayr (may Allah be pleased with him). His father was az-Zubayr ibn al-Awwam. His mother was Asma bint Abi Bakr. His aunt was Aisha (RA).

He grew up admiring his elder brother, the warrior Abdullah ibn az-Zubayr. But when the time came to choose his road, Urwa chose another one. While others took up the sword, he took up knowledge. His father had warned them young: you are the children of your people today, but one day you will be the elders. And what good is an old man who is ignorant?

So Urwa did not wait. He had one advantage no one else had. While other students asked their questions through a curtain, he could simply walk into Aisha's house and sit with her. He asked her about everything: how the Prophet ﷺ lived, how he prayed, where he slept. He memorised, and he wrote. Much of the seerah we read today was gathered there.

Late in his life, his leg became diseased on a journey and had to be amputated. There was no anaesthetic. They offered him wine to dull the pain. He refused, and asked instead to be in prayer. They took his leg while he was in prostration, and he did not cry out, and he did not break his prayer. On that same journey, his young son died. His answer was a supplication: I had four limbs, You took one and left me three. I had four sons, You took one and left me three. If You have taken, You have given. I have no complaint against You.

He led no army and governed nothing. He simply sat, and wrote, and the whole world still reads it. There is a lesson in him that is easy to miss: quiet, patient work can outlast the loudest life.

May Allah be pleased with him, and let us begin our own quiet work before we feel ready.
