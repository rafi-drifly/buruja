The first man to believe said yes without hesitating

Preview: He emptied his wealth to free the weak, and feared for the Prophet before himself.

Assalamu alaikum,

Some people hesitate when they hear something new. They weigh it, they wait, they ask what it will cost them. Abu Bakr as-Siddiq (may Allah be pleased with him) was not one of these people.

He was an honest merchant of Makkah, respected and wealthy, and a close friend of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ for nearly forty years. So when the Prophet ﷺ told him that Allah had made him a Messenger, Abu Bakr did not pause. He simply said: I believe you. The Prophet ﷺ later said that everyone he invited to Islam hesitated at first, except Abu Bakr. He was the first man to believe.

He did not stop at his own faith. Islam first reached the weak and the enslaved, the people Makkah felt free to brutalise, and Abu Bakr made it his work to find them and buy their freedom. The most famous was Bilal, tortured under the desert sun. He freed men and women alike, whether or not they could ever be of any use to him. When his own father asked why, he answered that he sought only what was with Allah. He had entered Islam with forty thousand dinars, and left Makkah with five thousand, most of it spent this way.

His loyalty cost him. When the Prophet ﷺ was being choked near the Kaaba, Abu Bakr pushed through the crowd and cried out, would you kill a man for saying his Lord is Allah? They beat him unconscious. When he woke, his first words were not about himself. They were: where is the Messenger of Allah?

There is a lesson in him that is easy to miss: real loyalty puts the other first. In the cave of Thawr, with their pursuers at the very mouth, he trembled not for his own life but for his friend's. And the Prophet ﷺ calmed him with words the Qur'an still carries: do not grieve, Allah is with us.

May Allah be pleased with him, and gather us in the company of those who were first.
