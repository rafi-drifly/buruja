The believer whose own father was the enemy

Preview: She held her faith alone, then woke to a dream she could not explain.

Assalamu alaikum,

Imagine being the only believer in your family, with your own father the most powerful enemy of the faith you have chosen. That was the world of Umm Habiba bint Abi Sufyan (may Allah be pleased with her).

Her father was Abu Sufyan, the proud Makkan chief who would become the leading opponent of the Prophet ﷺ. Yet she was among the very first to believe, ahead of her whole family. When the persecution grew too heavy, she and her husband crossed the sea to Abyssinia. She made that journey while expecting her first child, and her daughter Habiba was born in exile.

Then her husband died, far from home. She was left a widow with a small daughter, no relative beside her, and no way back to Makkah while she held her faith. She was, in every sense, stranded.

It was in that stranded time that she had a dream. A voice called her Umm al-Mu'minin, mother of the believers. She woke in shock, because only the wives of the Prophet ﷺ carried that name, and she had no tie to him at all. The dream made no sense. But it pleased her, and she waited.

Then a knock came at the door. The Prophet ﷺ had heard of her hardship, and had sent a proposal across the sea, asking the king Najashi to stand in his place. She was so overcome with joy that she gave away her jewellery to the girl who brought the news. The dream had come true.

That servant girl asked for one thing in return: if you ever meet the Prophet ﷺ, give him my salaam, for I will likely never meet him. Years later, Umm Habiba carried that greeting to him, and he replied to it.

There is a lesson in her that is easy to miss: faith can stand without the family around it, and relief can come from a door we were not even watching. She held her quiet line in the emptiest hours, and she was not forgotten.

May Allah be pleased with her, and gather us in the company of those who held firm.
