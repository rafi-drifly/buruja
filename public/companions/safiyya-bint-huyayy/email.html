The daughter of an enemy who became a Mother of the Believers

Preview: Safiyya lost everything at Khaybar, and was given a place she never stopped having to defend.

Assalamu alaikum,

Some of the companions are remembered for a single great moment. Safiyya bint Huyayy (may Allah be pleased with her) is remembered for something quieter and harder: a soft heart that held its shape under years of suspicion.

She was a noblewoman of Madinah, the daughter of one of the Prophet's most committed enemies, and a descendant of the Prophet Harun. At the Battle of Khaybar she lost both her father and her husband. When she was brought before the Prophet ﷺ, he gave her a choice she did not expect: accept Islam and remain with him, or keep her religion and return to her people, free.

Her answer surprised him. She told him she had believed in his prophethood before he ever invited her, and that she had no one left among her people. "Allah and His Messenger," she said, "are more beloved to me than my freedom." He freed her, and that freedom became her dowry.

But a shadow followed her for the rest of her life. People doubted her. Her faith was questioned, her intentions were questioned, again and again, simply because of where she came from. When co-wives reminded her of her origins and she wept, the Prophet ﷺ comforted her: "What can they say to you, when your father is Harun, your uncle is Musa, and your husband is Muhammad?"

She never let the suspicion make her bitter. When a servant girl slandered her years later, she did not punish her. She said, "Go. You are free." She kept her ties with relatives who had nothing left to offer her. When the Prophet ﷺ lay dying and she wished his pain onto herself, and others mocked her for it, he defended her with his failing breath: "By Allah, she is telling the truth."

There is a lesson in her that is easy to miss: gentleness is strength, not weakness. She met cold looks with warmth, and let the truth speak for itself in time.

May Allah be pleased with her, and soften our hearts the way He softened hers.
