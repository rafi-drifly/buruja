He hated the Prophet for twenty years without meeting him

Preview: Adi expected a king in a palace. What he found undid two decades of resentment.

Assalamu alaikum,

For about twenty years, Adi ibn Hatim al-Tai (may Allah be pleased with him) hated the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. He had never once met him.

Adi was a Christian chief, the son of Hatim al-Tai, the most generous man the Arabs had ever known. His father kept a fire burning at night so travelers would know that food and shelter waited at any hour. Adi inherited the wealth and the standing, but not the open hand, and he saw this new religion only as a threat to his power.

So when the Muslim army drew near, he fled to Syria to live among his fellow Christians. In the panic, he left his own sister behind. She was brought to Madinah, and the Prophet ﷺ set her free with kindness, a camel, fine clothes, and provision for her journey.

When she reached her brother, she told him something he could not argue with. "Go to this man. If he turns out to be a prophet, you will have followed a prophet. And if he is only a king like other kings, you will find nothing from him but good."

So Adi went, braced to meet a king. On the way to the house, an old woman stopped the Prophet ﷺ to speak of her troubles, and he stopped everything and stayed with her until her need was met. Adi thought: this is not how a king behaves.

Then they reached the house. Adi expected a palace. He found a small mud room with almost nothing inside it, and a single cushion. The Prophet ﷺ offered him that cushion and sat himself on the ground.

Twenty years of resentment had no defense against this. Adi believed. And the chief who had once owned a quarter of his people's spoils became a man who longed for each prayer before it was called, first into the mosque and last to leave, for the rest of a very long life.

There is a lesson in him that is easy to miss: sincere character can move a heart where no argument ever could.
