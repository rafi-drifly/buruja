The first hand in the pledge belonged to a dying man

Preview: Al-Baraa ibn Ma'roor was the first to pledge himself to the Prophet, and he is remembered for two mistakes.

Assalamu alaikum,

On the nights when the people of Madinah came in secret to pledge themselves to the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, hands were placed in his hand one by one. On both of those nights, the very first hand belonged to the same man: Al-Baraa ibn Ma'roor (may Allah be pleased with him).

He was not chosen by chance. He was the oldest of them, the most respected, the chief of the tribe of Banu Salima. When his people needed someone to step forward first into something this serious, they looked at him.

What is strange and beautiful about Al-Baraa is that the two deeds we remember him for were both mistakes.

The first was about prayer. In those earliest days the believers faced Jerusalem, as the Prophet ﷺ did then. But Al-Baraa could not bring himself to turn his back on the Kaaba, the house built by Ibrahim for the worship of one God. So he prayed toward Makkah instead. When his companions told him he was wrong, he did not argue. He went to the Prophet ﷺ and said, simply, that he had been doing this and did not know if it was right. You had been upon a valid direction, the Prophet ﷺ told him gently, if only you had been patient with it.

The second was about wealth. As death drew near, he left a third of everything he owned to the Prophet ﷺ, out of love. The Prophet ﷺ returned it to his rightful heirs. The gift was set right. The heart behind it was honoured.

Al-Baraa died in the month of Safar, one month before the Hijra. He had done as much as almost anyone to open Madinah for the Prophet ﷺ, and he never lived to see him walk through its gate. When the Prophet ﷺ arrived and asked after him, he was told Al-Baraa had passed. Take me to his grave, he said. He stood there and prayed: O Allah, forgive him, and have mercy upon him, and admit him into Paradise.

There is a quiet lesson in him. Our effort is not wasted by our timing. He planted, and did not live to harvest, and it counted in full.

May Allah be pleased with him, and gather us in the company of the first to believe.
