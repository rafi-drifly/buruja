The man inside the Prophet's home

Preview: He built the furniture and ran the errands. The companions still named him their example.

Assalamu alaikum,

Some companions are remembered for a battle or a migration. This one is remembered for something quieter.

Abu Rafi al-Qibti (may Allah be pleased with him) was a Coptic man, once enslaved, in the household of al-Abbas, the uncle of the Prophet ﷺ. We do not even know for certain how he came to the Hijaz. What we know is what happened to his heart.

Quraysh once sent him to the Prophet ﷺ with a message. The moment he looked at him, he said, Islam was placed in his heart. "By Allah," he told him, "I will never return to them."

For a time he kept his faith secret in Makkah, even sharing a house with Abu Lahab. When the news of Badr arrived and he heard that angels had fought, he could not hold it in. "By Allah, those were the angels," he said, and was beaten for it.

Later the Prophet ﷺ freed him and brought him close. Abu Rafi had been a carpenter, so he built the simple furniture of the Prophet's house. He carried his private messages. He settled his debts. He pitched his tent on journeys. Offered a share of charity once, he refused to take a coin until he had asked whether it was lawful for him.

Then one day the companions were asked who among them had a truly pure heart: a heart with no sin, no malice, no envy. They could name only one man. Abu Rafi. He did not quarrel with anyone. He was never angry. Every time they saw him, he was smiling, busy in some quiet service, carrying nothing heavy inside.

There is a lesson in him that is easy to miss. He was honoured not for a great deed, but for what he refused to carry. A clean heart, it turns out, is a rank of its own, and it is within reach of anyone, in any life.

May Allah be pleased with him, and empty our hearts of every grudge we are holding.
