The companions who met death with a steady heart

Preview: Twenty five thousand companions were taken by one sickness. Their answer to it still teaches us.

Assalamu alaikum,

They had survived a dozen battlefields without a scratch. Most of them never imagined they would die anywhere but in the field, sword in hand. Allah had decreed something quieter.

Around the year 18 after the migration, a plague spread through the land of Sham, near a town called Amwas. By the time it passed, it had taken close to twenty five thousand companions of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, including some of the greatest among them.

The most beloved of them was Abu Ubaydah ibn al-Jarrah (may Allah be pleased with him), the man the Prophet ﷺ called the trustworthy one of this ummah. When the plague broke out, Umar wrote to him, hoping to call him to safety without quite saying so. Abu Ubaydah understood at once, and wrote back that he would not leave his people to die while he saved himself. He stayed. The plague took him.

There is one exchange from those days that believers have repeated ever since. Someone asked Umar, are you running away from the decree of Allah? He answered: I am running from the decree of Allah to the decree of Allah. If you bring your camels to fertile land or to barren land, both are by His decree. You still take the means. You simply trust the One who knows the outcome.

Another who was taken was Muadh ibn Jabal (may Allah be pleased with him), whom the Prophet ﷺ had told plainly, by Allah, I love you. The plague reached his own family. As his son lay dying, Muadh told him to hold fast, and his son answered with the words of the Quran: this is the truth from your Lord, so do not be one of those who doubt.

They were not numb to loss. They grieved. But underneath the grief was a settled certainty that what reached them came from a Lord who loved them, and that their task was to receive it well.

Most of us will never stand on a battlefield. But all of us meet things we did not choose: an illness, a loss, a door that closes. Their example is a quiet invitation to meet those moments with trust instead of fear.

May Allah be pleased with them all, and gather us in their company.
