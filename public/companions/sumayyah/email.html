The first person to die for Islam was a woman named Sumayyah

Preview: No tribe, no wealth, no protector. She held to the truth anyway.

Assalamu alaikum,

The first person in this ummah to die for the truth was not a chief or a warrior. It was a woman: Sumayyah bint Khayyat (may Allah be pleased with her).

She was elderly, enslaved, and far from the land of her birth. She had no tribe to protect her, no wealth to shield her, and no one in Makkah who thought her name worth remembering. By every measure the world keeps, she had nothing.

When she accepted Islam, she told the Prophet ﷺ that she bore witness he was the Messenger of Allah, and that his promise was true. That certainty in the promise of Allah would carry her through everything that came next.

She was among the first seven to make their faith known. The ones with no tribe to defend them were the ones made into a public example, and the worst of it fell on her. Abu Jahl, a chief of Makkah, tortured her in every cruel way he could invent, because he knew no one would come to her aid.

Every time, she answered with the remembrance of Allah. When he demanded that she curse the Prophet ﷺ, she would not. The more he hurt her, the steadier she became.

The Prophet ﷺ could do nothing to save her. He could only pass by as she was tortured and say: "Be patient, family of Yasir. Your appointed place is Paradise." He did not promise her rescue. He promised her Paradise, and for her that was enough.

In the end, Abu Jahl killed her, and she became the first martyr of Islam. She never lived to see the religion she died for take root. Yet she was the first of this whole ummah to cross into the gardens of the next.

There is a lesson in her that is easy to miss: honour is given by Allah, not by people. Makkah saw an enslaved foreigner of no account. Allah raised her to the front of the martyrs.

May Allah be pleased with her, and gather us in the company of those who held to the truth.
