He was offered half a fortune. He asked for the market instead.

Preview: The companion who feared his own wealth more than he ever feared poverty.

Assalamu alaikum,

When Abdurrahman ibn Awf (may Allah be pleased with him) arrived in Madinah as a refugee, he had left his money and his home behind. The Prophet ﷺ paired him with a wealthy man of the city, Sa'd ibn al-Rabi, who offered to share everything: half his wealth, a choice of his two gardens, even to step aside so Abdurrahman could marry.

It was an extraordinary offer. Abdurrahman thanked him, and asked for one thing only. "Just show me where the marketplace is."

He started small, selling yogurt, butter, and dates. Then horses, then saddles. He used to say he could turn over a stone and find a way to gold beneath it. Before long he had rebuilt everything by his own hand, without taking a thing from his host.

But what stays with you is not how much he earned. It is how loosely he held it. When the Prophet ﷺ called for funds, Abdurrahman would give half of all he owned, and keep half for his family. He did this again and again. It was said that the whole of Madinah lived off his charity: debts he paid for others, debts he forgave, and gifts he never expected back.

And still he was afraid. He had heard the Prophet ﷺ warn that wealth could turn people against one another, and he admitted that ease tested him more than hardship ever had. Once, making tawaf around the Kaaba, the only words on his lips were these: "O Allah, protect me from the greed of my soul."

Despite his great caravans, you could not pick him out in a crowd from the poorest of servants. He freed many people from bondage, and provided for the widows of the Prophet ﷺ as a son cares for his mother. When he died, Ali (may Allah be pleased with him) looked at the crowds who had gathered for his funeral and said, "May Allah have mercy on you, Abu Muhammad. You gained the good of this world, and you were never deceived by it."

There is a quiet question in his life for any ordinary day. What is flowing through your hands right now, and where is it going?
