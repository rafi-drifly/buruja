Three thousand men, and an army they could not count

Preview: The three companions who carried the banner at Mu'tah, one after another.

Assalamu alaikum,

Three thousand companions stood on a plain in the north and looked across at an army many times their size. Behind them was the city of the Prophet ﷺ. In front of them was a force they could not count.

Some of them wanted to send word back to Madinah and wait. One man, Abdullah ibn Rawahah (may Allah be pleased with him), refused. He told them that the thing they feared, martyrdom in the path of Allah, was the very reason they had come. They went forward.

This is the Battle of Mu'tah, and the Prophet ﷺ had done something remarkable before it began. He named the leaders in advance, in order. Zayd ibn Harithah would carry the banner first. If Zayd fell, Ja'far ibn Abi Talib would take it. If Ja'far fell, Abdullah would take it after him.

Zayd was a man who had once chosen to stay with the Prophet ﷺ over his own family. He carried the banner first, and fell first. Far away in Madinah, the Prophet's voice shook as he named the loss before any news could reach the city.

Ja'far took it next. The narrations describe it plainly. His right arm was cut, so he caught the banner with his left. His left arm was cut, so he held it against his chest with what remained of his arms. He was struck more than fifty times, and he never let it touch the ground.

Abdullah took it third. He had been the one urging everyone forward, yet he had wept before the battle out of fear for himself. When the banner reached him and his heart pulled back, he spoke to his own soul out loud, and asked it why it disliked the Garden. Then he went forward, and he too was killed.

The Prophet ﷺ grieved for the three men he loved. But the Quran had already told him not to think of them as dead. They are alive, with their Lord, well provided for.

There is a lesson here that is easy to miss: courage is not the absence of fear. These men felt it, looked at it honestly, and moved anyway.
