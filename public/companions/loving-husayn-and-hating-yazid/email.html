Loving Husayn, and the question of Yazid

Preview: A calm Sunni reflection on deep love, honest grief, and keeping the heart within bounds.

Assalamu alaikum,

Every Muslim who prays sends peace upon the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and his family. To say those words and not love Husayn ibn Ali (may Allah be pleased with him) would leave something hollow at the centre of the prayer.

Set aside, for a moment, how he died. Picture only the gentle scenes: the Prophet ﷺ lifting him, holding him, kissing him, the joy this child brought to his grandfather. Think of who his grandfather was, and his parents, Ali and Fatima. That alone is reason enough to love him. The tragedy of Karbala only deepens a love that was already there.

Around the month of Muharram, that story stirs strong emotion, and often a lot of heat. This is a calm reflection instead, drawn from Dr. Omar Suleiman's series The Firsts.

A question comes up every year: do we curse Yazid? He was no companion and no one a believer can love. When Imam Ahmad was told that some people claimed to love Yazid, he asked how anyone who loves Allah could love such a man. Yet when he was asked why he did not curse him, he answered with a question of his own: when have you ever seen your father curse anyone? Many scholars refrained out of caution, since a person's final state is known only to Allah. Some scholars did curse him. The point is this: you do not have to excuse the crime, and you do not have to curse anyone, to stand inside the mainstream Sunni position. Not one of them ever used that restraint to shrink the murder of Husayn.

There is a harder lesson underneath it. Dr. Suleiman recalls people who answered an insult at a graveside with an insult of their own: two transgressions, and nothing but loss between them. When others do wrong, we are not freed to do wrong back.

And there is comfort in it too. Husayn was killed, and still he triumphed, because his death was martyrdom. A life can look like defeat in this world and be, in the only accounting that lasts, a complete success.

May Allah be pleased with Husayn, and keep our hearts soft toward the people He loves.
