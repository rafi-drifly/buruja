The richest boy in Makkah who walked out with nothing

Preview: He had the finest silk and the best perfume in the city. Then he gave all of it away.

Assalamu alaikum,

Mus'ab ibn Umair (may Allah be pleased with him) was the best dressed young man in Makkah. He wore the finest silk and the best perfume, and had sandals made to measure in Yemen. People copied the way he dressed, and diluted their perfume just to smell a little like him. His wealth alone was said to be close to half the wealth of the whole city.

Then he heard about a quiet house where the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ was teaching a small group about one God and the life to come. He went at night, knocked softly on the door, and accepted Islam that evening, sitting among the poor and the enslaved that no one expected him to sit with.

When his mother found out, she could not bring herself to strike the son she adored. So she confined him to a corner of the house and waited for him to give up his faith. He did not. He recited the Qur'an quietly and waited, until he could slip away and migrate with the other Muslims.

Later she offered him everything back, all his wealth and his fine clothes, if he would only return to his old way. He told her to take it all, that he wanted nothing, and walked out of that house without a single coin to his name.

The Prophet ﷺ trusted him with one task: to carry Islam to the city of Madinah. He taught it so beautifully that the whole city was waiting in the streets for the Prophet ﷺ before he even arrived.

At the Battle of Uhud, Mus'ab carried the flag. When the enemy pushed toward the Prophet ﷺ, he drew them to himself instead. His right hand was cut, so he held the flag in his left. His left was cut, so he held it with his arms, until a spear took his life.

They buried him in a single cloth too short to cover his whole body: the man who once owned the finest clothes in Makkah. He never saw the ease that later believers enjoyed, and he would not have traded what waited for him for any of it.

May Allah be pleased with him, and gather us in the company of those who gave everything for what they believed.
