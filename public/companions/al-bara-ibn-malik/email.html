The companion everyone underestimated

Preview: People walked past him. His word with Allah turned battles.

Assalamu alaikum,

When people passed Al-Bara' ibn Malik (may Allah be pleased with him) in the street, they looked away. He was small and thin, his clothes worn, his hair untidy. He owned two garments and spoke very little. Most people decided he was a nobody.

They were looking at the wrong thing.

There is a hadith about a certain kind of person: unkempt, dusty, turned away from people's doors, owning almost nothing. The Prophet ﷺ said that if such a person swore an oath by Allah, Allah would honour it. Out of all the companions, the only one he named as an example was Al-Bara'.

He was the older brother of Anas ibn Malik, and the son of Umm Sulaym. He left behind no hadith of his own. His whole recorded life is battle, and a prayer that was always answered. When the fighting grew desperate, the army would call out to him: swear an oath upon your Lord, ask Him for victory, for we know your prayer is heard. He never lost.

Umar (may Allah be pleased with him) once told his governors never to make Al-Bara' a commander. Not out of any fault, but because his courage was so total he might lead an army to its end. He was the finest of soldiers, just not the right general. There is quiet wisdom in that: being beloved to Allah does not make a person right for every role.

His last act tells you who he was. At the Battle of Tustar, the Persians threw burning iron hooks to drag men away to be killed. One caught his brother Anas. Al-Bara' threw himself onto him and pulled the hook off with his bare hands, until the skin was gone, until Anas was free. Shortly after, he was martyred, and the Muslims won.

Think of what that instant held. Anas would live another seventy years, and most of what we know about the daily life of the Prophet ﷺ reached us through him. The overlooked brother saved the one who carried the most.

His story asks something gentle of us. Who have we walked past without a second look? And how is our own private word with Allah, the part no one sees?
