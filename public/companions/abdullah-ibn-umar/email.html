The companion who followed every footstep

Preview: People prayed for him to live long, so the way of the Prophet ﷺ would stay alive.

Assalamu alaikum,

There was a man in Madinah who walked with his eyes down, watching where his feet landed. He was not lost. He was trying to set his own foot exactly where he had once seen the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ set his.

This was Abdullah ibn Umar (may Allah be pleased with him), the son of Umar ibn al-Khattab. As a boy, the Prophet ﷺ used to ask after him by name. One day the Prophet ﷺ rested a hand on his shoulder and told him something he would repeat for the rest of his life: be in this world as if you are a stranger, or a traveller passing through.

He took it to heart completely. He counted the grey hairs in the Prophet's ﷺ beard. He narrated more than two thousand hadith, and it was said he never mentioned the Prophet ﷺ without tears in his eyes. When he passed on a hadith, he would not add a single word or drop one.

There is one small story that says everything. People once found him watering a tree in the desert. Why, they asked. He said he had seen the Prophet ﷺ sit beneath it once, and he did not want it to die.

His devotion was not blind. When a practice was quoted on his own father's authority, but he had heard the Prophet ﷺ say otherwise, he chose the Prophet ﷺ. He loved his father, and still chose the higher source.

He lived with little and gave away much, freeing more than a thousand people from slavery and never eating a meal without an orphan beside him. People used to pray that he would live long, so the way of the Prophet ﷺ would stay visible among them. That prayer was answered.

His life leaves a quiet question for an ordinary day: what would change if we treated the small good as worth getting right, and guarded our hearts as closely as our deeds?

May Allah be pleased with him, and let us love the truth the way he did.
