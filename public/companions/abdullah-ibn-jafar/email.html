The boy the Prophet ﷺ held in a grieving house

Preview: Abdullah ibn Ja'far was born far from home, and his life touched almost every great event of early Islam.

Assalamu alaikum,

Some companions are remembered for one famous moment. Abdullah ibn Ja'far (may Allah be pleased with him) is remembered for a whole life that touched almost everything.

He was not born in Makkah, and not in Madinah. He was born in Abyssinia, across the sea, the child of believers who had fled the persecution of Makkah. His first home was a place of refuge. He had never even seen the city his family came from.

As a young boy, he crossed the water by boat to Madinah. There he watched the Prophet ﷺ run to embrace his father, Ja'far, so glad to be reunited that he said he did not know which made him happier: his cousin's return, or the victory at Khaybar.

Then his father was sent to the battle of Mutah, far away in Jordan, and was martyred there. Both of his arms were cut from him on the field. The Prophet ﷺ came to the grieving house, and with tears in his own eyes he held the fatherless boy and promised to care for him.

Soon people began to greet Abdullah with a new name: the son of the man of two wings. For the Prophet ﷺ had said he saw Ja'far flying in Paradise, given two wings in place of the arms he had lost.

Grief returned to Abdullah more than once. His wife was Zaynab, the sister of Husayn, and the tragedy of Karbala reached his own household. At the end of his life he died as a pilgrim, lost in a flood during the days of Hajj.

Through all of it, he was remembered not for being spared, but for how faithfully he carried what he was given. It is a reminder that sometimes Allah chooses certain people for difficulty, and that the difficulty is matched by a reward. The father who lost his arms was given wings.

There is a quiet lesson in that for any of us. Grief does not have to harden the heart. It can be carried with patience, and with kindness still left over for others.

May Allah be pleased with him, and soften our hearts the way his stayed soft.
