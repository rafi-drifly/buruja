The Companions

Hasan ibn Ali

The One Who Brought Them Together

Imagine you are in Madinah, in the masjid of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. He is on the pulpit, and every eye is on him. Then a small boy comes running toward him in a little red robe, trips, and falls. He begins to cry, and the Prophet ﷺ cannot bear it. He comes down from the pulpit, picks the child up, and holds him close.

This is the story of Hasan ibn Ali (may Allah be pleased with him): the grandson the Prophet ﷺ adored, the master of the youth of Paradise, and the man who later gave up power so that Muslims would stop killing one another.

The Companions

Hasan ibn Ali

The One Who Brought Them Together

A birth the whole city waited for

It was the third year after the hijra. The Prophet ﷺ was sitting with his companions when the news arrived: Fatima had given birth to a boy. His face lit up. He rose and hurried out of the masjid toward her home, and the companions rushed after him, waiting outside so they could share in his joy.

The child being born was the son of Fatima (may Allah be pleased with her), the most beloved of his children, the one who resembled him most in how she carried herself. He was also the son of Ali (may Allah be pleased with him), the boy who had stood with the Prophet ﷺ at only eight years old and said, I will stand with you. After losing his own sons in infancy, this was a grandson of his own blood.

The Prophet ﷺ walked in and said, Give me my son. He asked what they had named him. The answer was Harb, which means war. He gently changed it on the spot. He named him Hasan, which carries the meaning of goodness and excellence. Then he called the adhan softly into the child's ear, and prayed: O Allah, put blessing in him and make him from the righteous.

A grandfather who could not hide his love

The companions were not used to what they saw. These were people of a hard land, and here was the Messenger of Allah ﷺ kissing a small boy, carrying him, playing with him. One man watched him kiss Hasan and said he had ten children and had never kissed any of them. The Prophet ﷺ replied, Whoever does not show mercy will not have mercy shown to them.

The narrations of this love are almost too many to count. He would get down on all fours and let Hasan and Husayn ride on his back, calling them excellent riders. He would hold Hasan tight and say he was like sweet basil to him in this world. One companion said that after the Prophet ﷺ died, he never saw Hasan again without his own eyes filling with tears.

Even in prayer, the boy would climb onto his back. Once the Prophet ﷺ stayed so long in prostration that the companions feared something had happened to him. When he finished, he explained that the children had climbed onto him and he did not want to hurt them by rising too soon. Another time he came down from the pulpit mid-sermon, picked them both up, and finished while holding them. He recited:

إِنَّمَآ أَمْوَٰلُكُمْ وَأَوْلَـٰدُكُمْ فِتْنَةٌ ۚ وَٱللَّهُ عِندَهُۥٓ أَجْرٌ عَظِيمٌYour wealth and your children are only a test for you. There is great reward with God:Quran 64:15

The masters of the youth of Paradise

One night a companion saw a figure standing before the Prophet ﷺ and did not know if it was a man, an angel, or something else. It was an angel that had never descended before that night. It had come with glad tidings: that Fatima is the leader of the women of Paradise, and that Hasan and Husayn are the masters of the youth of Paradise.

Scholars reflected on what this means, since no one grows old in Paradise. One understanding is that the two of them carried every quality we hope to see in the young. Hasan and Husayn each had zeal, but their zeal was paired with the things that keep it from going astray: courage and wisdom. They championed exactly what the young are meant to be praised for.

The Prophet ﷺ taught the believers that loving his family was a way of drawing close to Allah. He would hold the two boys and pray, O Allah, I love them, so love them, and love whoever loves them. He once gathered Fatima, Ali, Hasan, and Husayn under his cloak and said, This is my family, in the moment the Quran calls the believers to invoke the truth.

What he carried from his grandfather

Hasan was very young when the Prophet ﷺ died, so he held only a few direct memories of him. One was about a date. As a small child he took a date from the charity dates and put it in his mouth, and the Prophet ﷺ reached in and took it out. They asked why he would do that to a child he loved so much. He explained that the family of Muhammad ﷺ does not consume charity. It was not harshness. It was integrity.

He remembered two more lessons. The Prophet ﷺ used to say, Leave what causes you doubt for that which causes you no doubt, teaching him to trust the quiet alarm in his heart and step away from the gray areas. And he taught him that truthfulness brings tranquility, while lying leads only to confusion.

Hasan resembled his grandfather so closely that Abu Bakr (may Allah be pleased with him) once lifted him onto his shoulders and said, by my father, he looks more like the Prophet ﷺ than he looks like Ali. The likeness was in his face and his upper body, while Husayn resembled the Prophet ﷺ below. People who had loved the Prophet ﷺ could see something of him every time they looked at the boy.

These were the things a grandfather chooses to pass on: do not touch what is not rightfully yours, trust your conscience, and hold to the truth. Hasan would live by them, and they would shape the hardest decision of his life.

The year he chose peace over power

When the fitna broke out among the Muslims, Hasan was known as someone who did not like discord and tried to calm things rather than inflame them. After his father Ali was killed, the people turned to Hasan as the eldest son, and he was given their pledge of allegiance. Tens of thousands of men were ready to fight under him.

But Hasan looked at what was happening. Blood was being spilled. Families were fighting one another. The roads were unsafe and people were suffering. He decided to reach out to Muawiya and reconcile, handing over authority on the condition that the killing stop and the community be protected. When he asked his brother Husayn, who saw things differently, Husayn answered that Hasan was the elder and their command followed his.

He was mocked for it for the rest of his life. People called him weak, a man who had disgraced his father, a man who had humiliated the believers. Each time, he answered with grace. He said he could not bear to be the cause of their blood being spilled. The Prophet ﷺ had said years before, holding him as a baby, that Allah would bring two great groups of Muslims together through this son of mine. Hasan lived that prophecy. The Muslims call what followed the year of unity, and a decade of peace came from it.

Generosity, forbearance, and a quiet end

Back in Madinah, Hasan became known for his generosity. It is said that three times in his life he divided everything he owned and gave half of it away, even giving away one of his two sandals. Once he overheard a man asking Allah for ten thousand dirhams. He went home, gathered the amount, and gave it to him.

His forbearance was just as well known. One day a servant dropped a cup and broke it. The servant began to recite a verse about those who restrain their anger. Hasan answered, I am restraining my anger. The servant continued, and those who pardon people. Hasan said, I forgive you. The servant finished, and God loves those who do good. Hasan said, You are free, for the sake of Allah. Words long passed down in his name hold that forbearance is a kind of beauty, and that to be hasty is a kind of weakness.

He carried the same patience into worship. It is reported that he made the pilgrimage many times on foot from Madinah, even when he could have ridden, saying he felt shy to meet Allah without having walked to His house. There was an echo of his father in it, who had once walked the long road from Makkah to Madinah until his feet were covered in blisters.

ٱلَّذِينَ يُنفِقُونَ فِى ٱلسَّرَّآءِ وَٱلضَّرَّآءِ وَٱلْكَـٰظِمِينَ ٱلْغَيْظَ وَٱلْعَافِينَ عَنِ ٱلنَّاسِ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ يُحِبُّ ٱلْمُحْسِنِينَwho give, both in prosperity and adversity, who restrain their anger and pardon people- God loves those who do good-Quran 3:134

Buried beside his mother

In Madinah, Hasan was poisoned. Many stories surround who was responsible, but no single person can be named with certainty. As the poison overtook him, his brother Husayn sat with him and begged to know who had done it, so that he could seek justice. Hasan refused to say. I am leaving him, he said, and I will find him on the Day of Judgment.

He had asked, if it were possible, to be buried near the Prophet ﷺ. But he added a condition that revealed his whole character: if there is any objection, do not raise your voice over it. When some did object, Husayn remembered his brother's words and let it go. Hasan was buried instead beside his mother, Fatima.

They said his funeral in Madinah was the largest the city had ever seen. He died at about forty-seven years old. The one who had spent his life bringing people together, giving to them, and refusing to spill their blood, was mourned by a city that loved him. The master of the women of Paradise and one of the two masters of the youth of Paradise now rest together.

What Hasan's life teaches us

His brother is remembered for a great stand of justice, and rightly so. Hasan is remembered for something quieter that the world forgets too easily: the courage it takes to make peace.

  • Peacemaking is its own kind of courage. He gave up power he was entitled to so that Muslims would stop killing one another, and accepted being called weak for the rest of his life.
  • The reconciler is rarely thanked at the time. When you bring two sides together fairly, both may resent you. Hasan answered every insult with grace and trusted the outcome to Allah.
  • Forbearance is a form of beauty. He freed a servant who broke a cup, line by line, answering the Quran with mercy. Restraint, he said, is nobility, not weakness.
  • Let go of what you are owed for a greater good. He refused to name the one who poisoned him, leaving the matter to the Day of Judgment rather than starting a new round of bloodshed.

Questions

Who was Hasan ibn Ali?

He was the elder grandson of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, the son of Fatima and Ali (may Allah be pleased with them). The Prophet ﷺ loved him deeply and named him Hasan, meaning goodness. He and his brother Husayn are called the masters of the youth of Paradise.

Why did Hasan give up the caliphate?

After his father Ali was killed, Hasan was pledged allegiance and could have fought Muawiya. Instead he chose to reconcile and hand over authority to stop the bloodshed among Muslims, on conditions meant to protect the community. The Prophet ﷺ had foretold that Allah would unite two great groups of Muslims through him.

What is the year of unity?

It is the name given to the year Hasan made peace with Muawiya and the Muslims came together under one banner again. It opened a long period of relative peace, and it fulfilled the prophecy the Prophet ﷺ had made when Hasan was a baby.

What can we learn from the life of Hasan?

That making peace can take more courage than fighting, that the one who reconciles is often unthanked, that forbearance is a strength rather than a weakness, and that letting go of what you are owed can serve something greater.

Why this story stays with us

It is easy to admire the person who takes the bold stand and refuses to back down. It is harder to admire the one who steps back, absorbs the insults, and quietly holds people together. Hasan did the harder thing. He had the love of the Prophet ﷺ, a rightful claim, and an army behind him, and he laid it all down so that ordinary families would not bury their sons.

Most of us will never command an army, but most of us know a quarrel we could either calm or inflame. We know the satisfaction of being proven right, and the cost of letting it go. Hasan's life gently asks us to be the one who brings people together, even when no one thanks us for it, and to trust that what we release for the sake of Allah is never truly lost.
