The young man taught to lower his gaze

Preview: On the Prophet's only Hajj, a quiet correction that carried no shame at all.

Assalamu alaikum,

On the only Hajj the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ ever performed, a young man named al-Fadl ibn al-Abbas (may Allah be pleased with him) was given a rare honour. He rode behind the Prophet ﷺ on his camel, holding on as they moved through the rites that Muslims still follow today.

Al-Fadl was the eldest son of al-Abbas, the Prophet's uncle. He came from a family known for its beauty, and he was a strikingly handsome young man. That detail matters, because of what happened next.

On the day of Eid, the Prophet ﷺ came down from his camel and began answering the people's questions one by one, while al-Fadl held the reins. A young woman from the tribe of Khath'am came forward with a question. She was beautiful, al-Fadl was handsome, and the two of them began to look at one another.

The Prophet ﷺ noticed. He did not shout. He did not embarrass the young man in front of the crowd. He simply reached up, placed his hand on al-Fadl's cheek, and gently turned his face the other way. Al-Fadl turned back, and the Prophet ﷺ turned his face again. It happened three times, until he understood: lower your gaze.

The woman's question, it turned out, was a serious one. Her father was too old to perform Hajj, and she wanted to do it for him. The Prophet ﷺ told her yes, she could.

Later the Prophet ﷺ spoke to al-Fadl kindly: "O son of my brother, this is a mighty day. Whoever guards his eyes and his ears and his tongue will be forgiven." He was not humiliating him. He was protecting his reward on a sacred day.

There is something here for both sides of a correction. When we are corrected, we can receive it the way al-Fadl did, as care rather than insult. And when we must correct someone we love, we can do it gently, turning a face softly instead of raising a voice.

May Allah be pleased with al-Fadl, who stood firm beside the Prophet ﷺ at Hunayn, rode behind him on Hajj, and helped carry him to his final rest.
