The old man who only wanted to walk into Paradise

Preview: He came to faith late, with a limp, and longed for one thing only.

Assalamu alaikum,

Amr ibn al-Jamuh (may Allah be pleased with him) was the chief of his tribe in Madinah. By the time the Prophet ﷺ arrived in the city, he was already in his sixties, an elder known for his generosity, and a man with a severe limp.

For most of his life, he loved a small idol of wood. He kept it in his home, perfumed it, dressed it, and placed jewellery upon it. When Islam came, he had no quarrel with the Prophet ﷺ. He was simply not interested in leaving the religion he loved.

What he did not know was that his own wife and sons had already believed, in secret. They kept it from him because they knew how much it would hurt him, and they gently warmed him to the idea. One day his son recited him some verses of Surah Yusuf. The old man listened and admitted, honestly, "How beautiful, how perfect are these words."

His sons later took his idol and threw it in a well, tied to a dead dog. When Amr found it lying in the dust, unable even to lift the sword he had left for its defence, something broke clean in him. "If you were truly a god," he said, "you would not be here."

He believed late, but completely. He never became a scholar. His faith was beautifully simple: believe in Allah, pray, and reach Paradise. And he wanted Paradise so badly that when his sons tried to keep him, an old disabled man, safe at home from the Battle of Uhud, he went to the Prophet ﷺ in tears. "My sons are keeping me from Paradise," he said.

He asked the Prophet ﷺ whether, if he were killed, he would be able to walk on both his feet in Paradise. He had limped his whole life. The Prophet ﷺ told him: yes. At Uhud he went forward saying, "I want Paradise," until he was killed. Over his body, the Prophet ﷺ said he could see Amr walking in Jannah, with no limp at all.

There is a lesson in him that is easy to miss: it is never too late to turn. Old, set in his ways, the last of his people to believe, the door did not close on him.

May Allah be pleased with him, and grant us his longing for what lasts.
