Tortured for one word, he would not say another

Preview: He was born into slavery and crushed under a rock for his faith. All he ever said was: One, One.

Assalamu alaikum,

He was born into slavery in Makkah, a city that measured a man by his tribe. His mother had once been a princess before she was captured and enslaved, and as a boy he watched her beaten and could do nothing.

This is Bilal ibn Rabah (may Allah be pleased with him).

Then he heard of one God, the same God for the master and the slave, and something in him answered at once. He believed. He had no clan to protect him, and his master decided to make an example of him.

They left him in the desert sun without water. They dragged him bleeding through the streets. At last they laid a rock on his chest so heavy it took several men to carry, and told him it would all stop if he would just say the name of one of their idols.

He would not. All he said, again and again, was one word: Ahad, Ahad. One, One. Later, when he was asked why that was all he ever said, he answered simply that he knew nothing else of Allah to say. He had one truth, and he held it with his life.

Abu Bakr bought his freedom. When his old master sneered that he would have sold him for a single coin, Abu Bakr said he would have paid a hundred times the price. That was the community Bilal was joining: one that valued him for his love of Allah, and for nothing else.

Later, the Prophet ﷺ chose Bilal to be the first to call the adhan. Not because of what he symbolised, but because the man who had said the truth under a rock had earned the right to proclaim it from above the mosque. At the conquest of Makkah, Bilal climbed onto the Kaaba itself and called the people to prayer.

There is a lesson in him that is easy to miss: hold one thing well. Bilal knew almost nothing to say but One. A single sincere truth, lived fully, carried his whole life and raised him to the highest places.

May Allah be pleased with him, and let a little of his certainty into our own hearts.
