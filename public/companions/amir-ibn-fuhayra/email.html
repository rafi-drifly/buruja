The man who hid the Prophet's footprints

Preview: He fed two men in a cave, then walked the sheep back over their tracks so no one could follow.

Assalamu alaikum,

Some of the most important moments in early Islam had almost no witnesses. The cave on the way out of Makkah held the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and one friend. That friend had been born into slavery.

His name was Amir ibn Fuhayra (may Allah be pleased with him). He accepted Islam very early, before the Muslims even gathered in the house of al-Arqam. As a slave with no protection, he was tortured in almost every way his captors could devise. Abu Bakr (RA) bought his freedom, and Amir, owing nothing now, chose to stay close and become his shepherd.

That humble job placed him at the heart of the Hijrah. While the Prophet ﷺ and Abu Bakr hid in the cave for three days, Amir brought the flock near each day so the sheep could feed them. Then he walked the animals back over the path, so their hooves erased the footprints. Food and cover, in one quiet act, day after day.

When the three days ended, he brought the camels and rode out as their guide. On the road, when the pursuer Suraqah gave up the chase and asked for a promise of safety, it was Amir who wrote it down at the Prophet's instruction.

He fought at Badr and held his ground at Uhud. A few months later, he was sent with a group of reciters to teach people who had asked for guidance, and the party was betrayed and killed near the well of Ma'una. As a sword passed through him, Amir looked up and said five words: I have succeeded, by Allah.

The man who killed him could not forget what he had seen in Amir's face. He went to the Muslims asking what those words meant, and what he learned led him to accept Islam.

There is a lesson in Amir that is easy to miss. His greatest service was work no crowd would ever see: feeding two men in a cave, brushing away their tracks. He did it well without an audience, and he died sure he had succeeded.

May Allah be pleased with him, and give us his certainty when it matters most.
