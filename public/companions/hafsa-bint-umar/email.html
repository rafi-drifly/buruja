The wife the angel told the Prophet to take back

Preview: Hafsa was remembered not for what she did in public, but for what she did when no one was watching.

Assalamu alaikum,

Some companions are remembered for a great public moment. Hafsa bint Umar (may Allah be pleased with her) is remembered for something quieter: what she did when no one was watching.

She was the eldest daughter of Umar ibn al-Khattab, and a copy of her father in devotion. If you had met her, you would have found her doing the same two things, day after day: fasting through the day, and standing in prayer through the night. She memorised the whole Quran when that was rare, and learned to read and write when literacy was uncommon.

Her life had its hard hour. There came a time when the Prophet ﷺ withdrew from his wives, and word raced through Madinah that he had divorced them all. Hafsa wept, not knowing what her fate would be. Her father went in to ask, and found the Prophet ﷺ lying on a mat of palm fibre that had marked his side, in a room that held almost nothing.

He had not divorced them. And when the matter passed, the angel told him: take Hafsa back, for she is a woman who fasts and a woman who prays, and she is your wife in Paradise.

There is something worth pausing on here. Her rescue was not because she was the daughter of Umar. It was because of how she spent her nights and her days. Worship she did for no audience, in the small hours, was known in the heavens, and it spoke for her when she could not speak for herself.

She lived the rest of her years the same way, until it was as if she fasted every single day. The first compiled Quran was kept in her home. She cared for orphans, and when the community later tore itself apart, she quietly stayed out of it.

Most of our own worship happens the way hers did: unseen, in ordinary days. Her life is a reminder that none of it is lost.

May Allah be pleased with her, and gather us in the company of those who prayed in the night.
