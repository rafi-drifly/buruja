The conquest of Makkah, part 1
A broken treaty, and the man who came to beg
cover
Day 76 of 104.

The treaty of Hudaybiyyah held for two years. Today it breaks, and the conquest of Makkah begins.
01 / 08
An old blood feud. A night raid on the tribe of Khuzaah.

Banu Bakr struck in the dark and killed more than twenty.

And Quraysh, secretly, had armed the raid.
02 / 08
A man of Khuzaah fled to the sacred Haram and turned: I am in the Haram now, fear your Lord.

The answer was a blasphemy, and a blade.

The treaty was broken.
03 / 08
Their chieftain rode to Madinah and recited a single page of poetry: our blood, our grandfathers' treaty, our alliance with you.

The Prophet ﷺ was moved. You shall be helped.
04 / 08
Then the scene no one forgets.

Abu Sufyan, who once led armies against Madinah, rides in alone to beg for the treaty.

Door after door. Abu Bakr: no. Umar: mockery. Ali: no.
05 / 08
He turns to his own daughter, Umm Habiba.

She pulls the Prophet's ﷺ bedding away so he cannot sit on it: you are an idolater.

Nine years had turned the whole world over.
06 / 08
Ten thousand marched on Makkah in nine days.

Seven years earlier, at Badr, there were three hundred.

War is deceit, he said: misdirect the enemy, never break your word.
07 / 08
And on the eve of the march, one companion wrote a secret letter to Makkah: get ready, he is coming.

His name was Hatib. That story, tomorrow.
08 / 08
