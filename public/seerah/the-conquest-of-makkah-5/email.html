Go forth, you are free

Preview: Day 80 of 104: what the Prophet ﷺ did the hour after he won.

Assalamu alaykum,

The conquest is the hour almost no one survives with their soul intact: total power over the people who hurt you.

The man Makkah drove into the desert is now standing at the open doors of the Ka'bah, and the families who tortured his companions and plotted his death are gathered in front of him, waiting for the reckoning. He gives them one sentence: go forth, you are free.

Then, instead of revenge, the day fills with something gentler. He sends Bilal to the top of the Ka'bah to call the adhan, the same voice that cried out the oneness of Allah while he was crushed under the noon sun for saying it, now lifted above the very people who once stood over him. As you deal, so you are dealt with.

He sits on Safa, the hill where the public call began twenty years before, and the whole city lines up to pledge, the people entering the religion of Allah in throngs. He tells a companion there is no hijra after the conquest of Makkah, and a door that defined a generation quietly closes. He meets Hind, who had mutilated his uncle Hamza, and neither punishes her nor pretends the wound is nothing, he simply lets her pass. He reassures the Ansar, who feared he would forget them, that his life is their life and his death their death.

And then he waits, hand open, for three patient weeks while the hardest hearts in Arabia find their way in one at a time: the man who came to assassinate him and left in love, the one who fled to drown himself and was won back by sheer generosity, the proud negotiator, the old blind father led in by the hand.
