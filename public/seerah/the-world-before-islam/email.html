The world he ﷺ was sent to save

Preview: Day 4 of 104: the darkness before Islam, and why we study it on purpose.

Assalamu alaykum,

Before the seerah tells the story of his birth, it stops to show us the world he ﷺ was born into. And Dr. Yasir Qadhi gives a simple reason for it: we understand day through night, and white through black. Only when you feel how dark the world had become can you feel what a mercy it was that Allah lit it.

So today's entry walks back into that dark on purpose.

The Arabs had a prophet once: Ibrahim, who taught one God and no idol, and who gave them the sacred sanctuary, the sacred months, and the hajj. So where did all the idols come from? From one man, generations later, who admired a powerful idol-worshipping people, carried an idol home, and set it beside the Kaaba. It took five hundred years to spread. The Sheikh names the three reasons a single man could do it: an inferiority complex toward the powerful, impressive credentials, and plain ignorance. Then he turns to us and says we face the exact same three today.

By the end, the people were worshipping stones they had chipped from the Kaaba itself, and burying their daughters. And yet a sincere few still refused: Zayd ibn Amr, who stood against a whole city and was raised an ummah of one man; and Salman al-Farisi, born tending a fire in Persia and pulled, teacher by teacher, all the way to the feet of the Prophet ﷺ.

That is the night's gift. Allah looked at a world that had forgotten Him, and still, anyone whose heart truly wanted the truth was guided to it. Man was never created to be left neglected.
