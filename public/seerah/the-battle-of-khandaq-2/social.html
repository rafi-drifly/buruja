The Battle of the Trench, part 2
Hunger, miracles, and the night the city was surrounded

Day 58 of the seerah.

The trench is dug. The army is gathering. And from the very first day, there is no food.

The Battle of the Trench, part 2.
01 / 08
It was winter. The stores were thin, the caravans stopped, every man in the ditch.

The companions ate rancid barley paste and the pits of dates.

Then Jabir saw the Prophet ﷺ lift his shirt, and a stone was tied to his stomach. He was starving too, and had told no one.
02 / 08
Jabir had one small goat and a little bread.

He invited the Prophet ﷺ and one or two others.

The Prophet ﷺ invited the whole trench. They came in tens. Around a thousand ate their fill, and the food remained.

That is barakah: not more, but stretched.
03 / 08
A rock on the trench line would not break.

He struck it once: I see the palaces of Sham.
Twice: I see the palace of Persia.
Three times: I see the gates of Yemen.

Starving, besieged, he named the empires that would fall. And they fell, in that order.
04 / 08
The Confederates: ten thousand, from many tribes, no single leader.

Quraysh's four thousand were only the core.

The Ansar were asked to buy peace with a third of the harvest. They refused: we never knelt in ignorance, we will not kneel now.
05 / 08
Aisha said she never knew a more terrifying night.

Men patrolled the freezing trench till dawn, calling Allahu akbar so the enemy would think a host stood waiting.

The Prophet ﷺ took a watch too, and slept so exhausted they could hear him breathe.
06 / 08
Then the blow from within.

Banu Qurayzah, bound by treaty, broke it. Now an enemy sat inside the city, behind the trench, the women and children exposed.

Ten thousand outside. Two thousand within. The food nearly gone.
07 / 08
Allah recorded that night: when they came at you from above and below, eyes glazed, hearts in the throats.

The believers were shaken to the core.

To be shaken is not hypocrisy. They trembled, and they held. The tide is about to turn.

Day 58 of 104: the Battle of the Trench, part 2. The ditch is dug in the dead of winter, the believers starve, and the Prophet ﷺ feeds a thousand from one small goat and splits a rock that shows him three empires. Then, as ten thousand close in outside, the tribe inside the city breaks its treaty, and the hearts of the believers climb into their throats.
