The story of Ka'b ibn Malik
The dawn that broke after fifty nights
cover
Day 89 of 104.

Fifty nights, an entire city looking through him as if he were a ghost.

Then, at dawn, a voice from the mountain: Ya Ka'b ibn Malik, be glad.
01 / 08
Allah described his state in the Qur'an: the earth, for all its vastness, had closed in on him, and his own self had become unbearable.

Until he knew: there is no refuge from Allah except in Allah.
02 / 08
He prayed Fajr alone on his rooftop, too broken to walk to the masjid.

Then a man on the mountaintop cried out his name.

Ka'b fell straight into sajda. He knew. The relief of Allah had come.
03 / 08
A horseman galloped the whole way from the masjid just to tell him to his face.

Ka'b had given everything in charity. All he owned were the clothes on his back.

He took off his garment and gave it to the rider.
04 / 08
Out of all the muhajirun, one man stood up to greet him: Talha.

Ka'b never forgot it. Forty years later, blind and old, he still remembered.

One handshake, when the world has left you, can last a lifetime.
05 / 08
Ka'b asked: O Messenger of Allah, is this from you, or from Allah?

He answered: it is from Allah.

The pleasure of the Prophet ﷺ is immense. But Heaven and Hell belong to Allah alone.
06 / 08
Allah saved me because I told the truth, Ka'b said. So I will never tell a lie as long as I live.

Four decades on, he kept the vow. Truthfulness, the Prophet ﷺ said, leads to Paradise.
07 / 08
Allah announced his forgiveness from above the seven heavens, in Surah at-Tawbah, recited to this day.

A real sin. A fifty-night boycott. And still, complete forgiveness.

No sincere heart is beyond His mercy.

Caption

Day 89 of 104: the deliverance of Ka'b ibn Malik. After fifty nights as a stranger in his own city, the earth closing in on him, Allah accepts his repentance from above the heavens, in words we still recite. Ka'b vows never to lie again, and keeps it for forty years. One of the most beloved repentance stories in the seerah.
