He crossed a desert to kill him ﷺ

Preview: Day 43: a poisoned sword, a broken treaty, and the lines being drawn.

Assalamu alaykum,

Weeks after Badr, a rider slips out of Makkah without telling even his wife. His sword is steeped in poison. His cousin has bought his debts and promised to raise his family. His target is the Prophet ﷺ, and his cover story is the captive son he is supposedly coming to ransom.

He makes it all the way into the masjid. And then the Prophet ﷺ calmly recounts to him the secret conversation in the Hijr of Makkah that produced this plot, a conversation exactly two men on earth knew about. Umayr ibn Wahb does the math in a heartbeat: only heaven could have carried that secret. He walks in an assassin and walks out a believer, his son freed as a gift, and years later he is the one who brings his co-conspirator cousin to Islam too.

That is half of day 43. The other half is harder: the first of the Jewish tribes of Madinah, Banu Qaynuqa, breaks the treaty, a believing woman is humiliated in their market, and the Prophet ﷺ shows how a treaty ends in Islam: announced in daylight, never by ambush. A siege, spared lives, an expulsion, and a passage of Surah al-Ma'idah that quotes the week's conversations almost word for word. Dr. Yasir Qadhi tells it the way he always does: no sugarcoating, no apologies, just what they did and how he ﷺ answered it.

Badr has split the world, and every heart in Madinah is declaring itself. This day asks you the same question it asked them: when the lines are drawn, whose are you?
