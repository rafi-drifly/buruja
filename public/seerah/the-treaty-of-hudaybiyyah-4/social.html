The Treaty of Hudaybiyyah, part 4
The defeat that Allah called a clear victory
cover
Day 66 of 104.

The day the believers signed a treaty that felt like a slap, and turned home without ever touching the Kabah.

And the day Allah called it a clear victory.
01 / 08
While the terms were still being argued, a boy ran into camp.

Abu Jandal. Suhayl's own son. Tortured for years, chains still on his wrists, the lashes open on his chest.

O Muslims, save me.
02 / 08
His father pointed: this one is the first you return to me.

The Prophet ﷺ pleaded for him. Gift him to me. Again. Again. Three, four times.

Dr. Yasir Qadhi: nowhere else in the seerah does he plead like this.
03 / 08
He could not save him.

So he gave him what was left: be patient, Abu Jandal, for Allah will make for you a way out.

A Companion later called it not the day of Hudaybiyyah, but the day of Abu Jandal.
04 / 08
Umar, in agony: are you not the Messenger of Allah? Are we not on the truth?

Then why this humiliation?

He answered: I am the Messenger of Allah, I will not disobey Him, and He will help me.
05 / 08
Stand up and shave, he told them.

Nobody moved. He said it again. Nobody moved. A third time. Frozen.

Umm Salamah: say nothing. Shave your own head first. They did, the moment they saw his blade touch his hair.
06 / 08
On the road home, his face lit up.

A whole surah had come down: al-Fath. The Victory.

Indeed, We have given you a clear conquest. (48:1)

Is this a victory? Yes, he said. It is.
07 / 08
The clause meant to choke off Islam backfired.

Abu Basir, Abu Jandal, then 80 converts raiding the caravans, until the Quraysh begged him to take them in.

You cannot outwit the best of planners.
08 / 08
