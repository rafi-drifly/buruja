The campaign with no battle, that nearly broke them

Preview: Day 87: the army of hardship, the thirst, and the giving of Uthman and Abu Bakr.

Assalamu alaykum,

It is the last campaign the Prophet ﷺ ever leads, and no battle is fought in it. No swords drawn, no enemy met. And yet the companions remembered it by a name heavier than any victory: jaysh al-usrah, the army of hardship.

The order came into the hottest part of the year. March north, a thousand miles, on foot and on thirsty camels, through the desert in late July. And it fell in harvest season, the one stretch of the year a farming city earned its whole income. To go was to walk away from your family's bread, in the heat, with no enemy promised at the end of the road.

Why march at all? Dr. Yasir Qadhi weighs every explanation the historians give, a Roman army, a hostile tribe, and finds none of them hold, least of all the question of why it had to be July. His conclusion: there was no clear earthly threat. This was a command from Allah, sent to test these companions and forge the men who would carry Islam out of Arabia after the Prophet ﷺ was gone.

And the test bit hard. Two or three men split a single date for the whole day, then passed the pit between them for any taste left. The thirst was worse, until Abu Bakr begged the Prophet ﷺ to make du'a, and the moment his hands went up the sky opened. But notice, the Sheikh says: he did not call the rain until they had reached the very edge. You take the means first. Allah blesses the ending, not the wishing.

Then the giving. Whoever finances the army of hardship, the Prophet ﷺ promised, will be given Jannah. Uthman emptied a whole caravan. Umar gave half of everything he owned. And Abu Bakr, asked what he had left his family, answered: I left them Allah and His Messenger.
