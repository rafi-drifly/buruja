The seerah ends where the age of the companions begins

Preview: The final day of the seerah: the Saqifah, the first Khalifah, and the door into the lives of the companions.

Assalamu alaykum,

One hundred and four days ago, this series began before his birth, with a single question: who is he ﷺ?

Today we reach the far side of the whole life. It is a Monday morning in Madinah. The Prophet ﷺ has just passed away; his blessed body is not yet washed. And the ummah he left behind is already facing the first question of a world without him: who leads now?

Dr. Yasir Qadhi opens this final session with a careful warning, because this is where the divide between Sunni and Shia Islam begins. We walk it the way he walks it: slowly, with respect for everyone in the story, raising our voices at no one.

Today's entry is the Saqifah of Banu Sa'idah and the election of Abu Bakr as-Siddiq, radiyallahu anhu, the first Khalifah. The two Qur'anic indications the Sheikh finds substantial, both in Surah at-Tawbah: the second of the two in the cave, and the foremost of the believers. The dream of the well in Bukhari. The hand Umar stretched out to give the first pledge. And Abu Bakr's three-line sermon, the most powerful any leader gave after the prophets: I have been put in charge of you, though I am not the best of you.

The Sheikh closes a hard subject the way a teacher of mercy does: we respect every one of the companions, and on their later disputes, we keep our tongues out of what Allah kept our swords out of.

And so the seerah ends, not in grief, but in continuity. The age of the Prophet ﷺ has closed; the age of his companions begins. If your heart is not ready to leave him, then don't. Step straight from his life into the lives of those he raised, in the Companions pillar, The Firsts.
