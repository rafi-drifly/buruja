Istighfar

الاستغفار
Turning back to Allah

The Prophet ﷺ was forgiven his past and future, and still he sought Allah's forgiveness more than seventy, even a hundred times a day. Istighfar is not only for after sin; it is the constant turning of a heart that knows it can never thank Allah enough.

These are the formulas of seeking forgiveness from the sunnah, from the short word you can repeat all day to the chief of them all.

Istighfar

الاستغفار
Turning back to Allah

How to seek forgiveness

Istighfar needs no special time or place. Say it walking, working, lying awake; after every prayer, and especially in the last third of the night when Allah descends and asks who is seeking His forgiveness so He may forgive them.

Begin with the simplest, 'Astaghfirullah wa atubu ilayh,' and grow into Sayyid al-Istighfar, the chief of seeking forgiveness. And remember the Prophet's ﷺ words: the closest a servant comes to his Lord is in prostration, so make much du'a there.

Allāhumma anta Rabbī lā ilāha illā ant, khalaqtanī wa anā `abduk, wa anā `alā `ahdika wa wa`dika mastaṭa`t, a`ūdhu bika min sharri mā ṣana`t, abū'u laka bi ni`matika `alay, wa abū'u bidhanbī faghfir lī fa'innahu lā yaghfirudh-dhunūba illā ant.

O Allah, You are my Lord, there is none worthy of worship but You. You created me and I am your slave. I keep Your covenant, and my pledge to You so far as I am able. I seek refuge in You from the evil of what I have done. I admit to Your blessings upon me, and I admit to my misdeeds. Forgive me, for there is none who may forgive sins but You.

Al-Bukhari 7/150 · sahih
The Prophet ﷺ called this the best way to ask forgiveness: whoever says it by day with certainty and dies before evening, or by night and dies before morning, is among the people of Paradise.

Why we keep returning

Allah does not tire of forgiving; we only tire of asking. Istighfar is the door He left open on purpose, the one He promised is never shut while the sun has not yet risen from the west. Whoever keeps to it, the Prophet ﷺ said, Allah grants a way out of every distress and provides for him from where he never expected.

Astaghfirullah, astaghfirullah, astaghfirullah. O Allah, You are my Lord; there is no god but You; forgive me, for none forgives sins but You.

Frequently asked

What is Sayyid al-Istighfar?

It is the 'chief' or 'master' way of seeking forgiveness, taught by the Prophet ﷺ: 'Allahumma anta Rabbi la ilaha illa anta, khalaqtani wa ana abduk...' He said whoever says it during the day with firm faith and dies that day, or says it at night and dies that night, is among the people of Paradise.

What is the simplest istighfar?

'Astaghfirullah wa atubu ilayh' (I seek the forgiveness of Allah and turn to Him in repentance). It is short enough to repeat throughout the day. The Prophet ﷺ, though already forgiven, said it more than a hundred times daily.

When is the best time for istighfar?

Any time, but especially after each prayer and in the last third of the night, the time of Allah's descent, when He invites those seeking forgiveness. Prostration is also described as the closest a servant is to his Lord, so it is a powerful place to ask.
