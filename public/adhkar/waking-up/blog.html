Waking Up

أذكار الاستيقاظ
The first words of the day

Before your feet touch the floor, before the phone, there is a breath in which you were just returned to life. Sleep, the Qur'an says, is a lesser death, and waking is a small resurrection. The Prophet ﷺ met it with words.

These are the first words of the day: gratitude that you were given back your soul, and the verses he would recite as his eyes opened.

Dhikr & Du'a

Waking Up

أذكار الاستيقاظ
The first words of the day

How to begin

Say these as you wake, before the day begins. They are short. The point is to make your very first thought a turning toward the One who held your soul through the night and chose to return it.

Then flow straight into the morning adhkar once you are up. Waking, then morning: the day opens in remembrance.

4 adhkar in this wird
1Praise for being given life again
الحَمْـدُ لِلّهِ الّذي أَحْـيانا بَعْـدَ ما أَماتَـنا وَإليه النُّـشور

Alḥamdu lillāhil-ladhī 'aḥyānā ba`da mā 'amātanā wa 'ilayhin-nushūr.

Praise is to Allah Who gives us life after He has caused us to die and to Him is the return.

Muslim 4/2083 · sahih
2If you wake in the night
لا إلهَ إلاّ اللّهُ وَحْـدَهُ لا شَـريكَ له، لهُ المُلـكُ ولهُ الحَمـد، وهوَ على كلّ شيءٍ قدير، سُـبْحانَ اللهِ، والحمْـدُ لله، ولا إلهَ إلاّ اللهُ واللهُ أكبَر، وَلا حَولَ وَلا قوّة إلاّ باللّهِ العليّ العظيم، رَبِّ اغْفرْ لي

Lā 'ilāha 'illallāhu waḥdahu la sharīka lahu, lahul-mulku wa lahul-ḥamdu, wa huwa 'alā kulli shay'in qadīr Subḥānallāhi, walḥamdu lillāhi, wa lā 'ilāha 'illallāhu, wallāhu 'akbar, wa lā ḥaula wa lā quwwata 'illā billāhil-`aliyyil-`aẓīm, rabbighfir lī.

There is none worthy of worship but Allah alone, Who has no partner, His is the dominion and to Him belongs all praise, and He is able to do all things. Glory is to Allah. Praise is to Allah. There is none worthy of worship but Allah. Allah is the Most Great. There is no might and no power except by Allah's leave, the Exalted, the Mighty. My Lord, forgive me.

Ibn Majah 2/335 · sahih
If you stir in the night and say this, then ask Allah for something, the Prophet ﷺ taught that your du'a is answered; and if you rise to pray, your prayer is accepted.
3When your soul is returned to you
الحمدُ للهِ الذي عافاني في جَسَدي وَرَدّ عَليّ روحي وَأَذِنَ لي بِذِكْرِه

Al-ḥamdu lillāhil-ladhī `āfānī fī jasadī, wa radda `alayya rūḥī, wa 'adhina lī bidhikrihi.

Praise is to Allah Who gave strength to my body and returned my soul to me and permitted me to remember Him.

Indeed, in the creation of the heavens and the earth and the alternation of the night and the day are signs for those of understanding.

Qur'an · Aal-Imran 3:190-200
The Prophet ﷺ would recite these on waking. Recite from here to the end of the surah (3:190-200). Tap play to hear all ten.

Why we start here

You did nothing to earn the morning. Your heartbeat, your breath, the light, all of it was returned to you while you slept helpless. To wake with His name on your tongue is to start the day honest about who is holding it.

O Allah, You gave us back our souls this morning when You could have kept them. Make us grateful for the day, and make our first thought always of You.

Frequently asked

What is the first thing to say when you wake up?

The Prophet ﷺ taught the words: Alhamdulillahil-ladhi ahyana ba'da ma amatana wa ilayhin-nushur, 'Praise is to Allah who gave us life after He caused us to die, and to Him is the return.' It is the first dhikr of waking.

What did the Prophet ﷺ recite when he woke?

Among the things he did was to recite the last ten verses of Surah Aal-Imran (3:190-200), the verses on the signs in the heavens and the earth. They are included here with audio.

What if I wake in the middle of the night?

There is a beautiful dhikr for that: declaring Allah's oneness and praising Him. The Prophet ﷺ said whoever says it and then asks Allah will be answered, and whoever then rises to pray, his prayer is accepted.
