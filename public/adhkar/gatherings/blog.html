What to say while you sit, and the words that seal it

Every gathering leaves a residue. A sitting that begins well can drift into idle talk, a careless word, a laugh at someone's expense, and we rarely notice the moment it slips. The Prophet ﷺ knew this about human company, and he gave us two gifts for it: words to keep the heart soft while we sit, and words to wipe the slate clean before we stand.

These are the adhkar of the majlis. Say the first to keep returning to Allah mid-conversation, and the second as you rise, so that whatever slipped in is forgiven and the gathering is sealed in your favour.

أذكار المجلس
What to say while you sit, and the words that seal it

How to use these

The first is what the Prophet ﷺ would quietly repeat as he sat, seeking forgiveness again and again in a single sitting. The second is the kaffarat al-majlis, the expiation of the gathering, said right before you leave.

Both are short. Say them in Arabic where you can, lean on the transliteration while you learn, and make the closing words a habit you reach for as you stand to go.

2 adhkar in this wird
1Seeking forgiveness as you sit
عن ابن عمر قال : كان يعد لرسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم في المجلس الواحد مائة مرة من قبل أن يقوم "رب اغفر لي وتب علي إنك أنت التواب الغفور"

Ibn Umar (ra) said:

My Lord, forgive me and accept my repentance; indeed You are the Ever-Relenting, the Most Forgiving.

Ibn Majah 2/321 · sahih
Ibn Umar reported that the Prophet ﷺ would repeat this many times in a single sitting, asking his Lord to forgive him and turn to him in mercy. It keeps the heart returning to Allah even mid-conversation.
2Kaffarat al-majlis: the expiation of the gathering
سُبْحـانَكَ اللّهُـمَّ وَبِحَمدِك، أَشْهَـدُ أَنْ لا إِلهَ إِلاّ أَنْتَ أَسْتَغْفِرُكَ وَأَتوبُ إِلَـيْك

Subhānaka Allāhumma wa biḥamdik, ash-hadu an lā ilāha illā ant, astaghfiruka wa atību ilayk.

Glory is to You, O Allah, and praise is to You. I bear witness that there is none worthy of worship but You. I seek Your forgiveness and repent to You.

At-Tirmidhi 3/153 · sahih
The words said before you rise. Aisha reported the Prophet ﷺ sealed every sitting, recitation and prayer with them, and he taught that whoever says them is forgiven whatever idle talk slipped into the gathering.

Sealing the sitting

There is a mercy in being given a way to undo what a gathering may have spoiled. The Prophet ﷺ said that whoever sits in a gathering with much idle talk and then says the words of expiation before rising will have it forgiven. So nothing has to be carried out of the room with you.

O Allah, glory and praise are Yours. We bear witness that there is none worthy of worship but You. Forgive whatever we let slip as we sat, and let us rise lighter than we came.

Frequently asked

What is kaffarat al-majlis?

It is the expiation of the gathering, a short du'a the Prophet ﷺ taught us to say before leaving any sitting. He said that whoever says it will be forgiven whatever slipped into the gathering, such as idle or careless talk. It seals the sitting on a clean note.

When exactly do I say it?

Right at the end, as you are about to get up and leave the gathering. Aisha reported that the Prophet ﷺ did not sit in a gathering, recite Qur'an, or pray without sealing it with these words.

Is there something to say during the gathering too?

Yes. Ibn Umar reported that the Prophet ﷺ would seek forgiveness many times in a single sitting, asking his Lord to forgive him and turn to him in mercy. It keeps the heart returning to Allah even in the middle of company.
