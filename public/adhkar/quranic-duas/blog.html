أدعية قرآنية
The supplications Allah taught in His Book

When Allah wanted to teach us how to ask, He put the words in His own Book. The Qur'an is full of du'as: the cry of Adam after he slipped, of Yunus from inside the whale, of Ibrahim for his children, of ordinary believers asking for good in both worlds.

To make these du'as is to ask Allah in the very words He chose. There is no safer way to speak to Him than with the speech He revealed.

How to use these

These are not tied to a time. Reach for them in sujood, after prayer, in the last third of the night, or whenever your heart needs words. Many begin with 'Rabbana' (Our Lord) or 'Rabbi' (My Lord), the gentle, intimate way the Qur'an teaches us to open.

Each card shows the supplication from its verse, with the meaning and the reference. The recitation plays the full ayah. Learn a few by heart; they will come to you exactly when you need them.

13 adhkar in this wird
1Good in this world and the next
رَبَّنَآ ءَاتِنَا فِى ٱلدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً وَفِى ٱلْءَاخِرَةِ حَسَنَةً وَقِنَا عَذَابَ ٱلنَّارِ

Our Lord, give us in this world [that which is] good and in the Hereafter [that which is] good and protect us from the punishment of the Fire.

Qur'an · Al-Baqarah 2:201
The du'a the Prophet ﷺ made more than any other.
2Patience and steadfastness
رَبَّنَآ أَفْرِغْ عَلَيْنَا صَبْرًا وَثَبِّتْ أَقْدَامَنَا وَٱنصُرْنَا عَلَى ٱلْقَوْمِ ٱلْكَٰفِرِينَ

Our Lord, pour upon us patience and plant firmly our feet and give us victory over the disbelieving people.

Qur'an · Al-Baqarah 2:250
3Keep my heart from going astray
رَبَّنَا لَا تُزِغْ قُلُوبَنَا بَعْدَ إِذْ هَدَيْتَنَا وَهَبْ لَنَا مِن لَّدُنكَ رَحْمَةً ۚ إِنَّكَ أَنتَ ٱلْوَهَّابُ

Our Lord, let not our hearts deviate after You have guided us and grant us from Yourself mercy. Indeed, You are the Bestower.

Qur'an · Aal-Imran 3:8
A du'a for the gift you can lose: guidance itself.
4We have believed, so forgive us
رَبَّنَآ إِنَّنَآ ءَامَنَّا فَٱغْفِرْ لَنَا ذُنُوبَنَا وَقِنَا عَذَابَ ٱلنَّارِ

Those who say, 'Our Lord, indeed we have believed, so forgive us our sins and protect us from the punishment of the Fire,'

Qur'an · Aal-Imran 3:16
5The du'a of Adam, turning back after sin
رَبَّنَا ظَلَمْنَآ أَنفُسَنَا وَإِن لَّمْ تَغْفِرْ لَنَا وَتَرْحَمْنَا لَنَكُونَنَّ مِنَ ٱلْخَٰسِرِينَ

Our Lord, we have wronged ourselves, and if You do not forgive us and have mercy upon us, we will surely be among the losers.

Qur'an · Al-A'raf 7:23
The words Adam said after he slipped. Carry them when you fall.
6Make me steadfast in prayer, the du'a of Ibrahim
رَبِّ ٱجْعَلْنِى مُقِيمَ ٱلصَّلَوٰةِ وَمِن ذُرِّيَّتِى ۚ رَبَّنَا وَتَقَبَّلْ دُعَآءِ رَبَّنَا ٱغْفِرْ لِى وَلِوَٰلِدَىَّ وَلِلْمُؤْمِنِينَ يَوْمَ يَقُومُ ٱلْحِسَابُ

My Lord, make me an establisher of prayer, and [many] from my descendants. Our Lord, and accept my supplication. Our Lord, forgive me and my parents and the believers the Day the account is established.

Qur'an · Ibrahim 14:40-41
7Increase me in knowledge
رَّبِّ زِدْنِى عِلْمًا

My Lord, increase me in knowledge.

Qur'an · Ta-Ha 20:114
The only thing Allah told His Prophet ﷺ to ask for more of.
8The du'a of Yunus, from the depths
لَّآ إِلَٰهَ إِلَّآ أَنتَ سُبْحَٰنَكَ إِنِّى كُنتُ مِنَ ٱلظَّٰلِمِينَ

There is no deity except You; exalted are You. Indeed, I have been of the wrongdoers.

Qur'an · Al-Anbiya 21:87
The Prophet ﷺ said no Muslim ever supplicates with it for anything but that Allah answers him.
9Forgive us and have mercy
رَبَّنَآ ءَامَنَّا فَٱغْفِرْ لَنَا وَٱرْحَمْنَا وَأَنتَ خَيْرُ ٱلرَّٰحِمِينَ

Indeed, there was a party of My servants who said, 'Our Lord, we have believed, so forgive us and have mercy upon us, and You are the best of the merciful.'

Qur'an · Al-Mu'minun 23:109
10Coolness of the eyes, for spouse and children
رَبَّنَا هَبْ لَنَا مِنْ أَزْوَٰجِنَا وَذُرِّيَّٰتِنَا قُرَّةَ أَعْيُنٍ وَٱجْعَلْنَا لِلْمُتَّقِينَ إِمَامًا

Our Lord, grant us from among our wives and offspring comfort to our eyes and make us a leader for the righteous.

Qur'an · Al-Furqan 25:74
11In need of whatever good You send, the du'a of Musa
رَبِّ إِنِّى لِمَآ أَنزَلْتَ إِلَىَّ مِنْ خَيْرٍ فَقِيرٌ

My Lord, indeed I am, for whatever good You would send down to me, in need.

Qur'an · Al-Qasas 28:24
Musa said it alone, broke, and a stranger with nothing. Days later he had work, a home and a wife.
12For those who came before us in faith
رَبَّنَا ٱغْفِرْ لَنَا وَلِإِخْوَٰنِنَا ٱلَّذِينَ سَبَقُونَا بِٱلْإِيمَٰنِ وَلَا تَجْعَلْ فِى قُلُوبِنَا غِلًّا لِّلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ رَبَّنَآ إِنَّكَ رَءُوفٌ رَّحِيمٌ

Our Lord, forgive us and our brothers who preceded us in faith and put not in our hearts [any] resentment toward those who have believed. Our Lord, indeed You are Kind and Merciful.

Qur'an · Al-Hashr 59:10
13Complete our light
رَبَّنَآ أَتْمِمْ لَنَا نُورَنَا وَٱغْفِرْ لَنَآ ۖ إِنَّكَ عَلَىٰ كُلِّ شَىْءٍ قَدِيرٌ

Our Lord, perfect for us our light and forgive us. Indeed, You are over all things competent.

Qur'an · At-Tahrim 66:8

Why we ask in His words

Every one of these du'as was answered. Adam was forgiven, Yunus was lifted from the dark, Musa was given work and a home and a family within days of his prayer. When you borrow their words, you join a line of the answered stretching back to the first human being.

Our Lord, give us good in this world and good in the next, and protect us from the Fire. Our Lord, accept it from us; indeed You are the Hearing, the Knowing.

Frequently asked

What are the Qur'anic du'as?

They are the supplications that appear in the Qur'an itself, many beginning with 'Rabbana' (Our Lord). They include the du'a of Adam, of Yunus in the whale, of Ibrahim, of Musa, and the prayers of the believers for good in both worlds, for steadfastness, for a righteous family and for forgiveness.

Why make du'a in the words of the Qur'an?

Because they are the words Allah Himself chose and praised. Asking with them is asking in speech that is guaranteed to be pure, complete and beloved to Him. They are also easy to memorise and stay with you for life.

What is the most comprehensive du'a in the Qur'an?

Many scholars point to 'Rabbana atina fid-dunya hasanah wa fil-akhirati hasanah wa qina adhab an-nar' (2:201), 'Our Lord, give us good in this world and good in the Hereafter and protect us from the Fire.' The Prophet ﷺ made this du'a more than any other.
