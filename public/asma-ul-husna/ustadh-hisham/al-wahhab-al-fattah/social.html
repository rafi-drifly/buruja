Al-Wahhab and Al-Fattah.

The Gift-Giver and the Opener.

Two names of Allah made for the moments you most need Him: hands open, asking. Ustadh Hisham teaches them as the nights of du'a draw near.
01 / 08
Al-Wahhab comes from the root for a gift.

A gift is the one exchange where nothing comes back. It arrives as a surprise. It has nothing to do with your rank.

And the form means: the constant Giver, whose gifts are immense.
02 / 08
The gifts Allah names in the Qur'an:

Faith. The greatest of all.
Children, and family who are the coolness of your eyes.
Power, like Sulayman's kingdom.
A clean heart and good character.

None of them can be bought.
03 / 08
Sulayman made a mistake, then asked Allah for the greatest kingdom in history.

He was calling on Al-Wahhab, and he knew nothing is too big to ask.

Ask Allah for the penthouse, not the basement. He gives according to His means, not your imagination.
04 / 08
Al-Fattah is the One who opens the doors that have closed on you.

A job. A marriage. Peace of mind.

When you hit a wall, do not try to break the door with your own hands. Ask the One who holds the key.
05 / 08
At Hudaybiyyah, the treaty looked like a defeat. The companions were crushed.

Then Allah called it a clear opening.

Two years of peace. 1,400 Muslims became 10,000.

Sometimes Allah sends His gifts wrapped in His calamities.
06 / 08
He opens a different door for everyone.

One memorises with ease. Another gives charity no one can match, or cares for an ill parent.

If your door feels narrow, ask Al-Fattah for more. He holds every key.
07 / 08
Yusuf in the well. Musa in exile. They knew no one but Allah.

Stop asking Him last, after everyone else has failed.

And if the answer is slow, remember: His delay is not His denial.

Ya Wahhab, Ya Fattah. 🤍

Caption

Two names of Allah for the moments you most need Him, and Ustadh Hisham Abu Yusuf teaches them as the nights of du'a draw near.

Al-Wahhab is the Gift-Giver. The word comes from the root for a gift, the one exchange where nothing comes back, where the present arrives as a surprise and owes nothing to your rank. The blessings Allah names this way, faith, children, family, dominion, a clean heart, all share that fingerprint: they cannot be bought. So when Sulayman slipped and came back to Allah, he did not ask small. He asked for the greatest kingdom in history, because he knew nothing is too big to ask Al-Wahhab. Ask Him for the highest part of Paradise, not the basement.

Al-Fattah is the Opener, the One who opens the doors that have shut on you. At Hudaybiyyah the treaty looked like a defeat, and then Allah called it a clear opening: two years of peace in which the Muslims grew tenfold. Sometimes Allah sends His gifts wrapped in His calamities.

Which door are you asking Him to open? 🤍
