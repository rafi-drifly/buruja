The insurance the world cannot sell you

Preview: Al-Mu'min, the Giver of Security, and the three policies Allah already wrote for you.

Assalamu alaikum,

This is one of the few names of Allah that stops you the first time you hear it. Al-Mu'min, the Giver of Security. You already know this word, but you know it as a description of yourself: a believer is a mu'min. So how can a word we give to the lowliest worshipper be a name of the Most High?

Ustadh Hisham Abu Yusuf opens it at the root. The letters a-m-n carry the meaning of safety, security, and freedom from fear. A believer is called a mu'min because faith makes the heart safe. Allah is Al-Mu'min because He is the One who makes everything safe. The name appears as His only once in the entire Qur'an, tucked into a single verse in Surah Al-Hashr.

Then comes the gift. The Giver of Security, he shows you, has already written you three protections you never had to buy. He calls them, with a smile, your three kinds of insurance.

The first is the Sacred Law itself, which guards your mind, your family, and your soul from the things that would destroy them. Not a cage, a wall of safety. The second is the community: the brother who lends before you ask, the people who carry you when you stumble, the bond the Prophet ﷺ built in Madinah by pairing every migrant with a helper. And the third, the greatest, is faith, which secures you from the Fire forever, the security the Qur'an promises to those who believe and do not stain their faith with wrongdoing.

Meanwhile the world spends a quarter of a trillion a year on insurance, an idea barely two centuries old, born the moment people stopped believing that Allah is their protection. Ibrahim left his family in a barren valley with no deposit and no plan B. His only policy was Allah, and that is where real trust comes from: the heart with no net beneath it.

None of this means sitting still. You tie the camel, then you trust. The Prophet ﷺ sought safety through his uncle, a Christian king, and the tribe of Ta'if, by every lawful means. So feel safe in your worldly life, because the Giver of Security is your Protector. But never feel safe about your faith, because even Imam Ahmad, on his deathbed, refused to say 'I am saved' until his soul was taken.

Ya Mu'min, make us safe in our deen and our dunya, and gather us where there is no fear and no grief.
