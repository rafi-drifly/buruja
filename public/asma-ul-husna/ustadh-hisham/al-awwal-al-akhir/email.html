The One still standing when everything is gone

Preview: Al-Awwal and Al-Akhir fix the two ends of everything. Here is what they say about the middle you are living in.

Assalamu alaikum,

This is the last set of names on the journey, and it gathers up everything that came before: Al-Awwal and Al-Akhir, the First and the Last, and Al-Wahid and Al-Ahad, the One and the Only.

Most teachers hand you a name on its own. But Allah never revealed a name on its own. He set it inside a surah and a story, and where He places it is half the meaning. So instead of just defining these names, Ustadh Hisham stands where Allah stood them.

Al-Awwal is the First: the One already there when nothing else was. The Prophet ﷺ said it plainly, Allah was, and nothing else was with Him. Al-Akhir is the Last: the One who remains when the whole world wears out and only the Face of your Lord is left. And here is the question hanging between those two ends. If He alone was there at the start, and He alone will be there at the finish, what does that say about the middle you are living in right now? It says only He matters in it. Almost everything you chase from morning to night dissolves at both ends. These names are Allah quietly resetting the scale.

That is why they open Surah Al-Hadid, a surah that asks believers to give up their wealth and even their lives in His path. You can only give something up for Allah, Ustadh Hisham explains, once you truly know the One you are giving it up for. So Allah names Himself first.

And Al-Ahad is sharper than it looks. Wahid can begin a count, one, two, three. Ahad closes the door on any second at all: the One and Only, unique, with nothing of His kind beside Him. You see it lived, not defined, in Bilal, crushed under a boulder in the desert and ordered to renounce his Lord, answering with a single word from his crushed lungs: Ahad. Tawhid is not a definition you memorise. It is a life you live.

So gather your scattered worries into one worry of Him, and the rest grows small. Like the Prophet ﷺ taught his exhausted daughter Fatima, go to Allah before the shortcut, and say Hasbiya Allah, Allah is enough for me. The higher you climb toward Him, the smaller the world below, until the only thing left in view is Him.

Hasbuna Allah, Allah is enough for us.
