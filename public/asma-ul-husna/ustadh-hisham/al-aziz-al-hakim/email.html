The verse the Prophet ﷺ wept over all night

Preview: Al-Azeez and Al-Hakeem turn the page from the gentle names to the undefeatable might and perfect wisdom of Allah.

Assalamu alaikum,

Until now this journey has walked you through the gentle names of Allah: the Merciful, the Loving, the Kind, the Provider. This pair turns the page. Al-Azeez and Al-Hakeem, the Almighty who can never be beaten and the All-Wise who places everything exactly where it belongs.

Ustadh Hisham Abu Yusuf begins with a picture. Think of the door of an x-ray room. Why is it lined with lead? Because lead is the one material the radiation cannot pass through. The waves hit it and stop. Everything that tries to get through gets caught at the wall. That is Al-Azeez: the One who can never be penetrated, never overcome, never defeated, not for a moment and not for eternity. And He is more: He is the source of all honour. So when you chase status through a branded car, a watch, a job title, a famous surname, the Qur'an tells you plainly where real honour lives: to Allah belongs all of it. Your worth is with Him.

Then comes the second name. Al-Hakeem is the All-Wise, the One who puts everything in its right place, who says the right thing at the right time and acts at the right moment, with nothing random and nothing wasted. And here is the heart of the lesson: why are these two names almost always paired in the Qur'an? Because power without wisdom is a catastrophe. The tyrants of history were dominating, but they did not know where to stop, on whom to be gentle, how to use what they held. Allah is Al-Azeez and Al-Hakeem together: perfect might married to perfect judgement, which is why His power is never abused and never misplaced.

There is one night that shows you this name of power is soaked in mercy. The Prophet ﷺ stood in prayer and reached a single verse, the plea of Isa: 'If You punish them, they are Your slaves; but if You forgive them, then You are the Exalted in Might, the Wise.' And he did not move past it. He recited it, and again, and again, the same words, until Fajr, asking Allah out of sheer love for his ummah, for you and me, to forgive.

And if His law ever feels like a cage, hold this image. In Arabic the bridle on a horse is called a hakama, from this very name. It restrains the horse, but it turns it for the horse's own good. The Maker who built you knows what harms you and what helps you, and the Sharia is the manual He sent down with you.

Walk with this Qur'an, the Book that once brought down empires, and you are azeez: a person of real honour the towers can never give.

Ya Azeez, Ya Hakeem, make us take our honour from You alone, and let us trust Your wisdom through every hard turn.
