The Protective Guardian and Helper

Al-Wali and An-Naseer.

Two names of Allah that almost never travel alone. Across the Qur'an, more than a dozen times, they arrive side by side.

Ustadh Hisham asks: why does Allah keep pairing them?
01 / 08
Al-Wali comes from a word the Arabs first used for the saddle, the seat pressed flush against the animal's skin, nothing in between.

So close. And responsible for you. The One who takes your affair upon Himself.
02 / 08
Picture a swimming pool.

The lifeguard is responsible for you. It is his job to watch you, and he never has to be asked. That is Al-Wali.

A stranger who jumps in to save a drowning child? He had no duty. He just rushed in. That is An-Naseer.
03 / 08
That is why the order never changes.

You rely on the one in charge of you (Al-Wali). You cry out for any helper at all (An-Naseer) only when every other door has closed.

Allah is both. And He never takes a break.
04 / 08
He is the Wali of those who believe.

He keeps pulling them out of the darkness into the light, while the world drags everyone else the other way.

The pull you feel toward Him? That is Him taking charge of you.
05 / 08
Read Yusuf.

The well. The slave market. The slander. The prison. And Allah watching through every stage.

A lifeguard saves you at once. A swimming instructor lets you struggle, because that is how you learn to swim.
06 / 08
Sometimes the rescue looks like hardship.

The prison was how Allah saved Yusuf from the palace.

Sometimes Allah guards you by leaving you in the deep end. Only later do you see He was looking after you the whole time.
07 / 08
When you are cornered, call An-Naseer.

Like Nuh: 'I am overpowered, so help.'

There is no corner anyone can place you in where you cannot turn and ask the Helper. Ya Wali, Ya Naseer. 🤍

Caption

Two names of Allah that almost never travel alone.

Ustadh Hisham Abu Yusuf unlocks Al-Wali and An-Naseer, the pair the Qur'an places side by side more than a dozen times. Al-Wali comes from a word first used for the saddle pressed flush against an animal's skin: the One who is nearest to you and has taken your affair upon Himself. Like a lifeguard, He is responsible for you and never has to be asked. An-Naseer is the helper who rushes in like a stranger diving after a drowning child, the One you cry out to when every other door has shut. That is why Al-Wali always comes first: you lean on the One in charge of you, and call for any helper only when there is no one else.

And when life hurts? Read Yusuf, guarded by Allah in the well, the market, the slander, and the prison. Sometimes Allah lets you struggle in the deep end so you learn to swim, and sometimes the rescue itself looks like the thing you would not have chosen.

Who is your true guardian today? 🤍
