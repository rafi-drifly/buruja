Al-Jabbar.

The Compeller. A Name of pure might, the power nothing can stand in front of.

But hidden inside it is one of the gentlest meanings in the whole Qur'an.
01 / 08
In the Qur'an, the word jabbar describes Allah only once (Al-Hashr 59:23).

Every other time, it describes a human tyrant, and it is always a rebuke.

That gap is the whole lesson.
02 / 08
Jabr has two meanings:

1. To compel. A will that simply happens, no choice left to resist it.

2. To mend what is broken. The dentist filling a gap. The bonesetter resetting a fracture so it heals stronger.

Allah is both.
03 / 08
So why is 'jabbar' an insult for a human?

The scholars say the tyrant dominates others to fill a gap inside himself. He is broken, and instead of letting Allah mend him, he crushes everyone else.

The harshness was never strength.
04 / 08
Al-Qahhar, the Prevailing, almost always comes with Al-Wahid, the One.

Total power can only belong to one. If many held it, they would cancel each other out.

Because Allah is One, nothing can stand before Him.
05 / 08
Watching a tyrant 'win'?

Never think Allah failed His promise. He is not unaware. He is not unable.

He is delaying, so that when the reckoning lands, it lands in full. Every oppressor has an expiry date.
06 / 08
'If He can lift my hardship, why hasn't He?'

The wisdom of Allah is timing. Where you see something wrong, He has folded in a benefit you cannot yet see.

Ibrahim waited years. The Prophet ﷺ waited over twenty.
07 / 08
Bring your broken places to the One who mends.

Do not be your own bonesetter, grasping for control.

Hand the fracture to Al-Jabbar, the only One who truly sets it right. 🤍

Caption

Al-Jabbar, the Compeller, is a Name of might, the power nothing can stand in front of. But Ustadh Hisham Abu Yusuf draws out the meaning hidden inside it.

The Arabic root j-b-r carries two faces at once: to compel, a will that simply happens, and to mend what is broken, the way a bonesetter resets a fracture so it heals stronger. Allah is both. And here is the striking part: in the Qur'an, jabbar describes Allah only once (Al-Hashr 59:23). Every other time it describes a human tyrant, as a rebuke, because the scholars say a person who must dominate is covering a gap inside himself, a brokenness he refuses to let Allah mend.

So when a tyrant seems to win, or your own relief is slow, never read it as weakness. Allah is timing it, and every oppressor has an expiry date. Anchor your strength in Him and no jabbar of flesh and blood is something to fear.

What have you been trying to fix by force, that you could hand to Al-Jabbar instead? 🤍
