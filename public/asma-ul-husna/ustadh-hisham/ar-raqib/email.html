The One who has your back

Preview: Ar-Raqib watches you, but not the way you fear. Here is the difference, and why it changes everything.

Assalamu alaikum,

There is a feeling everyone knows: someone has your back. Someone is watching out for you, ready to step in, refusing to let you fall. Now imagine that someone never blinks, never sleeps, and never misses a thing. That is Ar-Raqib, the Ever-Watchful.

Ustadh Hisham Abu Yusuf draws this name out of a single word the Prophet Isa speaks in the Qur'an. A raqib is one who watches. But there are two completely different ways to watch a person. Picture a speed camera by the road: it is not looking for the most careful driver, it is waiting for the one who slips, so it can catch him out. Now picture the exam invigilator, or the security guard at the door. They are watching just as closely, but for the opposite reason, to keep you safe. Allah's name lands squarely on the second. He is not the camera waiting for your mistake. He is the One watching over you to protect you.

Know that, and two things change at once.

First, you stop feeling alone. When the Prophet ﷺ was hidden in the cave with the enemy standing above him, and Abu Bakr whispered that one glance would finish them, he answered: what do you think of two, when Allah is the third of them? You are never two. There is always a third, watching, with His hand over you. The histories even tell us that when Uthman was killed reading the Qur'an, his blood fell on the verse, 'Allah will be sufficient for you against them, and He is the Hearing, the Knowing.' The One watching had missed nothing.

Second, you start living as though you are seen. Be honest about the gap between who you are in public and who you are when the door is shut. The size of that gap is the real measure of how deeply this name has reached you. When you slip and your first instinct is to glance around, relieved that no one saw, that relief is the sign: the eyes you feared were human, and the One actually watching had slipped your mind. Feeling watched by Ar-Raqib is not a threat. It grows haya, the gentle shyness before Allah that keeps a heart decent and alive.

The Prophet ﷺ summed the whole of it up in one word, ihsan: worship Allah as though you see Him, and if you cannot, then know that He sees you.

Ya Raqib, make us shy before You in our solitude, and let us feel that You are the third in the cave who never looks away.
