Al-Qareeb and Al-Mujeeb.

The Near, and the Responsive.

These are the names for the moment you feel most alone: the night you can't explain, the call you're too ashamed to make.

Ustadh Hisham opens them with one strange, beautiful verse.
01 / 08
In the middle of the rules of Ramadan, Allah pauses to talk about something else: how close He is to you.

'And when My servants ask you concerning Me, indeed I am near.'

(Al-Baqarah 2:186)
02 / 08
Around 10 verses in the Qur'an say: 'they ask you, so tell them.'

The question goes from the people, to the Prophet ﷺ, to Allah, and back. There's always a middleman.

Except here.
03 / 08
On how close He is, Allah does not say 'tell them I am near.'

He removes the messenger and answers in His own voice: I am near.

No one stands between you and Him. Your call reaches Him directly.
04 / 08
How near?

'We are closer to him than his jugular vein.' (Qaf 50:16)

The vein you'd die without. He is nearer still, in His knowledge, in His care. He knows the thought no one else can hear, and He stayed close anyway.
05 / 08
But near isn't enough on its own.

We all know people who are close and still reply in 5 working days. Al-Mujeeb is different.

He is the One who answers the desperate the moment they call. (An-Naml 27:62)
06 / 08
So why does du'a feel unanswered?

Because we treat it like Amazon. Add to basket. Next-day delivery. Check the tracking.

Du'a is not an order. It's a heartfelt conversation. The way you ask shapes the way He answers.
07 / 08
However far you've walked away, it's only ever one step back.

Go a thousand steps from Allah, and one sincere step turns you around, because He never left.

Ya Qareeb, Ya Mujeeb. 🤍

Caption

These are the two names for the moment you feel most alone.

Ustadh Hisham Abu Yusuf opens Al-Qareeb (the Near) and Al-Mujeeb (the Responsive) with one verse that breaks the Qur'an's own pattern. Around ten times Allah says 'they ask you, so tell them,' always with the Prophet ﷺ carrying the message. But on the question of how close He is, in Al-Baqarah 2:186, Allah removes the messenger and answers in His own voice: indeed I am near. No one stands between you and Him.

How near? Nearer to you than your own jugular vein (Qaf 50:16), aware even of what your soul whispers. And He doesn't only stay close, He answers the desperate the moment they call. So why does du'a so often feel unanswered? Because we treat it like ordering on Amazon, with next-day delivery and a tracking number. Du'a is a conversation, not a transaction. Call on Allah the way Zakariyya did, raw and broken-hearted, and the response will be nothing like you imagined.

And however far you have wandered, it is only ever one step back to Him.

Which secret will you finally take to Al-Qareeb today? 🤍
