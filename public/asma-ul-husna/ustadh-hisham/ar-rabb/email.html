The name on almost every page of the Qur'an

Preview: After Allah's own name, this one runs through the whole Qur'an, and most of us never think of it as a name at all.

Assalamu alaikum,

After the name Allah itself, there is one name woven through the Qur'an more than any other, and most of us have never once thought of it as a name at all. It opens the second verse of the Book, right after Allah's name, and from the first page to the last you would struggle to find a page without it: Ar-Rabb, the Lord, your Master and your Nurturer.

Ustadh Hisham Abu Yusuf gives the most time to the names Allah Himself repeats the most, and by that measure this one comes early and loud. English hands you 'Lord' or 'Master,' but a translation is only a sad little attempt at a word this deep. Inside its three letters, r-b-b, are two living meanings.

The first is the Nurturer: the One who takes something from nothing to full maturity and carries its every need along the way. Think of how a parent receives a helpless child, or a teacher an empty mind, and pours years of selfless work into it, mostly unseen, mostly unthanked. Now lift that from a tired mother to the One who placed that instinct in her. Your Rabb holds up the ceiling, runs the water cycle so the sea falls as a gentle patter instead of a wall, and darkens the sky when your body is spent, none of it asking your permission.

So where is your Rabb when life hurts? Right there in it. Yusuf was thrown in a well by his brothers, sold, and imprisoned for years, and none of it made sense while he was inside it. The day his life finally came together, he called Allah one thing: Rabbi, my Lord, the One who was looking after me the whole time. Look back on your own ups and downs and you will see the same hand nurturing you through all of it.

The second meaning is the Master, which makes us His servants, the abd. Not slaves in chains, but servants out of love, the way you do things for a parent you cherish with no one forcing you. The Prophet ﷺ prayed until his feet swelled and called himself a grateful servant. And this Master is never once tied in the Qur'an to a wish to harm you. Even when we wrong our own souls, He calls: My servants, do not despair, you are still Mine. The door stays open.

That is why 'Rabbi, my Lord' is almost the only name of Allah you can make personal, and why the prophets reached for it more than any other. If you truly knew your Rabb, this word would rarely leave your lips, and you would never feel completely alone.

Ya Rabb, keep this name on our tongues, and never leave us to ourselves for the blink of an eye.
