Al-Lateef and Al-Khabeer.

The Subtle and the All-Aware.

The two names you reach for on the days that don't make sense: the loss you didn't choose, the door that slammed, the prayer that seemed to go unanswered.
01 / 08
Luqman taught his son these names.

If a deed were as small as a mustard seed, hidden in a rock or lost in the heavens, Allah would bring it out.

Indeed, Allah is Lateef, Khabeer. (31:16)
02 / 08
Al-Lateef means two things at once:

Gentleness, the soft kindness you show stroking a stray cat.

And fineness, something so subtle you can't even detect it. Like ginger hidden in biryani, working before you saw it coming.
03 / 08
Put together: Al-Lateef is the One whose gentleness reaches you in ways you don't even notice.

How often does a calamity turn out to be a blessing in disguise?

Al-Khabeer is the One who knows the hidden good folded inside the loss.
04 / 08
When tragedy strikes good people, the mind jumps to: hardship = punishment.

These names dismantle that.

We hold one pixel. Allah holds the full picture. Stop reading the cover as the whole book.
05 / 08
Look at Yusuf.

The well. The slave market. The prison. Years torn from his family.

At the end he calls all of it the gentleness of Allah: 'my Lord is Lateef in whatever He wills.'

The dark places were the road to the reunion.
06 / 08
It may be you hate a thing, and it is good for you. (2:216)

The locked door that soaked his rival's suit and ruined his notes?

He presented from memory, dripping wet, and was hired on the spot.

The gift was wrapped in the difficulty.
07 / 08
The river the mother feared became a red carpet to the palace.

The tyrant's house became a nursery.

Only Al-Lateef turns a killing river into a road home.

Ya Lateef, Ya Khabeer. 🤍

Caption

Al-Lateef and Al-Khabeer are the names for the days that don't add up.

Ustadh Hisham Abu Yusuf unlocks them through the lesson Luqman gave his son: a deed as small as a mustard seed, hidden in solid rock, and Allah brings it out, because He is Subtle and All-Aware. Al-Lateef means gentleness AND fineness, the One whose kindness reaches you in ways too subtle to detect, often through what first looks like a difficulty. Al-Khabeer is the One who knows the hidden good folded inside the loss.

No life shows it like Yusuf, who at the end of every dark chapter, the well, the prison, the betrayal, calls all of it the gentleness of Allah. We hold one pixel; He holds the full picture. The locked door wins the job. The river the mother feared becomes a red carpet to the palace.

It may be you hate a thing and it is good for you. What hardship are you holding by its cover, when there might be a diamond in the rock?

Ya Lateef, Ya Khabeer. 🤍
