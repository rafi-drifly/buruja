The one word the whole world is hunting

Preview: Al-Qawiyy and Al-Mateen are the source of every strength there is. Here is what believing that does to the way you stand.

Assalamu alaikum,

There is one word the whole world is chasing. Every nation wants it over its rival, every leader over the people, and somewhere in every heart it is being quietly hunted: power. We spend our lives reaching for it and rarely stop to ask where it actually comes from.

Ustadh Hisham Abu Yusuf answers with two of Allah's names: Al-Qawiyy and Al-Mateen, the All-Strong and the Firm. Strength, in Arabic, is simply the ability to do what you intend. Close the gap completely between your will and your power and you have Al-Qawiyy, the One with no task beyond Him. Al-Mateen, which the Qur'an sets right beside it in Surah Adh-Dhariyat, adds the rest of the picture: that strength is firm and enduring, a power that never strains, never tires, and never gives way.

He draws it through a man from the earliest generations who walked, alone, into the palace of the Persian emperor. No army, no wealth, no fine clothes. Everyone else entered with their heads bowed. He kept his high and refused to bow to anyone. Where does that confidence come from? He was standing on one thing: the certainty that Al-Qawiyy, the source and the strongest of all power, was behind him.

And everything beside Him is weak. The Qur'an says every idol and government you could call upon, gathered together, could not create a single fly, and if a fly takes a drop of your blood you cannot get it back. A virus too small to see brought whole nations to their knees. He humbles the proud with the smallest of things.

So why are the believers often weak? Because Allah revives His religion through opposition: pressure wakes the heart, comfort makes us drift. Worldly power can be given to a tyrant and withheld from the righteous, and both are a test. The measure is never how much strength you were handed, but what you did with it.

Look at Musa at the edge of the sea, Pharaoh's army thundering behind him, his people crying that they were finished. He had a stick and a Lord, and he said: my Lord is with me, He will guide me. The power was never in the wood.

So take the revelation with strength. Stop editing your faith to borrow a stranger's approval. Say you went to pray. Carry your name. And anchor every day in the words the Prophet ﷺ called a treasure of Paradise: la hawla wa la quwwata illa billah, there is no power except with Allah.

Ya Qawiyy, Ya Mateen, let us stand from strength, and keep our hearts firm on the words You love.
