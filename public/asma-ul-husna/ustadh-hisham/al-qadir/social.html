Al-Qadeer.

The All-Powerful. The One able to do absolutely anything.

Ustadh Hisham starts with the ship they swore could never sink, and the speck of a virus that shut a whole world down.
01 / 08
The name comes from one root, q-d-r, with three meanings braided together:

ability to do a thing,
measure, to size something exactly,
and granting power, placing ability inside a creation.
02 / 08
Why did Allah build seven heavens and seven earths?

The Qur'an says it plainly: so you may know that He is able to do all things.

The universe is built, in part, as evidence.
03 / 08
Look at your own hands.

Forty people in a room, four hundred fingers, no two prints alike. Eight billion alive, billions before them, every fingerprint unique.

He could have copied us like robots. He varied every one, just to show you He can.
04 / 08
A man asked how Allah could revive a ruined town.

So Allah made him die for a hundred years, then raised him. His food untouched. His donkey's bones clothed in flesh before his eyes.

Then he said: I know that Allah is over all things able.
05 / 08
Musa carried a stammer, a killing, years as a fugitive and a servant.

And Allah told him his whole life back, gently:

you came at the decreed time, and I produced you for Myself.

Qadeer means He timed all of it perfectly.
06 / 08
Allah asked Musa what was in his hand.

'My staff. I lean on it. I need it.'

Allah said: throw it down. You have something greater now.

Whatever you think is your real strength, set it down. Nothing has power except through Him.
07 / 08
On the Day of Judgement the whole universe sits in His grip, the heavens folded in His right hand.

Every power, every fortune, a speck before Him.

Throw away the stick, and let Al-Qadeer split the sea. 🤍
08 / 08
Caption

We live in an age that mistakes its machines for real power, and quietly forgets the One who lent it every ability it has.

Ustadh Hisham Abu Yusuf unlocks Al-Qadeer, the All-Powerful, from one Arabic root that means ability, measure, and granted power all at once. The Qur'an keeps pointing the same way: look at the creation. Seven heavens built so you would know He is able to do all things. A room of fingerprints, no two alike. A man revived after a hundred years. And because Qadeer also means measure, it means He timed your whole life with precision, the way He told Musa, after every trial, 'you came at the decreed time, and I produced you for Myself.'

Then the turn that changes everything: throw away the stick. Whatever you believe is your real source of strength, set it down. Nothing has power except through Allah, and He can put strength into a plain staff until it splits a sea.

What impossible thing will you finally ask Al-Qadeer for? 🤍
