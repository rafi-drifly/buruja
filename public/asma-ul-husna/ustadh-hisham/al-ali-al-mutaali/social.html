Al-Aliyy and Al-Mutaali.

The Most High and the Supremely Exalted.

You meet them at the summit of the greatest verse in the Qur'an, Ayat al-Kursi. And yet these are the names that bend you to the floor.
01 / 08
In Arabic, the root means height. But the Qur'an uses it for power and status too.

Pharaoh made himself 'high' in the land, and crushed people beneath him.

The home of the Hereafter is promised to those who do not want to be high and mighty.
02 / 08
High in status is not the same as high in power.

A king can have a great title and no real command. A gangster can have terrifying power and no honour at all.

Allah is supreme in both. The highest in every way at once.
03 / 08
Why does Allah keep tying this name to prostration?

Ustadh Hisham gives the image of a teacher. He jokes and hands out sweets, until a pen flies from the back.

Then he shows the class its place. Not from cruelty. So learning can begin.
04 / 08
The Qur'an says everyone prostrates to Allah, willingly or unwillingly, even their shadows.

But some people never bow. So how?

Your heart beats, gravity holds you, your breath cycles, all by His will. You are submitting every second. The only question is whether you choose it.
05 / 08
When you go lowest, forehead on the floor, your tongue says: my Lord is the Most High.

The highest of His names meets the lowest point of your body.

That meeting is the heart of every sajdah.
06 / 08
Stuck in a dark place? A debt, a broken marriage, a way you cannot see out of?

Yunus called from the belly of the whale:

There is no god but You, exalted are You, indeed I was among the wrongdoers.

Know your place, know His, and He brings you out.
07 / 08
Here is the turn.

A king makes you bow to keep you distant.

Allah asks you to bow to bring you close, for the nearest you ever are to Him is in sajdah.

The road to His love runs through the floor. 🤍

Caption

We meet these two names at the summit of the greatest verse in the Qur'an, and it would be easy to leave them there, admired and far away.

Ustadh Hisham Abu Yusuf shows that Al-Aliyy, the Most High, and Al-Mutaali, the Supremely Exalted, are the names that bend you to the floor. Their root means height, and the Qur'an uses it for status and power both: Allah is not a king with a title and no command, nor a force without honour, but the highest in every way at once. And everywhere He raises this name, He is reaching for one response from you: that you lower yourself before Him.

The Qur'an says everyone prostrates to Allah, willingly or unwillingly, even their shadows, because every creature is utterly subject to Him. The only difference is whether you choose it. And the secret inside the bowing is the most beautiful part. A king makes you bow to keep you distant. Allah bows you to bring you close, for the nearest you ever are to Him is in sajdah.

When will you slow down in your next prostration? 🤍
