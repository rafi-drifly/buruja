Al-Muhaymin.

The Guardian. The Overseer.

We are one person when we think we are watched, and another when we think we are alone. We save our truest, most unguarded self for the moments we are sure no one can see.

This name reaches into exactly those moments.
01 / 09
It lives in the verse of names, the close of Surah Al-Hashr:

'...the Sovereign, the Pure, the Perfection, the Grantor of Security, the Overseer...' (59:23)

Notice the order: He tells you that you are safe with Him before He tells you that you are seen by Him.
02 / 09
What does the word mean?

Ibn Kathir, on 59:23: Al-Muhaymin is the Witness over the deeds of His creation, ar-Raqib, the One ever watching over them.

Al-Jalalayn and al-Muyassar say the same: the Witness, the Watcher, over all His servants do.
03 / 09
Not the witness of your public deeds only.

The Witness of your deeds. All of them.

The kind word no one heard. The unkind thought you never said. The worship in the dark, and the small dishonesty you were sure had no audience.

To Al-Muhaymin, they are all simply seen.
04 / 09
Ibn Kathir explains the name with three verses:

'He who stands over every soul, [knowing] what it has earned...' (13:33)

'Allah is Witness over what they do.' (10:46)

'And Allah, over all things, is Witness.' (85:9)
05 / 09
And nothing is ever lost on Him.

'...He will inform them of what they did. Allah had enumerated it, while they forgot it...' (58:6)

We forget our own deeds within a week. He counted them, kept them, held them whole. 🤍
06 / 09
The same word describes the Qur'an in 5:48: muhaymin over the scriptures before it.

Ibn Kathir: a trustee, a witness, a guardian over them.

So His watching is not a cold camera. It is the gaze of a Guardian who keeps what He watches.
07 / 09
Al-Sa'di, on Allah as ar-Raqib (4:1):

Knowing He watches your every state, secret and open, should grow an intense modesty before Him, lived out as taqwa.

The awareness that you are seen is not meant to crush you. It is meant to lift you.
08 / 09
You have never once been alone. You never will be.

Sometimes that lands as awe. Sometimes as the deepest comfort there is: your unseen struggle was always seen, your overlooked good was always witnessed.

Ya Muhaymin. He is watching over you right now. 🤍

Caption

We are one person when we think we are watched, and another when we think we are alone.

Al-Muhaymin, the Guardian and Overseer, is the name that reaches into exactly those private moments. Grounded in classical tafsir (Ibn Kathir, al-Sa'di, al-Muyassar, al-Jalalayn), the name appears in the verse of names that closes Surah Al-Hashr (59:23). Ibn Kathir explains it as the Witness over every deed His creation does, ar-Raqib, the One ever watching over them, and he lights it up with verse after verse: He stands over every soul knowing what it earned (13:33), and He is, over all things, the Witness (85:9).

And nothing is ever lost on Him. On the Day He raises everyone, He informs them of what they did, because 'Allah had enumerated it, while they forgot it' (58:6). The good you did in secret was kept whole. The wrong done to you in the dark was counted.

The same word describes the Qur'an in 5:48 as muhaymin, a guardian over the scriptures before it, which Ibn Kathir explains as trustee, witness, and arbiter. So His watching is never a cold stare. It is the gaze of a Guardian who keeps what He sees. And because the name Al-Mu'min, the Grantor of Security, comes just before Al-Muhaymin in the verse, we meet that watching already knowing we will not be wronged.

So live one ordinary day in the felt knowledge that He is watching over it.

Who are you when you are sure no one is looking? He is looking, and He is keeping it. 🤍
