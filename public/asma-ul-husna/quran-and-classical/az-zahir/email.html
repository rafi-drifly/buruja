The One you were looking at all along

Preview: Az-Zahir, the Manifest. The most exalted reality in existence, and the most easily overlooked one in your day.

Assalamu alaikum,

Some of the names of Allah meet a fear in you. This one meets a forgetting. We move through a world so full of Allah's signs that we have stopped seeing them, the way you stop hearing a sound that never stops: the sky, the faces, the food, the breath going in and out without your permission, all of it pointing somewhere, and most days we look straight through it.

One of the names of Allah turns your eyes back on: Az-Zahir, the Manifest.

It sits in a single verse alongside three other names. He is the First and the Last, the Manifest and the Intimate, and He is, of all things, Knowing (Al-Hadid 57:3). Al-Awwal and Al-Akhir bracket all of time. Az-Zahir and Al-Batin bracket all of height and nearness: the One above whom there is nothing, and the One nearer than whom there is nothing.

What does it mean to call Allah the Manifest? The Prophet ﷺ explained it himself, in a supplication recorded by Muslim: You are Az-Zahir, so there is nothing above You. The Manifest is the Highest, the One over everything. Sit with what that does to the size of your troubles. Every power you are afraid of has something above it, and that something is Allah, who has nothing above Him. The highest thing in existence is not any of the things that frighten you. It is the One you can turn to directly.

There is a second sense, and it reaches your daily life most. Az-Zahir is also the most obvious reality there is, because everything your eyes land on is a sign of Him. The Qur'an presses this in Surah Luqman: do you not see that Allah poured His favours over you, both apparent and hidden? (31:20). Commenting on it, al-Sa'di says Allah overwhelmed you and covered you in blessings, the ones you can see and the ones you cannot. The signs are not rare. They are draped over you, head to foot. The trouble was never that the evidence was thin. It is that a thing that is everywhere becomes invisible.

The Prophet ﷺ called the verse this name sits in better than a thousand verses. And Ibn Kathir relates that when a man came to Ibn Abbas troubled by whispers of doubt, the cure he was handed was not an argument, but this: recite, He is the First and the Last, the Manifest and the Intimate. Doubt makes God feel distant. These names leave no gap for it: He is before, after, above, and within everything, and He knows it all.

And here is the sweetest part. The One above whom there is nothing is paired with Al-Batin, the One nearer than whom there is nothing, and the very next verse adds, and He is with you wherever you are (57:4). You do not climb to Him. You wake up to Him, already here, already nearer than these words, already above everything that ever made you afraid.

Ya Zahir, open our eyes to the signs we sleep through, and keep us awake to a world full of You.
