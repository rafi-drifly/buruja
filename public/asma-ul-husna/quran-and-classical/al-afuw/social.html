Al-Afuw.

The Pardoner.

Most of us know what it is to be forgiven and still carry the thing. The other person let it go, and yet it stays there, a small permanent mark on the record.

This name answers that.

Al-Ghaffar covers the sin. Its root means to veil, to conceal.

Al-Afuw goes further.

The root of afw means to efface, to wipe away until no trace is left. He does not only hide the record. He erases it.

On Surah Al-Hajj 22:60, al-Sa'di says of this name:

Allah pardons the sinners, forgives their sins, removes them, and removes their very traces from them.

Not a stain covered over. A stain washed out completely.

And He does it while fully able to punish.

'...indeed, Allah is ever Pardoning and Competent [fully Able].' (4:149)

Ibn Kathir: among His attributes is that He pardons although He is fully able to punish. The power is what makes it mercy.

Look where this name sits in 4:43.

A ruling about prayer ends: when there is no water, purify with clean earth.

Ibn Kathir: part of His pardoning is that He made this easy for you. His afw even eases the small hardships.

Of all the prayers for the greatest night of the year, the Prophet ﷺ taught Aisha one, built entirely on this name:

Allahumma innaka afuwwun tuhibbu al-afwa faʿfu anni.

O Allah, You are Pardoning, You love to pardon, so pardon me. (at-Tirmidhi)

Erasing your sins is something He delights in, not something you have to wear Him down to get. 🤍

Caption

Most of us know what it is to be forgiven and still carry the thing. Forgiveness covered it. It did not erase it.

Al-Afuw, the Pardoner, answers that. Grounded in classical tafsir (Ibn Kathir, al-Sa'di, al-Qurtubi), this name means more than forgiveness. The root of afw is effacement: to wipe away until no trace is left. So where Al-Ghaffar covers the sin, Al-Afuw removes it. On 22:60, al-Sa'di says Allah pardons sinners, forgives their sins, removes them, and removes their very traces from them, and calls this His settled, essential way with His servants.

And He does it while fully able to punish. In 4:149 the name is paired with qadir, 'fully Able.' Ibn Kathir notes Allah pardons although He is perfectly able to punish, which is exactly what makes it mercy and not mere resignation.

This is the name the Prophet ﷺ pointed his ummah toward on the greatest night of the year. He taught Aisha to say on Laylat al-Qadr: Allahumma innaka afuwwun tuhibbu al-afwa faʿfu anni, 'O Allah, You are Pardoning, You love to pardon, so pardon me' (reported by at-Tirmidhi). Of all requests, He built it on this one name, and on the truth that He loves to erase our sins.

And because He erases, He commands you to. Al-Sa'di: pardon others, so that Allah deals with you as you deal with His servants.

What is the thing you are most ashamed of? To Al-Afuw, the sincere returner does not carry a covered-over stain. The page is wiped. 🤍
