Death is not nothing.

Allah says He created death (67:2), and you do not create a void.

Ibn Kathir: scholars drew from this that death is a created, existing thing. Not the universe deleting you. A thing He made, on purpose, and placed in your story.

And He says why He made it.

'He created death and life to test which of you is best in deed' (67:2).

A life with no end could be endlessly postponed. Because there is a last day, today has weight. Then He seals the verse: and He is the Forgiving.

'Every soul will taste death. Then to Us you will be returned.' (29:57)

Not destroyed by death. Tasted, and passed on from the table. Every human walks this door, prophets included.

A man holding a cup from the sea, claiming he owns the ocean. Ibrahim raised the sun. The king was struck dumb.

Caption

There is one fact none of us escapes and almost none of us can bear to look at for long: one day this heartbeat stops.

Al-Mumit, the Giver of Death, is the Qur'an's answer, and it is far gentler than the name sounds. An honest note first: the definite name al-Mumit reaches us through the tradition of the ninety-nine names. What the Qur'an gives directly is the act, again and again: nuhyi wa numit, 'We give life and We cause death' (Qaf 50:43, verified by morphology as a verb from the root of 'death'), and amata wa ahya, 'He caused death and gave life' (53:44).

And notice what the Qur'an does with death. It calls it a created thing: 'He created death and life to test which of you is best in deed' (67:2). Ibn Kathir notes that a created thing is not a void, death is something Allah made, on purpose. al-Sa'di, commenting on 7:158, calls death a bridge and a crossing to the abode of permanence, not a cliff where the road ends. And Allah seals the verse about death by naming Himself al-Ghafur, the Forgiving.

Feel it through the grief the Qur'an comforts: 'Every soul will taste death, then to Us you will be returned' (29:57). Ibn Kathir preserves the voice heard in the Prophet's household at his passing, peace and blessings be upon him: in Allah there is consolation from every calamity and a replacement for everything lost. Reading 3:156, the scholars explain no one dies except by Allah's decree, so the believer lays down the painful 'if' and knows a loved one was received, exactly on time, not lost to chance.

What would change if you stopped fearing death as nothing, and met it as a return to the One who forgives? 🤍
