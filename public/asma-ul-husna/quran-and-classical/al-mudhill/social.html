Al-Mudhill.

The Giver of Disgrace.

One of the hardest names to hold, and the easiest to misread. It is not a God who delights in crushing people.

It is the truth that all honour is His, and so is its withdrawal. 🤍
01 / 09
An honest note first.

The definite name Al-Mudhill does not appear in the Qur'an as a title. What appears is the verb, which Allah teaches the Prophet ﷺ to say to Him:

'wa tudhillu man tasha', 'and You abase whom You will' (3:26).

The noun-name comes from the scholars who gathered the 99. We say that plainly.
02 / 09
And it never stands alone.

The same verse gives you both at once:

'You honour whom You will and You humble whom You will.' (3:26)

Al-Muizz and Al-Mudhill, the Giver of Honour and the Giver of Disgrace, from one hand. In the middle it says: in Your hand is all good.
03 / 09
Who does this name actually abase?

Not the weak. Not the overlooked. Read the verses and a pattern jumps out: the one brought low is the one who lifted himself up.

The arrogant. The mocker. The one too proud to bow to his Maker.
04 / 09
'Call upon Me; I will respond to you.

Indeed, those who disdain My worship will enter Hell contemptible.' (40:60)

Al-Sa'di: lowly and small, the punishment and the humiliation gathered upon them, as the recompense for their arrogance.

The bowing they refused is forced on them.
05 / 09
Meet the man who got it backwards.

Abdullah ibn Ubayy, chief of the hypocrites, sneered that 'the mighty will drive out the lowly,' meaning himself.

Allah turned his words inside out in the same verse: 'to Allah belongs all honour, and to His Messenger, and to the believers.' (63:8)
06 / 09
He felt honoured. He carried himself as honoured. And he was, in the only ledger that counts, among the abased, and did not even know it.

Al-Sa'di: 'the hypocrites do not know, deceived by the falsehood they stand on.'

His own son blocked him at the gate: he is the Honoured, and you are the abased.
07 / 09
So where do you keep your honour?

Not in the bigger title or the last word. Izzah belongs to Allah, His Messenger ﷺ, and the believers.

Take your honour from the only One who grants it, and you can afford to be the smaller one in the room.
08 / 09
The hand that lowers is the hand that lifts, and it is open.

No one who turns back, however far they fell, is beyond His raising.

Bow before you are bowed. Be honoured by the only One whose honour lasts. 🤍

Caption

Al-Mudhill, the Giver of Disgrace, is one of the hardest names of Allah to hold, and one of the easiest to misread.

First, honestly: the definite name Al-Mudhill does not appear in the Qur'an as a title. What appears is the verb, which Allah teaches the Prophet ﷺ to say to Him: 'wa tudhillu man tasha,' 'and You abase whom You will' (Al Imran 3:26). The noun-name comes to us from the scholars who gathered the 99 names from verses like this. We say that plainly rather than overclaim.

And it is not cruelty. Grounded in classical tafsir (Ibn Kathir, al-Sa'di, al-Muyassar), this name aims its abasement at arrogance and at turning away from God, never at the weak or the overlooked. Those too proud to worship enter Hell 'contemptible' (40:60). And it never stands alone: the same verse gives you Al-Muizz too, 'You honour whom You will and You humble whom You will,' and in the middle, 'in Your hand is all good.' The hand that lowers is the hand that lifts.

Feel it through Abdullah ibn Ubayy, the hypocrite who sneered that 'the mighty will expel the lowly,' meaning himself, and was answered in the same verse: 'to Allah belongs all honour, and to His Messenger, and to the believers' (63:8). He felt mighty and was, in truth, among the abased, and did not even know it.

So stop racing for a status that was never in your hands. Take your honour from the only One who grants it. Bow before you are bowed.

Where have you been chasing an honour that was never yours to keep? 🤍
