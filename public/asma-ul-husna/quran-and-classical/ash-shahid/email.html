The parts of your life you think no one sees

Preview: Ash-Shahid, the Witness. The One present to everything, who saw the good no one credited and the wrong no one believed.

Assalamu alaikum,

Somewhere inside most of us is a quiet suspicion that the truest parts of our lives go unseen. The kindness no one noticed. The wrong done to us in a room with no other witness, where it became our word against theirs and nothing could be proven. The good we did when there was no one to tell. And on the other side, the things we got away with, that no eye caught.

One of the names of Allah answers that suspicion at the root: Ash-Shahid, the Witness.

The name comes from a root (sh-h-d) that holds two meanings the Qur'an keeps tied together: to be present, like an eyewitness who actually saw and heard, and to testify, to stand up and declare what was witnessed. So Allah as Ash-Shahid is both present to everything and the One who will testify to everything. Commenting on the verse 'Indeed Allah is, over all things, Witness' (Al-Hajj 22:17), Ibn Kathir explains Him as a Witness over His creation's deeds, a Preserver of their words, and Knowing of their secrets and of what their hearts conceal. Between the deeds, the words, and the hidden intention, there is no part of a human life that escapes Him.

This is a name with two edges. To the one who has been wronged, it is pure consolation. Surah Al-Buruj tells of believers thrown into a trench of fire for nothing but saying 'our Lord is Allah,' while their killers sat at the edge and watched them burn, and right after that horror the Qur'an says, 'and Allah, over all things, is Witness' (85:9). The tyrants thought there were no witnesses but themselves. There was a Witness above them all. And when the people of Madinah told the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, 'we do not know you are a messenger,' Allah Himself stood as his Witness: 'But Allah bears witness to that which He has revealed to you... and sufficient is Allah as Witness' (4:166). Al-Sa'di calls this the greatest testimony there is.

There is one verse where this name stands beside Ar-Raqib, the Watcher (5:117). Isa, son of Maryam, says he was 'a witness over them as long as I was among them; but when You took me up, You were the Watcher over them, and You are, over all things, Witness.' He could only witness his people while he was present in the room. Allah is the Watcher who is present always, and Ash-Shahid is that same complete presence turned toward the day of testimony.

And turn the edge the other way, toward yourself. If Allah is a Witness over what they do, He is a Witness over what you do. The private sin you are sure no one will ever know about has, in this very moment, a Witness. The classical teachers called this the heart of ihsan: that you worship Allah as though you see Him, and though you do not see Him, He surely sees you. To live with this name awake in your chest is to never again be alone with a temptation, because you are never alone.

So here is the gift of this name. You are not living unwitnessed, and you never were. The good you thought no one saw was seen. The wrong you thought no one would ever know was known. And the One who saw all of it is the One you are walking back to. Twice the Qur'an seals this name with one word, enough: sufficient is Allah as Witness. You can stop performing for the watchers and start living for the only Witness who never looked away.

Ya Shahid, be our Witness as You were the Witness for those before us, and let what You witness of us be what pleases You.
