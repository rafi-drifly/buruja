'And if you fear privation, Allah will enrich you from His bounty if He wills.' (9:28)

Not 'your fear is foolish.' But: here is who covers the gap.

What is richness, really?

Ibn Kathir cites the Prophet ﷺ: it is not abundance of possessions, but richness of the soul (ghina an-nafs).

Al-Mughni can fill your hands. He can fill your heart instead. The second is the greater gift. 🤍

Caption

There is a fear that grips the chest when the money runs low: the gap between what you have and what you owe, and the worst version of the future.

Al-Mughni, the Enricher, is the answer the Qur'an gives. An honest note first: the bare title al-Mughni is not a word the Qur'an uses for Allah; it is the name as the scholars settled it in the tradition of the 99. What the Qur'an gives directly is the action, said again and again: 'Allah will enrich you from His bounty' (9:28), 'He found you poor and enriched you' (93:8), 'it is He who enriches and gives possessions' (53:48).

Keep al-Ghani and al-Mughni apart and your prayer sharpens. Al-Ghani means Allah needs nothing, full in Himself. Al-Mughni means He gives YOU sufficiency, the One who fills the empty hand. And only the One who needs nothing can enrich a whole creation without ever being diminished.

Grounded in classical tafsir (Ibn Kathir, al-Sa'di, al-Muyassar). Al-Sa'di's line is worth carrying for life: provision is not confined to one door; no door closes except that Allah opens many others. Ibn Kathir, on Surah Ad-Duha, notes that Allah joined in His Prophet ﷺ two stations, the patient poor and the grateful rich, and cites his teaching that the truest richness is richness of the soul, not a larger pile.

So bring Him the gap. Pray for enough in the hand, and pray harder for enough in the heart.

What empty hand have you been afraid to hold up? He named Himself the One who fills it. 🤍
