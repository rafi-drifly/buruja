Caption

Something came too early in your life, and something else is taking far too long. The opportunity that landed years ahead of yours. The prayer you have made since you were young that still has no answer, while the person beside you is waved to the front of every line. Who decides the order?

Al-Muqaddim, the One who brings forward, meets you there. One honest note first: this name does not appear in the Qur'an in its definite form. What the Qur'an gives is the living verb, qaddama, to send ahead. Allah says wa qad qaddamtu ilaykum bil-wa'id, 'I had already put forth to you the warning' (50:28). The name is how the classical tradition gathered that verb, and the Prophet ﷺ himself said in a du'a recorded in Bukhari and Muslim, 'anta al-Muqaddim wa anta al-Muakhkhir,' You are the One who brings forward and the One who puts back.

Notice the Qur'an rarely separates the two. In one sentence: He delays them to an appointed term, and when it comes they can neither lag behind it nor bring it forward (16:61). The tradition always teaches Al-Muqaddim beside Al-Muakhkhir, the Delayer, because the same wise Hand advances and holds back. As al-Sa'di explains on 71:4, Allah's term, when it comes, is not delayed, so no impatience drags the good forward and no dread pushes the hard thing back.

And He left a share of this act in your hands. The one regret of the Hereafter is 'ya laytani qaddamtu li-hayati,' I wish I had sent ahead good for my life (89:24). Allah records what you send forward and your traces, the good that keeps being written after you are gone (36:12). You cannot control His order, but you can step toward Him today (74:37), and that is the one going-forward His timing never blocks.

So when the waiting is the heavy part, bring it back to Him: You are Al-Muqaddim and Al-Muakhkhir. Advance for me what is good, hold back what would harm me, and let me trust Your order more than I wanted the thing to come on my time.

What have you been waiting on, that you are only now starting to leave in His order? 🤍
