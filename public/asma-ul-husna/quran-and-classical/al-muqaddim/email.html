Who decides what comes early and what is held back?

Preview: Al-Muqaddim, the One who brings forward. He advances whom He wills and delays whom He wills, in an order that has never once been early or late.

Assalamu alaikum,

Something in your life came early, before you were ready for it, and something else is taking far too long. The opportunity that landed in someone else's lap years ahead of yours. The prayer you have been making since you were young that still has no answer, while the person beside you seems to be waved straight to the front of every line. And underneath the waiting sits a question we do not always let ourselves ask: who decides the order?

One of the names of Allah speaks directly to that order: Al-Muqaddim, the One who brings forward, the One who advances and puts ahead.

Let us be honest with you from the start, because this name deserves it. Unlike most of the names we write about, Al-Muqaddim does not appear in the Qur'an in its definite form. What the Qur'an gives instead is the living verb, qaddama, to send ahead. In Surah Qaf, on the Day of Judgment, Allah says wa qad qaddamtu ilaykum bil-wa'id, 'I had already presented to you the warning' (50:28). Ibn Kathir explains that Allah had excused Himself to people through the messengers and the Books, so the warning was brought forward while there was still time to turn. The name is how the scholars of the tradition gathered that verb, and it rests above all on the Sunnah: in a supplication recorded in Bukhari and Muslim, the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, said to his Lord, 'anta al-Muqaddim wa anta al-Muakhkhir,' You are the One who brings forward and You are the One who puts back.

Notice that the Qur'an rarely lets the advancing stand alone. In a single sentence in Surah An-Nahl, Allah names both motions with the very two roots the tradition drew these names from: He delays them to an appointed term, and when it comes they can neither lag behind it nor bring it forward (16:61). The tradition has carried them as a pair ever since, Al-Muqaddim beside Al-Muakhkhir, the Delayer, because the same wise Hand that brings forward is the very Hand that holds back. He is not sometimes generous with His timing and sometimes withholding it. He is always wise.

And His order is exact. Every matter has an appointed term that cannot be rushed or pushed back. Commenting on Surah Nuh, al-Sa'di says Allah delays you to a fixed, measured time, and then the sealed door: 'Allah's term, when it comes, is not delayed' (71:4). So much of our anxiety could be put down here. You spend yourself trying to drag the good forward before its time and to shove the hard thing further away, and the One who brings forward and puts back has already set both to the instant His wisdom chose. Not one hour early. Not one hour late.

And here is the gift in this name: Allah has left a share of His own act in your hands. The one aching regret of the Hereafter, in Surah Al-Fajr, is a single sentence: 'ya laytani qaddamtu li-hayati,' oh, how I wish I had sent something ahead for my life (89:24). The word for sending ahead is the very root of Al-Muqaddim. You cannot control His timing, but you can put something ahead of yourself on the road. Every prayer, every quiet charity, every wrong you walked away from is a thing you send forward to wait for you where you are going, and Allah records it, the Qur'an says, along with the traces you leave that keep being written after you are gone (36:12).

So when the waiting is the heavy part, you do not have to force a smile over it. You bring it back to Him: You are Al-Muqaddim and Al-Muakhkhir. Advance for me what is good for me, hold back from me what would harm me, let me send something ahead to meet me, and where You delay, let me trust Your order more than I wanted the thing to come on my time.

Ya Muqaddim, ya Muakhkhir, bring forward for us what holds our good, and make us of those who send something ahead for the life that lasts.
