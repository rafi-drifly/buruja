At-Tawwab
The Acceptor of Repentance
cover
At-Tawwab.

The One who keeps turning back.

There is a distance from God that belongs to the person who believed, then slipped, and now cannot face Him. Who suspects they have used up their turns.

This name was revealed to break that suspicion.
01 / 09
The name comes from the root t-w-b: to turn, to return.

And it sits on an intensive Arabic form. So At-Tawwab does not mean the One who forgave once.

It means the One who keeps turning back to His servants. Again. And again. And again.
02 / 09
Al-Sa'di on this name:

Whoever turns to Him, He turns to them, even if the disobedience were repeated from them many times over.

Allah does not weary of accepting repentance until the servant himself wearies of asking.
03 / 09
Here is the part most people never hear.

The scholars find two turnings in this name.

Al-Sa'di on Adam (2:37): first Allah grants the success to repent, then He accepts it.

He turns the servant home first. Then He turns to him.
04 / 09
Adam did not invent the words of his repentance.

The Qur'an says he received them from his Lord.

Even the words you use to ask are a gift from the One you are asking.
05 / 09
Feel it in one life: the three boycotted after Tabuk.

Ka'b ibn Malik stayed behind, no excuse, just delay. For fifty nights no one spoke to him. The earth, the Qur'an says, closed in on him for all its width.

Until a voice called from a mountain: O Ka'b, rejoice.
06 / 09
Read the exact words Allah uses:

'Then He turned to them so that they could turn.' (9:118)

Al-Sa'di: He permitted and enabled their repentance, so that it would happen, and then He would accept it.

He turned to them so that they would turn.
07 / 09
Even Ibrahim and Ismail, building the Ka'ba, sealed their prayer:

'...and turn to us. Indeed You are At-Tawwab, the Merciful.' (2:128)

You do not become clean and then approach. You approach in order to be made clean. 🤍
08 / 09
So whatever you have been carrying, the prayer you stopped, the thing you keep returning to, the distance you assumed was permanent:

He is At-Tawwab. He was facing you before you turned.

The door you thought was shut was only a back you needed to turn around.

Caption

There is a quiet fear that lives under a lot of our distance from God: that we have used up our turns, that the door is closed.

At-Tawwab, the Acceptor of Repentance, the Ever-Relenting, is the answer the Qur'an gives. Grounded in classical tafsir (Ibn Kathir, al-Sa'di, al-Muyassar), the name comes from the root t-w-b, to turn back, in an intensive form: the One who keeps turning to His servants again and again. Al-Sa'di says it plainly: whoever turns to Him, He turns to them, even if the sin is repeated many times, and Allah never wearies of accepting repentance until the servant wearies of asking.

The most astonishing part is what the scholars call the two turnings. On Adam's repentance (2:37), al-Sa'di explains that Allah first grants the servant the success to repent, and then accepts it. Adam did not invent his words of repentance; he received them from his Lord. Your very longing to come back is already the first half of His mercy.

You see it in the three boycotted after Tabuk: Allah closed the wide earth around them until there was nowhere to flee but to Him, then, in the Qur'an's exact words, 'He turned to them so they could turn' (9:118). And even Ibrahim and Ismail, raising the Ka'ba, sealed their prayer with 'You are At-Tawwab.'

So turn around. He was facing you before you moved. 🤍
