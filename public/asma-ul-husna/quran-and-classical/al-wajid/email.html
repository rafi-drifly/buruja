The God who is never looking for anything

Preview: Al-Wajid, the Self-Sufficient. The One who finds whatever He wills with no search and no shortage, and why that quiets a restless heart.

Assalamu alaikum,

There is a particular tiredness that comes from always looking for something: the missing piece, the thing you do not have yet that you are sure would finally make you whole. We spend most of our days as searchers, hands half open, reaching. And underneath it sits a quiet ache, the suspicion that we will always be a little short.

One of the names of Allah turns our eyes to the One for whom that is simply not true: Al-Wajid, the Self-Sufficient, the One who lacks nothing.

Let us be honest before anything else, because this name asks for it. Al-Wajid does not appear in the Qur'an as a name Allah calls Himself. It comes to us through the traditional list of the ninety-nine names, in the narration of al-Walid ibn Muslim recorded by al-Tirmidhi, and through the usage of the scholars. So we will not hang a verse on it and pretend the verse names Him by it. What we will do is what the scholars did: read the root the name is built from, w-j-d, where the Qur'an does use it.

The root opens two ways. The first is means, capacity, what a person has within reach. Allah uses it when He legislates kindness to a divorced wife: 'lodge them out of your means,' min wujdikum (At-Talaq 65:6). Ibn Kathir reports from Ibn Abbas and Mujahid that wujd here means your capacity, your affluence; al-Sa'di reads it as the husband's means and his hardship. Now lift that from the human who has a little to the One whose means have no edge, and you arrive at Al-Wajid: whatever He turns to, He finds already there. The second sense is to find, and the Qur'an puts it in the mouth of Allah about His servant Ayyub: 'Indeed, We found him patient' (Sad 38:44). Carefully: that verse describes Allah finding Ayyub, it does not name Allah Al-Wajid. But it is the root, in the very act the name points to. And His finding is never the end of a search, because nothing was ever hidden from Him; to say He finds is just to say everything is present to Him the instant He wills it.

Do the scholars actually use the word of Allah? They do. Commenting on An-Nisa 4:131, al-Sa'di pictures all of creation asking Allah their every wish at once, and His dominion not decreasing by a particle, because He is 'jawad, wajid, majid', generous, lacking nothing, and glorious. There is the word, placed on Allah by the scholar's own pen. And held beside Al-Ghani, the name the Qur'an does state ('you are the poor, in need of Allah, and Allah is the Free of need,' Fatir 35:15), it lands with full force: He needs nothing, and He lacks nothing.

So here is the invitation of this name. Everything in creation turns toward Allah in need; He turns toward nothing, because He needs nothing and lacks nothing. Which means He can give your need His full attention, since He has no need of His own pulling at Him. Stop carrying the exhausting belief that you must complete yourself by yourself. Ask big, without the fear that your request is too much, because the One who lacks nothing is not strained by it. And loosen your grip on what you fear to lose, because your security is a God who can lose nothing and to whom nothing is ever absent.

Ya Wajid, we are the poor at Your door and You are the Free of need. Quiet our endless reaching, and let us rest in the One who is already complete.
