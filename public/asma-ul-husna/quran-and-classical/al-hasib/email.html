The name that means both your reckoning and your rescue

Preview: Al-Hasib, the Reckoner and the Sufficient. The same Arabic root that means He counts every deed means He is enough for you.

Assalamu alaikum,

There is one Arabic root, three letters, ha, sin, ba, and it holds two ideas that we usually keep far apart. One is the account: the careful, total reckoning of everything you have done, nothing dropped. The other is enough: the One who is sufficient for you, who covers what you cannot. One of the names of Allah is built on that single root, and it refuses to let you separate the two.

Al-Hasib. The Reckoner who weighs every deed, and the Sufficient who is enough for whoever leans on Him.

Take the reckoning first. The Qur'an plants this name in surprising places. In a small verse about manners, returning a greeting, it suddenly seals: 'Indeed Allah is ever, over all things, an Accountant' (An-Nisa 4:86). Al-Sa'di explains that Allah preserves the servants' deeds, the good and the bad, the small and the large, then recompenses them by His grace, justice, and wisdom. Even your reply to a salam is on the account. And He places this name over the wealth of orphans (4:6), the most defenceless money there is, held in trust by a guardian who could quietly skim it and no human auditor would ever know. There the verse closes: 'and sufficient is Allah as Accountant.' Ibn Kathir says: enough that Allah is the reckoner, the witness, and the watcher, whether the guardian returns the wealth complete or diminished, shortchanged, its accounting tampered with. He knows all of it.

Then the name turns toward you. On the Day of Resurrection, the Qur'an says, every person is handed a record they meet spread open, and is told: 'Read your record. Sufficient is yourself against you this Day as accountant' (Al-Isra 17:14). Al-Sa'di calls this the very height of justice: you read the ledger you yourself lived, and there is no argument left. Ibn Kathir quotes the beautiful line of al-Hasan al-Basri: by Allah, He has been just to you, the One who made you the reckoner of your own self. The lesson the scholars drew is to read your record early, to take your own account now, in the quiet, while there is still time to change what is written.

Now cross to the other half of the same root, and feel how close it is. From hasib, the reckoner, to hasb, the enough, is one short step. After the battle of Uhud, the believers were warned that a great force had regathered to destroy them. It did not shrink their faith, it grew it, and they said the words that have been a believer's refuge ever since: 'hasbuna Allah wa ni'mal-wakil,' sufficient for us is Allah, and He is the best Disposer of affairs (Al Imran 3:173). Ibn Kathir reports that these were also the words of Ibrahim, peace be upon him, when he was thrown into the fire, and of the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, when he was warned of the armies. And there is a promise attached: 'whoever relies upon Allah, then He is sufficient for him,' and Allah provides for him 'from where he does not expect,' min haythu la yahtasib, from outside his own reckoning (At-Talaq 65:3).

This is the same name as the reckoning, turned to its tender side. The God who keeps so exact an account that not one deed is lost is the God who is so completely enough that one sincere person, standing in a fire or before an army, can hand Him the whole impossible situation and be covered. The precision and the mercy come from the same place. The exactness means you will never be wronged by an atom. The sufficiency means you do not have to carry the outcome alone.

So live as someone reckoned, and as someone who has Enough. Clean up what no one else can see, because it is already on a perfect account. Take your own account before it is taken for you. And with the thing you are quietly afraid will not add up, do your real effort, keep your fear of Allah, and then lean the result on the One the Qur'an calls your hasb, and let Him be enough, including from the direction you are not watching.

Hasbuna Allahu wa ni'mal-wakil. O Allah, be our reckoning and our rescue, and provide for us from where we do not expect.
