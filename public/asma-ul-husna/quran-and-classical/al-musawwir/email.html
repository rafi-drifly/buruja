The God who shaped your face in the dark

Preview: Al-Musawwir, the Fashioner of Forms. The One who shaped you in the womb however He willed, perfected your form, and called it good.

Assalamu alaikum,

Before anyone had ever seen you, your face was already being made. In a place no light reaches, where no hand could shape anything and no eye could supervise the work, the curve of your features and the colour of your eyes were being decided and drawn. You did not choose any of it. And yet you came out whole, fitted together so precisely that you have spent a lifetime inside this form without ever once thinking it strange.

One of the names of Allah is about exactly this: Al-Musawwir, the Fashioner of Forms.

It is the third of three names the Qur'an places together: 'He is Allah, the Creator, the Producer, the Fashioner' (Al-Hashr 59:24). Ibn Kathir takes the three apart and shows the order inside them. Al-Khaliq is the One who measures and decrees, settling what a thing will be. Al-Bari is the One who brings it into being and existence. And Al-Musawwir is the One who, having decreed it and brought it into being, gives it its actual form, in his words, upon the description He wills and the form He chooses. One making, watched from its first thought to its finished shape.

Where does the fashioning happen? The Qur'an takes you to the one place you can never see: the womb. 'It is He who forms you in the wombs however He wills' (Al Imran 3:6). Ibn Kathir reads it as Allah shaping each person, male or female, beautiful or plain, in a darkness hidden from every eye but His. And it is not bare assembly. Twice the Qur'an adds that He 'formed you and perfected your forms' (40:64, 64:3). Al-Sa'di could not get over it. Look at yourself, he says, limb by limb, and try to find a single organ that would be better placed somewhere else, that would do its work in any other position. You cannot. Every part sits exactly where it serves.

And there is mercy folded into the shape itself. 'In whatever form He willed has He assembled you' (Al-Infitar 82:8). Ibn Kathir relates that Allah had the power to assemble the human drop in some lowly or repellent form, and by His gentleness and forbearance gave it instead a form that is upright, balanced, complete, and beautiful to look at. Your ordinary human shape is not a default. It is a mercy He chose over every other shape He could have given you.

Here is where this name presses on us. So many of us carry a quiet contempt for the form we were given, the face, the height, the features we would change. But the Fashioner who made you described His work as good, and He formed every person differently, on purpose. To stand in front of the mirror and sneer is, in a small way, to argue with Him. The right response to being fashioned so carefully is not vanity and not shame. It is gratitude, and an end to measuring yourself against everyone else's shape.

Billions of faces, and not one ever truly repeated. Each one formed in a sealed darkness, shaped however He willed, by a Maker who never tires and never copies Himself. You were not churned out by a blind process. You were fashioned, on purpose, by Al-Musawwir.

O Allah, You made our form good; we ask You to make our character good to match it.
