The prayer that is taking too long

Preview: Al-Muakhkhir, the Delayer. The respite to the wrongdoer, the answer that comes late, and the difference between giving time and looking away.

Assalamu alaikum,

Almost everyone is waiting on something from Allah that has not come yet. The healing that is taking too long. The marriage, the child, the door that will not open no matter how hard you knock. The prayer you have made so many times that you have started to wonder if it was heard at all. And on the other side, there is the thing you keep being spared, and the people who hurt you who seem to walk away untouched, year after year.

Both of those are one name of Allah: Al-Muakhkhir, the Delayer, the One who puts back.

An honest note first. The name-form al-Muakhkhir is not a single word sitting in the Qur'an; it comes to us through the tradition and the scholars' lists of Allah's names. What the Qur'an gives, again and again, is the verb beneath it, yu'akhkhir, He delays, He defers. So we do not read the name off one verse. We watch what Allah says He delays, and we let His own action teach us the name.

And the first thing the Qur'an does with this delay is correct a misreading. 'And never think that Allah is unaware of what the wrongdoers do. He only delays them for a Day when eyes will stare in horror' (Ibrahim 14:42). Because that is exactly what delay looks like from the outside: as if God did not notice, or did not care. The verse closes both doors. He noticed. He cares. He is simply delaying, on purpose.

The whole meaning of this name lives in two Arabic words. Commenting on Allah delaying the wrongdoers, the scholar al-Sa'di says: He delays them, imhal, but He does not neglect them, ihmal. They are one letter apart and they are opposites. Imhal is to give time. Ihmal is to forget about. Al-Muakhkhir does the first and never the second. And Ibn Kathir adds the reason the delay is mercy at all: 'if Allah were to punish people for what they have done, there would be no living creature left on the face of the earth.' The delay you resent when the guilty go free is the same delay keeping the world, and you, alive long enough to turn back.

But do not mistake the respite for a reward. Al-Sa'di is blunt: Allah grants the wrongdoer respite, and lets him run on, so that he increases in sin, until, when He seizes him, He does not let him escape. The comfort was never approval. Sometimes it is rope. And the same patience that gives a tyrant rope is the patience that gave you the years you spent far from Allah before you came back.

He even delays your own death. To Nuh's people the offer was: 'He will forgive you of your sins and delay you for a specified term' (Nuh 71:4). Every morning you wake to is a deferral, a putting-back of the end. But the same verse names its limit: 'the time set by Allah, when it comes, will not be delayed.' The days are real, on loan, with a due date you do not know.

So bring this name to the prayer that has not been answered. He delayed Zakariyya into old age before giving him a child, and Yaqub wept for years over Yusuf, and not one of those delays was a no. They were the long shape of a yes. A delayed prayer is not lost on the way up. It is being held to its hour by the One whose delay is never neglect.

O Allah, Al-Muakhkhir, when our prayers are slow, let us trust that You are holding them to their hour and not letting them fall, and let us spend every day You defer to us as people who know it is borrowed.
