The only court left when no court will hear you

Preview: Al-Hakam, the Judge. A verdict that is final, perfectly just, and that no power can revise.

Assalamu alaikum,

There is a particular ache that comes from being judged wrongly. Someone decides who you are without hearing you out. A verdict lands on your name that you know is not true, and there is no court to appeal to, no one with the authority to look at the whole thing and set it right. Some of the deepest injustices in a life are exactly this: a wrong ruling that nobody ever overturns.

One of the names of Allah speaks straight into that ache: Al-Hakam, the Judge, the Arbiter.

First, an honest word, because it matters. The definite name Al-Hakam does not appear in the Qur'an as a standing title the way As-Sami or Al-Aziz do. What the Qur'an gives is the word Allah puts in the mouth of His Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, when others wanted some lesser authority to rule between them: 'afaghayra Allahi abtaghi hakaman,' 'then is it other than Allah I should seek as judge?' (Al-An'am 6:114). It also gives us 'His is the judgement' (28:88) and 'the most just of judges' (95:8). The noun-name Al-Hakam comes to us from the scholars who gathered the beautiful names from verses like these. We say that plainly rather than overclaim.

And what a name it is. Commenting on that verse, al-Sa'di writes a line worth memorizing: everything other than Allah is judged-over, not a judge, and every created ruling is shot through with deficiency, fault, or injustice, so that Allah alone is the One who must be taken as Judge. Sit with that contrast. Every human verdict, even the best of them, carries something broken: a missing fact, a hidden bias, a limit on what the judge could see. Al-Hakam carries none of that. And Ibn Kathir, explaining 'His is the judgement,' adds the phrase that makes the name unshakeable: there is none who can revise His ruling. Every earthly court can be appealed, reversed, or corrupted. His judgement, when it comes, stands forever.

If you want to feel it, watch how the prophets reached for this name at the exact moment everything else had failed. Shu'ayb, mocked by most of his town, did not call for a fight. He handed the whole matter up: 'be patient until Allah judges between us, and He is the best of judges' (Al-A'raf 7:87). Yusuf, wrongly enslaved and then wrongly imprisoned, stood in a cell and declared to his fellow prisoners that 'the judgement is only for Allah' (Yusuf 12:40), announcing from inside the injustice that there is a real right and wrong, not set by whoever holds the keys. And Nuh, in grief over a son who drowned, pleaded with Allah as 'the most just of judges,' and the moment the verdict came back, not the one his heart wanted, he did not argue. He sought refuge in Allah and submitted (Hud 11:45-47). That is the posture this name teaches: pour everything out to the Judge, then rest in the verdict.

So here is the invitation of this name. Stop trying to win every argument and clear your name in every room, because the only Judge who counts already knows the whole truth of you. Bring your wounds and your confusions to His court, and trust the ruling even when it is not the one you asked for. And carry His fairness into the small courts you preside over, as a parent, a manager, a friend, because He is judging the judges. Whatever this world decided about you, it did not have the last word. He does, and His word is just.

O Allah, Al-Hakam, judge between us and all who have wronged us in truth, and free our hearts from the fear of the world's verdict.
