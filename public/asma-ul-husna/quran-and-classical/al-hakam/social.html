There is a particular ache in being judged wrongly: a verdict lands on your name, and there is no court to appeal to.

Al-Sa'di on this verse, a line worth memorizing:

Everything other than Allah is judged-over, not a judge.

Every human verdict carries something broken: a missing fact, a hidden bias, a limit on what the judge could see. Al-Hakam carries none of that.

'Is not Allah the most just of judges?' (95:8)

Ibn Kathir: the One who wrongs no one, and who will establish the Day of Judgement so the wronged are given their due.

The Prophet ﷺ taught that when you recite this verse, answer: 'yes, and I am among those who bear witness to that.'

Three words at the close of Surah Al-Qasas:

lahu al-hukm. His is the judgement. (28:88)

Ibn Kathir adds the phrase that makes it unshakeable: there is none who can revise His ruling. Every earthly court can be appealed, reversed, or corrupted. His cannot.

Caption

Al-Hakam, the Judge, the Arbiter, is the answer the Qur'an gives to the ache of being judged wrongly.

First, honestly: the definite name Al-Hakam does not appear in the Qur'an as a standing title. What appears is the word Allah puts in the Prophet's ﷺ mouth: 'afaghayra Allahi abtaghi hakaman,' 'then is it other than Allah I should seek as judge?' (Al-An'am 6:114), along with 'His is the judgement' (28:88) and 'the most just of judges' (95:8). The noun-name comes from the scholars who gathered the 99 names from verses like these. We say that plainly rather than overclaim.

Grounded in classical tafsir (Ibn Kathir, al-Sa'di, al-Muyassar, al-Qurtubi), this name means the One whose verdict is final and perfectly just. Al-Sa'di puts it unforgettably: everything other than Allah is judged-over, not a judge, and every created ruling carries some deficiency or injustice. Al-Hakam carries none. Ibn Kathir adds that His judgement 'has none who can revise it,' which means no oppressor finally walks away clean.

Feel it through the prophets, who reached for this name when every other court had failed. Shu'ayb, mocked by his town, said 'be patient until Allah judges between us' (7:87). Yusuf, from a prison cell, declared 'the judgement is only for Allah' (12:40). And Nuh, in grief over his drowned son, pleaded with 'the most just of judges' and then submitted the moment the verdict came (11:45-47).

So stop trying to clear your name in every room. The only Judge who counts already knows the whole truth of you.

What verdict has the world handed you that you have been carrying alone? Hand it up to Al-Hakam. 🤍
