Caption

Al-Badi, the Originator without precedent.

Stand under a clear sky tonight. Not one star was copied from a blueprint. No two snowflakes, no two faces, no two fingerprints, no two mornings are ever the same. Creation is not a factory stamping out copies. It is an endless act of invention, and Someone is doing the inventing.

The Qur'an names Allah this in two places, both in the same phrase: 'Originator of the heavens and the earth' (2:117, 6:101). Grounded in classical tafsir (Ibn Kathir, including his citation of Ibn Jarir al-Tabari, and al-Sa'di), the name means the One who brings the heavens and the earth into being on no prior model, from no earlier example, with no partner to help. It is the same Arabic root that gives the word for an innovation that had no precedent before it.

We never truly originate. Every poem uses a language we were handed; every invention reuses parts that already existed; at our most creative, we only rearrange. Al-Badi breaks that pattern entirely: making from nothing, by a Maker who answers to no example. And look how 2:117 finishes: 'When He decrees a matter, He only says to it, Be, and it is.' The distance between His will and a brand new universe is a single word.

Here is why it steadies you. What crushes us in hardship is the feeling that our situation is unprecedented, that there is no way out. But the One you are asking is the Originator who specialises in what has no precedent. The youths of the cave, cornered with no plan, did not ask for the old normal back; they prayed, 'prepare for us from our affair right guidance' (18:10).

So ask Him for a way you cannot yet see. 🤍
