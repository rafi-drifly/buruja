Al-Wali.

The Governor.

Underneath the news, and underneath your own week, sits a quiet fear: is anyone actually running this?

This name is the Qur'an's answer. And it is not a maybe. It is a Governor.
01 / 09
First, a careful note.

There are two names that look alike.

#55 Al-Wali (waliyy): the close Friend and Protector, pressed near to you.

#77 Al-Wali (wali): the Governor, the One running the affairs of everything.

Same root. Two houses. This is the second.
02 / 09
What does it mean that He governs?

The Qur'an gives one verb, over and over: yudabbiru al-amr. He arranges the matter.

Al-Sa'di: He governs the affair in the upper world and the lower, giving life and death, sending provision, raising peoples and lowering others.
03 / 09
And none of it strains Him.

Ibn Kathir: not an atom's weight escapes Him, and no single matter distracts Him from another. The management of the immense never pulls His attention off the tiny.

A governance with no dropped files. No off-hours.
04 / 09
The affair has a direction of travel.

'He arranges [each] matter from the heaven to the earth, then it ascends to Him...' (32:5)

Decrees going out, reports coming in, and at the centre One who never leaves His place.
05 / 09
Four words that hold the whole name:

'Unquestionably, His is the creation and the command.' (7:54)

He made it. And He runs it. We split those two. The verse refuses to.
06 / 09
Even the ones who deny Him cannot name a rival.

'Who arranges every matter?' ... 'They will say, Allah.' (10:31)

There is simply no other administrator to point to. There is one command in the universe, and it is His.
07 / 09
And on the day it becomes visible to sight:

'There the authority belongs to Allah, the Truth.' (18:44)

Every other power people leaned on falls away. The walaya was His all along. 🤍
08 / 09
So put down the belief that it all depends on your grip.

Your grip was never what was holding it.

The wheel of the universe and the small thing you cannot sleep over are turned by the same untiring hand.

Ya Wali. He has not left the wheel.
