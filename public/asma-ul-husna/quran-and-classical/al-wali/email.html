Is anyone actually running this?

Preview: Al-Wali, the Governor: the One who runs every affair in creation. Not the close Friend (that is His cousin-name), but the One on the throne.

Assalamu alaikum,

Somewhere underneath the news, and underneath your own week, there is a question most of us keep from ourselves: is anyone actually running this? The markets, the weather, the diagnosis, the country on the screen, the small private chaos of your own life. Is there a hand on it, or is it all just happening?

One of the names of Allah is the Qur'an's answer, and it is not a comforting maybe. It is a Governor: Al-Wali.

A note first, because two names look almost the same. There is Al-Wali in the sense of the close Friend and Protector, the One pressed near to you who takes your side. And there is this name, Al-Wali in the sense of the Governor, the Patron who manages and conducts the affairs of everything. They grow from one root but they are two forms with two emphases. One is about His nearness to you. This one is about His command over the whole of creation.

What does it mean that He governs? The Qur'an gives one verb and uses it again and again: yudabbiru al-amr, He arranges the matter (10:3, 13:2, 32:5). Commenting on it, the scholar al-Sa'di writes that Allah, established above the Throne, governs the affair in the upper world and the lower: the giving of life and death, the sending down of provisions, the turning of the days between people, the lifting of harm from the afflicted, the answering of those who ask. And then a line worth keeping: every kind of governance descends from Him and ascends to Him. Ibn Kathir presses the scale of it until it almost frightens you: not an atom's weight escapes Him in the heavens or the earth, and the management of the immense never distracts Him from the tiny.

The Qur'an is so confident about this that it turns it into a question for the people who reject God outright: 'Who arranges every matter?' And it tells you their answer in advance: 'They will say, Allah' (10:31). Even the one who will not bow has no other administrator to name. His is the creation and the command (7:54): He made it, and He runs it. We split those two, as if God set the world spinning and governance became somebody else's job. The verse refuses the split.

And there is a day coming when this stops being a matter of belief and becomes a matter of sight. 'There the authority belongs to Allah, the Truth' (18:44). Every other power a person leaned on falls away, and what is left standing is the only Governor there ever was. Which means it is His now, too, in the part you cannot see.

So here is the rest this name offers. You can put down the exhausting belief that it all depends on your grip, because your grip was never what was holding it up. The wheel of the universe and the small matter you have been losing sleep over are turned by the same untiring hand, with the same perfect attention, as if each were the only thing being governed.

Ya Wali, You are our protector in this world and the Hereafter. Let us stop trusting powers You have merely subjected, and rest under the One who runs it all.
