The boast that history answered

Preview: Al-Khafid, the One who brings low. The arrogant who look like they are winning are being read at halftime, and the Day is khafidatun rafi'a.

Assalamu alaikum,

We live in a world that looks upside down. The arrogant are on top. The ones who lie loudest, who push to the front, who step on whoever is beneath them, seem to rise and rise, while the quiet and the wronged and the honest stay low. And a question forms that we are sometimes ashamed to say out loud: does anyone ever set this right?

One of the names of Allah answers that, and it does not answer gently: Al-Khafid, the One who brings low, the Abaser.

Let us be honest with you from the start, because this name deserves it. Unlike many of the names we write about, Al-Khafid does not appear in the Qur'an in its definite form. What the Qur'an gives instead is the living word in action. Describing the Day of Resurrection when it finally falls, Allah says of that Day: 'khafidatun rafi'a,' it brings down some and raises up others (Al-Waqi'a 56:3). The name is how the scholars of the tradition gathered that word, and they did not do it lightly. Al-Qurtubi, commenting on this very verse, settles it in one sentence: 'the One who truly lowers and the One who truly raises is Allah alone,' who raised His friends to the highest of degrees and lowered His enemies to the lowest of depths.

Notice what the verse refuses to do. It will not let you hear the lowering on its own. In the same breath comes the raising. The tradition has carried them as a pair ever since, Al-Khafid beside Ar-Rafi, the Exalter, because the same just Hand that brings the arrogant down is the very Hand that lifts the lowly up. And the scholars name exactly who is lowered: as-Suddi says the arrogant, Ibn 'Ashur says the tyrants and the corruptors who were in this world in elevation and lordship, and Ibn Kathir says those who were mighty in the world are cast to the lowest of the low, even as the lowly are raised highest.

You can watch it happen inside history. On a military expedition, the hypocrite Abdullah ibn Ubayy sneered that when they returned to Madina 'the mightier will expel the lowlier,' meaning, al-Sa'di explains, that he and his faction were the mighty and the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, and the believers were the lowly. Allah overturned it in the next breath: 'and to Allah belongs all honor, and to His Messenger, and to the believers' (63:8). The man who called himself the mightier was named the lowest. And al-Sa'di notes the saddest detail: 'but the hypocrites do not know' it. They went on feeling mighty while the ground had already opened beneath them. That is the most frightening way to fall, certain you are rising the whole time you are sinking.

So how does a person actually rise? Not by climbing over anyone. 'Whoever wants honor, then to Allah belongs all honor' (35:10), and, al-Sa'di says, it is reached only through His obedience. The verse shows the one thing that genuinely ascends: 'to Him ascends the good word, and the righteous deed raises it.' That is the real raising, and it is available to the lowest, quietest, most overlooked believer alive. Meanwhile the schemer's own plot, the verse says, 'perishes,' and al-Sa'di notes it only sinks him further. Al-Khafid often does not need to push the arrogant down. He lets their schemes run, and the schemes do the lowering.

Here, then, is the comfort of this name. If the arrogant around you look like they are winning, you are reading the scene at halftime. The Day is khafidatun rafi'a, and it is not finished. So make yourself small before Allah, so that you are never made small by Him. Seek your honor from the only Hand that owns it. And when falsehood towers over truth, hold onto the two words the Qur'an folded the whole Day into.

Ya Khafid, ya Rafi, raise us by Your obedience, and do not lower us by our own arrogance.
