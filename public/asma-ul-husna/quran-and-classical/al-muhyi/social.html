Caption

There is a dread most of us keep at arm's length: that one day this body stops, that the ones we lost are gone, and that is simply the end.

Al-Muhyi, the Giver of Life, is the Qur'an's answer. An honest note first: the definite name al-Muhyi reaches us through the tradition of the ninety-nine names. What the Qur'an gives directly is the living act, again and again: He is muhyi al-mawta, the One who gives life to the dead (Ar-Rum 30:50, Fussilat 41:39, verified by morphology as a participle from the root of 'life'), and yuhyi, He gives life, by His own hand.

And notice how He teaches it: not with the grave, but with rain. Look at how dead ground turns green, He says, then believe I will do the same for you. Ibn Kathir explains the reviving of the earth is meant to alert us to the reviving of bodies after death and decay. al-Sa'di: just as His power was not unable to revive the land, it is not unable to raise the dead.

Feel it through the man who broke a bone and asked who could ever bring it back (Ya-Sin 36:78), through Ibrahim who asked to watch four birds fly back together so his heart could rest (2:260), and through al-Sa'di's reading of Surah Al-Hadid: the Lord who revives the earth with rain revives dead hearts with the truth He sent down. So Al-Muhyi is not only the One who raises bodies one day. He is the One who can warm a cold heart now.

What dead thing have you given up on? Bring it to the Giver of Life. 🤍
