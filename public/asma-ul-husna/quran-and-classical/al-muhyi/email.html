The man who had himself scattered to the wind

Preview: Al-Muhyi, the Giver of Life. The dead earth that turns green, and the One who can revive what you gave up on.

Assalamu alaikum,

There is a thought most of us keep at arm's length, because it is too big and too final to hold for long. One day this body stops. The heart beating as you read this becomes still, and what remains is buried and, in time, undone completely. It is the most ordinary fact in the world and the most frightening. One of the names of Allah speaks straight into it: Al-Muhyi, the Giver of Life.

An honest word first, because this name asks us to trust it and trust is built on honesty. The exact definite name al-Muhyi comes to us through the tradition of the ninety-nine names rather than as a single word in the Qur'an. What the Qur'an gives directly, again and again, is the living act itself: He is muhyi al-mawta, the One who gives life to the dead (Ar-Rum 30:50, Fussilat 41:39), and yuhyi, He gives life, by His own hand. We build this reflection on those verses.

And notice how gently He teaches it. He does not open with the grave. He opens with rain. Look, He says, at the traces of My mercy, at how green returns to ground that was dead, and only then does He turn your face toward the larger promise: 'Indeed, that same One will give life to the dead' (30:50). Ibn Kathir explains that the reviving of the earth is meant to alert us to the reviving of bodies after death and decay, and al-Sa'di adds that the One whose power revived the land is not unable to raise the dead from their graves. Every spring, you have already watched Him do it. The resurrection is not asking you to believe in something you have never seen.

The Qur'an even carries the doubt out loud. A man broke a crumbling bone in his hand and demanded, who could give life to bones once they are this far gone (Ya-Sin 36:78)? The reply: he forgot his own creation. al-Sa'di says he judged the Creator's power by a creature's; you were once nothing at all, and remaking is lighter than making. And Ibn Kathir carries a story that stops you: a man so terrified of standing before Allah had himself burned, ground to dust, and scattered half to the wind and half to the sea, hoping to never be reassembled. Allah gathered every particle, said Be, and there he stood. Asked what drove him, he said, fear of You. And Allah forgave him. There is no scattering He cannot gather.

And this name reaches further than the grave. Reading the dead-earth verse in Surah Al-Hadid, al-Sa'di writes that the One who revived the earth with rain is able to revive dead hearts with the truth He sent down upon His Messenger. The cold, numb heart is its own dead earth. The same name is the answer. Al-Muhyi is not only the One who will raise your body one day; He is the One who can bring your heart back to life now.

So here is the invitation of this name. Do not live as someone heading toward an ending. Live as someone heading toward the One who gives life, and carry your dead things to Him along the way, the relationship that feels beyond saving, the faith that has gone flat, the hope you buried. The God who turns brown earth green and calls a scattered man back into standing is not defeated by the thing you gave up on.

Ya Muhyi, revive our hearts as You revive the land, and gather us, whole and forgiven, before You.
