The Throne beside which the heavens are a ring in a desert

Preview: Al-Majeed, the All-Glorious. Greatness with no edge: every perfect attribute at its fullest, running past every horizon.

Assalamu alaikum,

Try, for a moment, to think of something with no edge. Not something big, big is easy, you can picture a mountain or a sky. Try to picture greatness that simply does not stop: no rim, no ceiling, no far wall where it finally runs out. Your mind reaches for the boundary out of habit and finds none. That reach, and that failure to find the end, is the closest most of us come to feeling this name.

One of the names of Allah is Al-Majeed, the All-Glorious.

The scholars define its root, majd, as far more than a moment of triumph. Commenting on the verse where the angels tell the wife of Ibrahim that Allah is 'Praiseworthy and Glorious' (Hud 11:73), al-Sa'di explains majd as the breadth of Allah's attributes and their greatness: He has every attribute of perfection, and of each one the most complete, the most total, and the most all-encompassing share there is. Read that slowly. Not one tall quality, but the fullness of all of them, each stretched past the limit you can picture. His mercy without a far edge, His knowledge without a gap, His power without a ceiling.

Hold that against everything you know, because everything you know has an edge. The most generous person still has a limit to what they can give. The wisest reaches the end of what they understand. Take the fence away, and you begin, only begin, to face what this name means.

The Qur'an gives you an image for it, and it is staggering. In Surah Al-Buruj, Allah is named the Glorious Owner of the Throne (85:15). Al-Sa'di describes that Throne as so immense that the heavens, the earth, and the Kursi that contains them are, beside it, like a ring thrown down in an open desert next to the rest of the earth. Gather everything you have ever called vast, set it beside the Throne of Al-Majeed, and it shrinks to a single ring in a wilderness. And the Throne itself is only a creation, the faintest hint of its Owner. The same word, majeed, is even used of the Qur'an (50:1), which al-Sa'di calls vast in its meanings and overflowing in good, because the One who spoke it has no edge.

And this vastness is not cold. The very passage that crowns His glory frames it with mercy: just before the Throne, 'He is the Forgiving, the Affectionate' (85:14), and just after it, 'the Doer of whatever He wills' (85:16). His greatness does not hold Him too high to reach you. It is the size of the hand that reaches down to forgive and to love.

So here is the invitation of this name. Stop measuring Allah by what you have seen, because you have been picturing something far too small. And stop shrinking your du'a to fit a small God. To the One who has the most all-encompassing share of every perfection, the impossible request weighs the same as the easy one, which is nothing at all. Ask as if His resources have no bottom, because they do not.

Ya Majeed, let the knowing of Your glory empty us of pride and fill us with awe, and keep our small hearts safe in Your boundless hand.
