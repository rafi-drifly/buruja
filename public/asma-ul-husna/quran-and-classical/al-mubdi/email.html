You were once not a thing at all

Preview: Al-Mubdi, the One who begins creation. The God who made you from nothing, and why that means you are never beyond repair.

Assalamu alaikum,

There is a fear that hides underneath the more obvious fear of death, and it is quieter and stranger. It is the fear of being undone so completely that there is nothing left to bring back. Not just that you will die, but that you will be scattered, erased past the point of return. We can imagine being repaired. It is harder to imagine being remade out of nothing at all. One of the names of Allah reaches straight into that fear, and it does it from the strangest direction: it points you back to your own beginning.

The name is Al-Mubdi, the One who begins creation. An honest word first, because this name asks for your trust and trust is built on honesty: the definite name al-Mubdi, in that exact form, comes to us through the tradition of the ninety-nine names rather than as a single standalone word in the Qur'an. What the Qur'an gives directly, in Allah's own speech, is the verb, and it is the very form the name is carved from: 'Indeed, it is He who originates [creation] and repeats,' innahu huwa yubdi'u wa yu'id (Al-Buruj 85:13). We build this entirely on those verses.

Now here is how it answers the fear. The prophet Ibrahim, peace be upon him, argued with his people using this name, and Ibn Kathir explains the heart of it: Allah created them after they were not a thing worth mentioning, then they came to exist and became people who hear and see (Al-Ankabut 29:19). Read that slowly. There was a time, not long ago, when you were not. Not weak, not small. Simply not there. No body, no name, nothing of you in the world. And then al-Mubdi began you. Out of that nothing He drew a person, with eyes that catch light and a mind reading these very words. You already are the impossible thing.

And that is the whole argument of this name. The Qur'an almost never names the beginning without naming the return beside it: He begins creation, then He brings it back. al-Sa'di lays the logic bare: the One who is able to begin the creation is able to bring it back, and the person who accepts the first while denying the second has lost his reason, because he is rejecting the easier of two things while granting the harder. On Surah Ar-Rum, where Allah says He begins creation 'then repeats it, and that is easier for Him' (30:27), al-Sa'di calls this qiyas al-awla, the argument from the greater to the lesser: if He did the harder thing, you cannot doubt the easier.

Which means the lie finally dies, the one that says you are too far gone. al-Mubdi does not need good raw material; He began the universe with none at all. So the body you think is too broken, the life you think is finished, the hope you buried, He began harder things than these, out of nothing, without strain. Against that, every restoration you long for is the lighter task.

So here is the invitation of this name. Stop staring at the end in dread. Turn around and look at your beginning, where you find that the God who started you is a God who does not lose what He starts. Bring Him your dead places and ask the One who specializes in beginnings to begin again. The return itself belongs to His companion name, al-Muid, the One who brings it all back.

Ya Mubdi, You brought us out of nothing and made us know You. Begin in our dead places what we cannot begin ourselves, and do not abandon what Your own hand has begun.
