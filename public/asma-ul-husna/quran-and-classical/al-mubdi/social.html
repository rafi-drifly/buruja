Caption

There is a fear quieter than the fear of dying: the fear of being undone so completely that there is nothing left to bring back.

Al-Mubdi, the One who begins creation, is the Qur'an's answer. An honest note first: the definite name al-Mubdi reaches us through the tradition of the ninety-nine names. What the Qur'an gives directly is the verb, and it is the exact form the name is carved from: innahu huwa yubdi'u, 'indeed it is He who originates' (Al-Buruj 85:13), the same word again in Al-Ankabut 29:19, verified by morphology as a fourth-form verb from the root of 'beginning.'

And it answers your fear from the strangest direction: it points you back to your own beginning. Ibn Kathir, explaining Ibrahim's argument, says Allah created you after you were not a thing worth mentioning, then made you someone who hears and sees. There was a time you were simply not there. And al-Mubdi began you out of that nothing. You are, right now, the something that came from nothing.

That is the whole proof. He begins creation, then He brings it back, and the two are always named together. al-Sa'di: the One able to begin is able to bring back, and on Surah Ar-Rum he explains by qiyas al-awla that the bringing back is the easier of the two, so it is the more certain. Whatever you fear about being remade, the harder version, your origination from nothing, is already behind you.

Which kills the lie that you are too far gone. al-Mubdi began the universe with no raw material at all. So bring Him the dead places, the body you think is broken, the hope you buried, and ask the One who specializes in beginnings to begin again.

What have you written off as finished? Bring it to the One who begins. 🤍
