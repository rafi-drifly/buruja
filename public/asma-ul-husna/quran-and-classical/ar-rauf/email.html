The gentlest word God uses for Himself

Preview: Ar-Rauf, the Most Kind. The tenderest reach of mercy, and the gentleness that will not let your faith be lost.

Assalamu alaikum,

There is mercy that arrives after the blow has landed, the bandage after the wound, the rescue after the fall. We are grateful for it, and it is real. But there is a gentler thing still: a tenderness so fine it cannot bear to see you hurt in the first place, that moves to spare you the wound before it ever reaches you.

One of the names of Allah is exactly this, raised to a degree we cannot measure: Ar-Rauf, the Most Kind.

The scholars of the language draw a fine line here. Ibn Ashur, gathering the lexicographers in al-Tahrir wa al-Tanwir, reports Abu Amr ibn al-Ala saying that ra'fah is stronger than rahmah, and al-Jawhari defining it as the most intense degree of mercy. The cleanest summary he keeps is from al-Qaffal: rahmah is the broad name for every good thing given, while ra'fah is the tender core of it, the special mercy whose whole work is the warding off of harm and the removal of injury. Picture two circles, one inside the other. Ra'fah is the inmost part, the part that flinches from your pain.

And notice where the Qur'an places this name. Not over the strong, but over the nearly broken. At the close of Surah At-Tawba, after the crushing expedition to Tabuk, where Ibn Kathir relates that two men would split a single date and the thirst was so severe they feared their necks would be cut, a party of the believers had almost slipped. And Allah says: then He turned back to them, then He forgave them. 'Indeed, He was to them Kind and Merciful' (9:117). Al-Sa'di draws out the lesson: among His ra'fah is that He granted them repentance and kept them firm upon it. The tenderness is what steadied the buckling heart.

It is the same name that protects your past. When the qiblah changed, the believers worried their earlier prayers were now wasted, and the answer came: 'And never would Allah have caused you to lose your faith. Indeed Allah is, to the people, Kind and Merciful' (Al-Baqara 2:143). On this verse Ibn Kathir recalls the captive mother searching for her child, pressing every infant to her chest, and the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, saying that Allah is more merciful to His servants than this woman is to her child.

Even the warnings turn out to be this same gentleness. 'And Allah warns you of Himself, and Allah is Kind to His servants' (Al Imran 3:30). Al-Sa'di explains that He warns you out of ra'fah, so that the time will not stretch long over your heart and harden it. The pull back from the road is the love.

So here is the invitation of this name. Stop bracing against God. The tenderness was never waiting for you to become strong first. It is drawn to the exact weakness you are most ashamed of. Come closest when you feel least deserving, the way the believers in Surah Al-Hashr sealed their prayer: 'Our Lord, indeed You are Kind and Merciful.'

Ya Rauf, be gentle with us where we are most easily wounded, and do not let the faith we have spent be lost.
