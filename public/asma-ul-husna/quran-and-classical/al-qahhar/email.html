To whom belongs the kingdom today?

Preview: Al-Qahhar, the Subduer. Why the Qur'an only ever says al-Wahid al-Qahhar, and the day every crown is laid bare.

Assalamu alaikum,

There is a quiet comfort we reach for when life feels out of control, and most of us never trace it to its root. We say, it is in bigger hands now. We mean that somewhere above the doctors and the markets and the people who can hurt us, there is a hand nothing can pry open, a power no other power can overrule.

One of the names of Allah is exactly that: al-Qahhar, the Subduer, the One who prevails over all. Not a force that wins most of its battles, but the only power in existence that has never been resisted and never can be.

Here is something striking about it. The name appears six times in the Qur'an, and in every single one it is joined to another name, al-Wahid, the One. You never read it alone. It is always al-Wahid al-Qahhar, the One, the Subduer. And the scholar al-Sa'di explains that this is not a flourish, it is an argument: oneness and overpowering require each other. There can never be two beings who each prevail absolutely, because the moment one could check the other, neither would truly prevail. So total, unanswerable power can belong only to One.

Al-Sa'di draws out a thought from this that, once you see it, reframes every fear. Look at anything that overpowers, he says, and you will find something above it that overpowers it in turn. The predator is taken by the hunter, the strong man by death, the empire by the empire that buries it. The chain has to end somewhere, in a Subduer who is subdued by nothing. Which means everything you are afraid of is, itself, afraid of something. The thing holding power over you is being held by something larger, all the way up, until you reach the One above the whole ladder, the One you pray to.

Of all the places this name appears, hold on to where the Prophet Yusuf, peace be upon him, says it. He is in prison, betrayed, forgotten, with no power over his own life at all, and the name on his tongue is al-Wahid al-Qahhar (Yusuf 12:39). He trusted the Subduer while everything around him said otherwise, and history proved him right.

And twice the Qur'an ties this name to a single scene: the Day every other power finally drops its mask. The earth is changed and all of creation steps out barizun, laid bare, with nothing to cover or hide them (Ibrahim 14:48, Ghafir 40:16). Ibn Kathir relates that Allah rolls up the heavens and the earth and asks, where are the kings of the earth, where are the tyrants, and then, when no one remains but Him, He asks three times, to whom belongs the sovereignty today, and answers His own question: to Allah, al-Wahid al-Qahhar. Every crown that was ever worn will be on the floor.

So here is the gift of this name. The same power that humbles the tyrant is the power that shelters the believer. To the one who fights Allah, al-Qahhar is the wall he breaks against. To the one who runs to Allah, it is the wall nothing breaks through to reach him. Stop trying to be the strongest thing in your own life, and take shelter behind the One who actually is.

Ya Qahhar, place our fear of everything else beneath our awe of You, and shelter us behind Your power rather than crushing us against it.
