Al-Muqtadir.

The Perfectly Able.

We already know Allah is able. But there is a stronger word, and the Qur'an saves it for the two most extreme moments imaginable.

One where a tyrant is torn out of the earth. One where the righteous are seated in the highest place in existence.
01 / 09
It is the same root as Al-Qadeer, the All-Able.

But Al-Muqtadir is the intensive form: not power held in reserve, but power in the act of prevailing.

Able-ness you can feel landing, not just power you can affirm.
02 / 09
First time the name appears: Pharaoh's people.

They denied every sign. So Allah seized them, 'with a seizure of one Exalted in Might and Perfect in Ability.' (54:42)

Ibn Kathir: He left of them no reporter, no eye, no trace.
03 / 09
This is the seizing face of the name.

It is meant for the heart that thinks it has gotten away with something.

The drowning of the mightiest empire on earth was not a struggle for Allah. It was a hand closing. No one outruns Al-Muqtadir.
04 / 09
But then, thirteen verses later, the same name returns.

And this time the power is not closing on anyone. It is seating someone.

'In a seat of honor near a Sovereign, Perfect in Ability.' (54:55)
05 / 09
The exact attribute that destroyed the tyrant now hosts the believer.

Ibn Kathir: He is able over 'whatever they ask and desire.'

The hand that prevails over everything is the hand that seats you near the King. 🤍
06 / 09
And in Surah Al-Kahf, the same power scatters the whole world.

The dazzling green field becomes dry stubble in the wind.

'And Allah is ever, over all things, Perfect in Ability.' (18:45)

Hold the One in whose hand the wind sits.
07 / 09
One power. Two faces. One choice.

It will prevail either way, over Pharaoh, over the world, over you.

The only question is whether it meets you as the grip that seizes, or the hand that seats.
08 / 09
And the door between them is wide open.

The ones seated near the King are simply the muttaqin: those who feared Him enough to turn toward Him.

Ya Muqtadir. Make His perfect ability find us among the ones it draws close.
09 / 09
Caption

We are used to saying Allah is able. Al-Muqtadir, the Perfectly Able, asks us to say something harder: that His power prevails, always, and the only question is how it will meet us.

Grounded in classical tafsir (Ibn Kathir, al-Sa'di, al-Tabari, al-Muyassar), this name is the intensive of Al-Qadeer, the same root q-d-r taken to its absolute reach. And the Qur'an reserves it for the two ends of one surah, on purpose.

At one end: Pharaoh's people denied every sign, and Allah seized them 'with a seizure of one Exalted in Might and Perfect in Ability' (54:42). Ibn Kathir says He left of them no reporter, no trace. That is the seizing face of the name, for the heart that thinks it got away.

At the other end, thirteen verses later: the same name, now seating the God-conscious 'in a seat of honor near a Sovereign, Perfect in Ability' (54:55). Ibn Kathir notes He is able over 'whatever they ask and desire.' The power that crushed the tyrant is turned to fulfilling the believer.

Identical power. Opposite outcomes. The only difference is which side of it you chose, and the people drawn close are simply the ones who feared Him and turned toward Him.

So make your du'a bold. You are not asking a God who hopes He can. You are asking Al-Muqtadir, for whom your impossible is already small. 🤍
