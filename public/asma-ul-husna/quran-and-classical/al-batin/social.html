What does Al-Batin mean?

In the hadith Muslim records, the Prophet ﷺ explained it himself:

Caption

There is a loneliness that sits very deep in a person: the sense that no one can reach the innermost part of us, the thoughts we never speak, the aches we never name.

Al-Batin, the Inmost, is the answer the Qur'an gives. Grounded in classical tafsir (Ibn Kathir, al-Sa'di, al-Muyassar), this name carries two senses at once. It means the most near: as the Prophet ﷺ said in the supplication Muslim records, 'You are Al-Batin, so there is nothing nearer than You.' How near? 'We know what his soul whispers to him, and We are closer to him than his jugular vein' (50:16). And it means the One too vast to be grasped: 'Vision perceives Him not' (6:103), al-Sa'di says, because of His greatness. He even holds 'the keys of the unseen; none knows them but Him' (6:59).

So the inner room you thought was sealed off is not empty. The fear too private to say, the prayer you cannot phrase, the motive you barely admit to yourself, is already fully known to the One who loves you, and He has not turned away.

And the most beyond name is fastened to the most exalted one: Al-Batin, nearer than all, is paired with Az-Zahir, above all, while the next verse adds, He is with you wherever you are (57:4). Most near and most beyond, in one breath.

What have you been carrying on the inside, alone? He has been there the whole time. 🤍

Note: the bedtime supplication quoted here is a hadith recorded by Muslim, not a verse of the Qur'an.
