The thing almost everyone is chasing in the wrong direction

Preview: Al-Muizz, the Giver of Honour. All honour belongs to Allah, and you have been asking the wrong doors for it.

Assalamu alaikum,

Almost everyone is chasing the same thing under a hundred different names. We call it respect, status, a seat at the table, being someone, being taken seriously, not being looked down on. Underneath all of it sits one quiet hunger: to be honoured, and the matching fear of being humiliated. And most of us spend our lives asking the wrong doors for it.

One of the names of Allah walks straight into that hunger: Al-Muizz, the Giver of Honour.

Let us be honest with you first, the way this name deserves. You will not find the definite name Al-Muizz sitting in the Qur'an the way you find Al-Aziz. What the Qur'an gives is the living verb, spoken by Allah about Himself, in a verse the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, was taught to say as a prayer: 'You honour whom You will (tuizzu) and You humble whom You will' (Ali 'Imran 3:26). The scholars of the tradition drew the name Al-Muizz from that verb, and al-Sa'di, commenting on Surah Al-Fath, names Allah directly as al-Muizz al-Mudhill, the Giver of Honour and the Giver of Disgrace. So the name rests on Allah's own word, even though the definite form is the tradition's way of carrying it.

Notice that the Qur'an pairs honour with its opposite in a single breath: tuizzu, You honour, and tudhillu, You humble. Honour and humiliation are not loose in the world, scattered among bosses and crowds and rivals. They come from one Hand. That is why the tradition holds Al-Muizz beside Al-Mudhill and will not separate them.

And then the Qur'an tells you exactly where to take the hunger. 'Whoever desires honour, then to Allah belongs all honour' (Fatir 35:10). It does not shame the wanting. It redirects it. Al-Sa'di reads it as an instruction: seek honour from the One in whose Hand it lies, for it is attained only through His obedience. And al-Muyassar states the law in one unforgettable sentence: whoever seeks honour through the creation, Allah humbles him, and whoever seeks honour through the Creator, Allah honours him.

Sit with that as a law of your life. Every time you chained your worth to a person's approval, a title, a crowd, you handed your honour to something that could not hold it, and it slipped. The honour that lasts has only ever come from one direction.

The Qur'an even gives this name a scene. On the road back to Madina, the leader of the hypocrites sneered that the honoured ones, meaning himself, would drive out the lowly ones, meaning the Prophet, peace be upon him, and the believers. He had it exactly backwards, and Allah corrected him in the same verse: 'And to Allah belongs all honour, and to His Messenger, and to the believers, but the hypocrites do not know' (Al-Munafiqun 63:8). Ibn Kathir relates that the hypocrite's own believing son then stood at the gate of the city with his sword drawn and barred his father, telling him: by Allah you will not pass until you admit that the Messenger of Allah is the honoured one and you are the lowly one. He understood this name with his whole body.

So here is the rest this name offers. The person whose approval you are bracing for, the room you fear will look down on you, the rival who seems to hold the keys to your worth, none of them was ever the Muizz. They cannot raise you and they cannot truly lower you. Put the exhausting performance down. Seek your honour from the One who actually owns it, by drawing near to Him, and let the verdicts of people fall where they fall.

Ya Muizz, honour us with Your obedience, free us from chasing the approval of people, and let us seek our standing from You alone.
