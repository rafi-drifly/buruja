Al-Muizz.

The Giver of Honour.

Almost everyone is chasing the same thing under different names: respect, status, being someone. Underneath it sits one hunger, to be honoured, and one fear, to be humiliated.

This name resettles both.
01 / 09
First, the honest part.

The definite name Al-Muizz is not in the Qur'an the way Al-Aziz is. What the Qur'an gives is the verb, spoken by Allah of Himself:

'You honour whom You will (tuizzu) and You humble whom You will.' (3:26)

The scholars drew the name from His own word.
02 / 09
And the Qur'an pairs it with its opposite.

In the same breath: tuizzu (You honour) and tudhillu (You humble).

Honour and humiliation come from one Hand. So the tradition teaches Al-Muizz beside Al-Mudhill, the Giver of Disgrace, and will not separate them.
03 / 09
Where does honour actually come from?

'Whoever desires honour, then to Allah belongs ALL honour.' (35:10)

Al-Sa'di: seek it from the One in whose Hand it lies. The Qur'an does not shame the wanting. It redirects it.
04 / 09
Al-Muyassar puts the whole law in one line:

**Seek honour through the creation, and Allah lowers you.

Seek honour through the Creator, and Allah raises you.**

Every time you chained your worth to someone's approval, it slipped. It was never theirs to give.
05 / 09
Honour even has a road.

Al-Sa'di, on 3:26: Allah honours whom He wills through obedience to Him, and humbles whom He wills through disobedience.

The quiet believer in an empty room is being lifted in the only court that counts.
06 / 09
At the gate of Madina, the hypocrite sneered that he, the 'honoured' one, would drive out the 'lowly' believers.

He had it backwards. 'To Allah belongs all honour, and to His Messenger ﷺ, and to the believers.' (63:8)

His whole tragedy: he did not know where honour lives.
07 / 09
Then this.

Ibn Ubayy's own son drew his sword at the gate and barred his father:

'By Allah you will not pass until you admit the Messenger ﷺ is the honoured one and you are the lowly one.'

He understood Al-Muizz with his whole body.
08 / 09
So you can stop chasing it.

The boss, the crowd, the critic, the one whose approval you crave, none of them is Al-Muizz. They cannot raise you and they cannot truly lower you.

Seek your honour from the One who owns it. Hold your head before people, lower it before Allah. 🤍

Caption

Almost everyone is chasing honour under one name or another, respect, status, being taken seriously, and quietly fearing humiliation. And almost everyone is looking in the wrong direction.

Al-Muizz, the Giver of Honour, is the answer the Qur'an gives. Built from classical tafsir (Ibn Kathir, al-Sa'di, al-Muyassar, al-Jalalayn), with one honest note up front: the definite name Al-Muizz is not in the Qur'an the way Al-Aziz is. What the Qur'an gives is the verb, spoken by Allah of Himself, 'You honour whom You will (tuizzu) and You humble whom You will' (3:26), and the scholars drew the name from His own word. Al-Sa'di names Allah al-Muizz al-Mudhill directly (48:7).

The Qur'an pairs honour with its opposite in a single breath, tuizzu and tudhillu, because both come from one Hand. So we hold Al-Muizz beside Al-Mudhill, the Giver of Disgrace.

And it tells you exactly where to take the hunger: 'Whoever desires honour, then to Allah belongs all honour' (35:10). Al-Muyassar states the law plainly, seek honour through the creation and Allah lowers you, seek it through the Creator and Allah raises you. Al-Sa'di adds the road: Allah honours through obedience and humbles through disobedience.

Feel it at the gate of Madina, where the hypocrite called himself the honoured one and the believers lowly, and had it exactly backwards (63:8), and where his own believing son drew a sword and would not let him through until he admitted who truly held honour.

So put the chase down. Seek your worth from the only One who issues it.

Whose approval have you been auditioning for? He was never the source of your honour. 🤍
