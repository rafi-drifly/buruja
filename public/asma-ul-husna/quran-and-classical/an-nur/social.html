Caption

Think of the last time you were in real darkness. In the dark you do not reach for advice. You reach for light, and the moment it comes the whole world is handed back to you.

An-Nur, the Light, is the name that tells you where that light comes from. The Qur'an does not only say Allah gives light. In its most famous verse it says, 'Allah is the Light of the heavens and the earth' (24:35), the verse so beloved that the whole chapter is named after it.

What does that mean? Here we follow the scholars rather than our own guesses, because this is holy ground. Ibn Kathir relays from Ibn Abbas that it means the Guide of the people of the heavens and the earth, and from al-Suddi that by His light the heavens and the earth are lit. Al-Sa'di lays out the clearest map: the light is of two kinds, the sensory light by which the sun and moon and Throne are lit, and the spiritual light of His Book and of faith in the hearts of His servants, both of them from Him. Then he says the line to carry for life: were it not for His light, the darknesses would pile up.

That is the hope in this name. Darkness has no power of its own. It is only the place His light has not yet reached. And this God comes and gets you: 'He brings them out from darknesses into the light' (2:257), reviving the one who was dead in misguidance and giving him 'a light by which to walk among the people' (6:122).

So whatever dark you are sitting in tonight, hold the name. He can reach the corner you are in. Ask Him to. 🤍
