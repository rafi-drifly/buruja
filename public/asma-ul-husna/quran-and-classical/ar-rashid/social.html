Ar-Rashid
The Perfectly Rightly-Directing

Ar-Rashid.

The Perfectly Rightly-Directing.

First, an honest word: this name is not in the Qur'an. No verse calls Allah by it.

It comes through the traditional list of the 99 names and the scholars' usage. So we build it carefully.
01 / 09
And there is a trap.

The exact word 'ar-rashid' does appear once, at Hud 11:87. But there it is the people of Shu'ayb, sneering at their prophet:

'Indeed, you are the forbearing, the rightly-guided!' They meant it as an insult. It is not about Allah.
02 / 09
So we ground the name in the Qur'an's own word: rushd.

Al-Sa'di defines it as a comprehensive name for everything that directs a person to the good of their religion and their worldly life.

Rightness of direction. Soundness. Maturity.
03 / 09
And the Qur'an ties that quality straight to Allah.

'Our Lord is He who gave each thing its form and then guided [it].' (20:50)

Every creature, made and then steered to its purpose. The whole universe, quietly doing exactly what it should.
04 / 09
There is a second kind: the rushd He grows in a heart.

'...that they may be rightly guided.' (2:186)

Al-Sa'di: every believer has rushd in the measure of the faith he carries. It is given, and it is given by Allah.
05 / 09
Now notice who claims the word in the Qur'an.

The sneering crowd at Shu'ayb. And then, again and again, the single most dangerous man in the Book: Pharaoh.
06 / 09
'The command of Pharaoh was not rashid.' (11:97)

And Pharaoh, lying: 'I do not guide you except to the way of right conduct [ar-rashad].' (40:29)

Al-Sa'di: he dressed his own misguidance up as guidance. Every human claimant is mocked or lying.
07 / 09
That is the whole point.

Flawless, trustworthy direction is not something any creature owns. It is God's alone. He is the only One who never steers you wrong, never dresses error as guidance.

That is why the name is His. 🤍
08 / 09
So pray like the youths of the cave, cornered and afraid:

'Our Lord... prepare for us from our affair right guidance.' (18:10)

Not only show me the path. Steer me down it.

Ya Rashid.

Caption

An honest reflection on a hard name. Ar-Rashid, the One whose every directing is flawless right-guidance, is not a name the Qur'an gives Allah; there is no verse that calls Him by it. It reaches us through the traditional list of the ninety-nine names (the narration of al-Walid ibn Muslim recorded by al-Tirmidhi) and the usage of the scholars. And there is a trap worth naming: the exact word 'ar-rashid' appears once, at Hud 11:87, but there the people of Madyan are sneering at their prophet Shu'ayb ('you are the forbearing, the rightly-guided!'), not naming Allah. Al-Jalalayn, al-Muyassar, and al-Sa'di all read it as mockery.

So we build it carefully, from verified verses and classical tafsir (Ibn Kathir, al-Sa'di, al-Muyassar, al-Biqa'i). The Qur'an's own word is rushd: rightness of direction, soundness, maturity. And it ties that to Allah. He 'gave each thing its form and then guided it' (20:50), steering every creature to its purpose. The Qur'an 'guides to the right course' (72:2). He pours rushd into the hearts that turn to Him and calls them the rightly-guided (49:7).

Then notice who else claims the word: the sneering crowd, and Pharaoh, who lied that he guides 'to the way of right conduct' (40:29) while the Qur'an says his command 'was not rashid' (11:97). Every human claimant in the Book is mocked or lying, because flawless direction belongs to God alone. That is exactly why the name is His.

So pray like the youths of the cave: 'prepare for us from our affair right guidance' (18:10). Not only show me the path. Steer me down it. 🤍
