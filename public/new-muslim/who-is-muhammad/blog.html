Day 2 of the first week · The New Muslim Path

Who Is Muhammad ﷺ

The mercy you follow

You did not only turn toward Allah. In the same breath, you said something about a man: that Muhammad is His Messenger. Half of the doorway you walked through is about him. So today, meet him.

He lived a real life, in a real place, fourteen centuries ago, in the deserts of Arabia. We know him better than almost anyone from that age, because the people who loved him remembered everything: how he spoke, how he ate, how he treated the weakest person in the room. This is a first look at why so many people, every day, try to be a little more like him.

The New Muslim Path

Who Is Muhammad ﷺ

The mercy you follow
Just for today

When his name comes up today, add four small words: 'peace be upon him.' Muslims say them every time, out of love, and in writing you will see them as the symbol ﷺ. Say them once now, slowly, for the man who was sent as a mercy to you.

Sent as a mercy

His name was Muhammad, son of Abdullah. He was born in the city of Makkah, in Arabia, and orphaned young: his father died before his birth, his mother when he was six. He grew up knowing loss, knowing what it is to be the one without parents. Anyone who feels they began life a step behind has a friend in him.

Before he ever spoke of God, his people called him Al-Amin, the trustworthy one, because in a city full of liars he did not lie, and they left their valuables with him for safekeeping. At the age of forty, in a cave where he used to sit alone, the angel Jibril (Gabriel) came to him with the first words of the Qur'an, and his quiet life became a mission that did not end until the message was complete.

Allah says, in one short line, why he was sent at all:

وَمَآ أَرْسَلْنَٰكَ إِلَّا رَحْمَةً لِّلْعَٰلَمِينَAnd We have not sent you except as a mercy to the worlds.Al-Anbya 21:107
From the tafsir · Tafsir as-Sa'di

As-Sa'di explains that the Prophet is himself Allah's mercy, a gift handed to His servants: those who believe in him receive that mercy, are grateful for it, and live by it, while those who turn away refuse the very gift that was sent to save them.

The best of character

Allah praised many things, but of His Prophet ﷺ He praised one thing above all, his character:

وَإِنَّكَ لَعَلَىٰ خُلُقٍ عَظِيمٍAnd indeed, you are of a great moral character.Al-Qalam 68:4

His character was the Qur'an

Years later, someone asked his wife Aisha (may Allah be pleased with her), who knew him at home better than anyone, what he was actually like. Her answer was not a list of virtues. It was one sentence:

His character was the Qur'an.The Prophet ﷺ · Sahih Muslim 746 · sahih

Why we follow him, and do not worship him

She meant that everything the Qur'an asks a person to be, he simply was. He was gentle with children and patient with the rude. He mended his own clothes, helped in his home, and never struck a servant. When a man came shouting at him, he answered softly. The most influential man in Arabia was also the most approachable.

Be clear on this, because it matters: Muslims do not worship Muhammad ﷺ. Worship belongs to Allah alone. The Prophet ﷺ was a human being, who was born and who died, who ate and slept and grieved. What sets him apart is that Allah chose him to carry the message and to live it out in front of people, so that faith would not stay an idea but become a life we could see and copy.

The way he lived, the things he did and said and approved, is called the Sunnah, and it is the second source of guidance after the Qur'an, preserved in reports called hadith. When you learn to pray in the coming days, you are not inventing anything. You are copying him. Allah tied the two together:

لَّقَدْ كَانَ لَكُمْ فِى رَسُولِ ٱللَّهِ أُسْوَةٌ حَسَنَةٌ لِّمَن كَانَ يَرْجُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَٱلْيَوْمَ ٱلْءَاخِرَ وَذَكَرَ ٱللَّهَ كَثِيرًاThere has certainly been for you in the Messenger of Allah an excellent pattern for anyone whose hope is in Allah and the Last Day and who remembers Allah often.Al-Ahzab 33:21

Learning to love him

And following the Prophet ﷺ is the very path to being loved by God. This is the promise underneath it all:

قُلْ إِن كُنتُمْ تُحِبُّونَ ٱللَّهَ فَٱتَّبِعُونِى يُحْبِبْكُمُ ٱللَّهُ وَيَغْفِرْ لَكُمْ ذُنُوبَكُمْ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ غَفُورٌ رَّحِيمٌSay, 'If you should love Allah, then follow me, so Allah will love you and forgive you your sins. And Allah is Forgiving and Merciful.'Aal 'Imran 3:31
A du'a to carry
اللَّهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَىٰ مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَىٰ آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ

Allahumma salli 'ala Muhammadin wa 'ala ali Muhammad

O Allah, send Your blessings upon Muhammad and upon the family of Muhammad. (the form the Prophet ﷺ taught for sending blessings upon him)

Carry this with you

If you remember nothing else from this page, remember these four things about the one you said you follow.

  • He was a man, not a god. We do not worship him. We worship Allah alone. Muhammad ﷺ is the Messenger who showed us how.
  • His character was the Qur'an. Gentle, honest, patient, humble. Everything the Book asks us to be, he simply was.
  • We follow him to reach Allah. The way he lived is the Sunnah. Copying it is not a burden, it is the path to being loved by God.
  • Love comes from knowing. He may be only a name to you today. Learn his life, and the love arrives on its own.

Frequently asked

Do Muslims worship Muhammad?

No. Muslims worship Allah alone. Muhammad ﷺ is His Messenger, a human being chosen to deliver and demonstrate the message. The old label 'Muhammadans' is a mistake, because worship is for God only, never for the Prophet.

Why do Muslims say 'peace be upon him' after his name?

It is a short prayer of love and respect, encouraged in the Qur'an itself, said each time his name is mentioned. In writing it appears as the symbol ﷺ. Saying it is a gentle habit you can pick up slowly, with no pressure.

What is the Sunnah?

The Sunnah is the example of the Prophet ﷺ: what he did, said, and approved of. Preserved in reports called hadith, it is the second source of Islam after the Qur'an, and it is how Muslims learn the details of how to pray and live.

What is a hadith?

A hadith is a recorded report of something the Prophet ﷺ said or did, passed down through chains of narrators and graded by scholars for reliability. The most trusted collections include Sahih al-Bukhari and Sahih Muslim.

Do I have to love the Prophet right away?

No feeling is forced on you. Love grows from knowing him, and you have only just begun. Learn a little of his life, send blessings upon him when you can, and let the love arrive in its own time.

A du'a of love for the Prophet ﷺ

Today you met him: an orphan who became a mercy, the trustworthy one, the man whose character was the Qur'an itself. You said you follow him when you took the shahada. Now you have begun to see who it is you are following.

Tomorrow we return to the words you said at the door, the shahada, and look at them more closely, because they hold more than you think. For now, send your peace upon the one who was sent for you.

O Allah, send Your peace and blessings upon Muhammad ﷺ. Place his love in my heart, let me walk a few steps behind him, and gather me one day among those who followed him. He was sent as a mercy. Let me live as one of the ones he was sent for. Ameen.
